# Agentic AI keyword forecast (Orwell Lab)

## The idea in one line

The agentic AI head terms (agentic ai use cases, ai agent workflow, autonomous ai agents) are already owned by IBM, Salesforce, Gartner and the big consultancies, so instead of fighting them we predict and claim the rising terms one layer down, before the difficulty climbs, and point them at Orwell Lab's build-partner positioning.

## Why it might matter

This feeds goal 2 directly: grow the custom-build client channel. The build-partner commercial terms in this forecast (AI agent development company, AI automation agency for [industry], fractional chief AI officer) are buyer-intent searches by the exact people who pay for KlientFlo-type builds, and the current rankers are small agencies and listicles, not brands we cannot outrank. The forecasting method also transfers to the [[SEO Portfolio]] and [[Comparison Network]]: spot a term while its results page is still forums and Medium posts, publish, bank the authority before the incumbents arrive.

Note on naming: Orwell Lab appears to be the build-partner brand sitting under Orwell AI. It has no project or area note yet. Open loop below.

## The method (how to get ahead of the keyword game)

The plan is to publish against terms that have rising interest but a results page still soft enough to win.

- [ ] Track five signals monthly: Google Trends slope, Exploding Topics / Glimpse breakout lists, funding and product-launch news, analyst forecasts (Gartner Hype Cycle, a16z, Menlo, McKinsey), and the actual search-results composition.
- [ ] Trigger to publish: interest rising AND the results page still full of forums, Medium posts and small blogs (no Fortune 500 or major-analyst page).
- [ ] Validate every candidate's real volume and difficulty in Semrush before committing (units were out on the research run, so the figures below are inferred from momentum signals, not pulled).
- [ ] Cadence: one tier 1 or tier 2 pillar a fortnight, plus two supporting long-tail pieces. Re-audit the watchlist monthly.

## Key takeaways

- **The competition has split.** Incumbents own the abstract head terms. Winnable space is technical plumbing terms (too new to be claimed), commercial hire/build/agency terms (ranked by small specialists), and category labels still in the definitional phase.
- **Build-partner terms are the money cluster** and the priority for Orwell Lab. Map directly to revenue, weak current rankers.
- **vibe coding is the one term with hard data:** Exploding Topics logged a 6,700% search rise March to May 2025, coined by Karpathy Feb 2025, Collins Word of the Year 2025. Now crowded, but "vibe coding for business / agency / production" is open.
- **Protocol terms are rising and under-defended:** MCP passed 10,000+ public servers and 97m monthly SDK downloads by early 2026, donated to the Linux Foundation Dec 2025, yet results are vendor docs and explainers, not service brands.
- **Vertical AI is where the money is going:** Menlo Ventures put vertical AI spend at $3.5bn in 2025, near 3x 2024. a16z thesis: AI turns labour into software, lifting revenue per customer.
- **Regulation drives the governance cluster:** EU AI Act high-risk obligations land August 2026; Gartner predicts guardian agents take 10-15% of the agentic market by 2030.
- **Hype-cycle risk is real.** Gartner placed agentic AI at the Peak of Inflated Expectations and warned 40%+ of agentic projects get cancelled by end 2027. Spread bets across clusters, do not stake everything on one category label.
