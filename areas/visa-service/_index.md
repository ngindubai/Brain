# Visa Service

Research area for the global visa service idea: programmatic SEO route pages ("need a visa from X to Y"), a one-click "upload documents once, apply anywhere" vault, and a small margin per application with the visa processed on the client's behalf.

## Headline verdict

Late and crowded. The two things you think are the edge are both already built and funded. Full reasoning in [[feasibility-verdict]].

- The one-click document vault is **not novel**. Atlys (over $76m raised, ~700k visas a year) and iVisa (2.6m+ travellers since 2013) already do exactly upload-once-apply-anywhere across 150+ destinations.
- The programmatic route-page SEO model is **not novel**. SimpleVisa sells it as a white-label product with the exact /visa/{destination}/{nationality}/ structure. iVisa has owned these keywords for over a decade.
- The "we process it in one click" promise **partly does not work**. Government eVisa portals deliberately block automated submission with CAPTCHAs and anti-bot. Incumbents process with human teams, not agents.
- The SEO moat is **weak and getting weaker**. Visa is YMYL, the most scam-saturated keyword space in travel, and the December 2025 core update plus AI Overviews are hitting exactly this kind of thin programmatic content.

The only versions worth a second look strip out the processing entirely: a pure lead-gen affiliate play, or a B2B white-label angle. See [[feasibility-verdict]].
