## 1. FiveM for GTA V Enhanced enters early access today — day-one bugs expected, Asset Escrow confirmed absent

**Source:** [GTA BOOM](https://www.gtaboom.com/gta-v-roleplay-is-about-to-get-a-major-upgrade-on-july-21-cd6d), search-indexed PCGamesN and Cfx.re Support coverage

FiveM for GTA V Enhanced launched into early access today on a separate client and server stack, rebranded "Cfx Server," running alongside FiveM Legacy rather than replacing it. The headline technical upgrade is the OneSync rebuild: sync rate up from 30 to 120 updates per second, a single server able to hold up to 2,048 players versus the old cap, and roughly 50% lower RAM usage. The developers have said outright that day-one server hiccups, bugs and broken scripts are "basically guaranteed," and that they're relying on the community to surface issues as they're found, an unusually blunt admission for a launch day post. Confirmed and unchanged from prior reporting: Asset Escrow, the feature that protects and distributes paid resources, is not available at this early access launch, and Cfx.re has given no timeline for when it arrives. That keeps the standing NORTHLANDS guidance in place without any need to revise it: if the server runs escrow-protected paid resources, staying on Legacy through the early access period remains the safer default until Cfx.re confirms an Enhanced escrow date. Worth a direct check in a few days once the initial bug wave has been triaged, rather than migrating on launch-day enthusiasm.

## 2. SEED, Klang Games' persistent society-sim MMO, also enters early access today

**Source:** search-indexed Massively Overpowered and GamesBeat coverage of the SEED launch

SEED, the nine-year-in-development colony-building MMO from Klang Games, launched into early access today via Klang's own PC launcher (a Steam version follows in autumn) at an intro price of $29.99 with no planned subscription. The pitch is a genuinely persistent world: players lead one or more "Seedlings" building a society on the planet Avesta, with birth, death, ageing, inheritance, crime, law enforcement and governance all running whether or not anyone is logged in. It's a smaller, less-tracked launch than FiveM Enhanced but sits squarely in the survival/base-building crossover audience this beat follows, worth a first proper look once early community reaction settles.

## 3. ZeroSpace draws strong early reviews on day two of its early access run

**Source:** search-indexed Steam Community and GameTrader.SG coverage

A day after its early access launch (covered in yesterday's brief), ZeroSpace is drawing genuinely positive early reviews, with players describing it as "the best option as a successor to SC2 out there currently" and praising army control as comparable to Blizzard's RTS work. The early access build ships with 13 campaign missions, 10 co-op missions, 4 main factions, 6 mercenary factions and 10 heroes. No day-two player-count figures are available yet; worth checking again once the First Contact Invitational gets properly underway over the coming weeks.
