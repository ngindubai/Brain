## 1. Warhammer Blood Bowl announced as a full standalone release this fall, free upgrade for Blood Bowl 3 owners

**Source:** [Worthplaying](https://worthplaying.com/article/2026/7/22/news/150528-warhammer-blood-bowl-is-coming-to-pc-xbox-and-playstation-this-fall-free-upgrade-for-blood-bowl-3-owners-demo-soon-screens/), [Strategy and Wargaming](https://strategyandwargaming.com/)

Revealed yesterday at the Slitherine Next showcase, one day too late for yesterday's report, so covered here: Warhammer Blood Bowl ships this fall on PC, PlayStation and Xbox as a major standalone update to Blood Bowl 3, built on the tabletop's current Third Season Edition ruleset. It launches with 26 factions at launch, including new Bretonnian and Tomb Kings teams, a faster 7-a-side "Rumble Mode," and a reworked UI. Existing Blood Bowl 3 owners on PC and console get it as a free upgrade with all purchased teams and cosmetics carried over. This follows Slitherine's acquisition of the Blood Bowl licence from Nacon, which is in insolvency proceedings, with developer Cyanide Studio staying on the project. Worth a first-look once the promised summer demo lands.

## 2. Carnival Hunt launches today: asymmetric survival horror in a shifting mechanical carnival

**Source:** search-indexed Steam release coverage

Carnival Hunt released today on Steam, an asymmetric multiplayer horror title set inside a living, ever-shifting mechanical carnival: one player controls the Carnival Monster while the rest play puppet "Bunnies" trying to survive, ascend and escape the hunt. It sits at the survival-horror edge of this beat's secondary focus rather than its core, closer to a party horror game than a systems-heavy survival/base-builder, but it's today's one clean release in scope and worth a passing note.
