## 1. ZeroSpace enters early access today — the primary beat's genuine release of the day

**Source:** [Massively Overpowered](https://massivelyop.com/2026/05/31/kickstarted-mmo-ish-rts-zerospace-announces-a-july-20th-early-access-launch/), [Games Press](https://www.gamespress.com/en-US/ZeroSpace-Hits-250000-Wishlists-Ahead-of-July-20-Early-Access-Launch), [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1605850/ZeroSpace/)

ZeroSpace, Starlance Studios' Kickstarted sci-fi RTS/RPG hybrid, launched into early access today with its campaign, co-op Galactic War mode and competitive Skirmish all included. Two features anchor the launch: the First Contact Invitational, a 20-day tournament bringing twelve RTS pros into the game from scratch to compete on the ladder and in a culminating event; and Galactic War: Season Zero, a two-week community-driven mode (ending 2 August) where players align with one of three creator-led alliances to compete for control of the galaxy and crown a "Galactic Emperor." Early community reaction is mixed — some players are flagging that the game runs on Unreal Engine 4 after roughly seven years of development, calling the engine choice dated for a 2026 release, while others say it already looks stronger than competing titles in the genre. Worth watching how the invitational format plays out over the next three weeks as a marketing device other RTS launches may copy.

## 2. FiveM for GTA V Enhanced is one day out — no new Asset Escrow date, guidance unchanged

**Source:** [GTA BOOM](https://www.gtaboom.com/gta-v-roleplay-is-about-to-get-a-major-upgrade-on-july-21-cd6d), [Cfx Forum Development Update #3](https://forum.cfx.re/t/development-update-3-fivem-for-gtav-enhanced/5415045)

FiveM for GTA V Enhanced enters early access tomorrow, 21 July, running on a separate client and server stack — rebranded "Cfx Server" — from GTA V Legacy. The confirmed spec sheet: sync rate up from 30 to 120 updates per second, a rebuilt OneSync engine targeting beyond the current 2,048-player cap, up to 50% lower RAM usage, WebSocket-based initial connections for better DDoS resistance, and a new .NET-based C# scripting runtime phasing out Mono. Backward compatibility for existing Lua, JavaScript and C# scripts is confirmed. What hasn't changed since Cfx.re's most recent developer post (15 July): no Asset Escrow support at launch, and no timeline given for when it arrives. That keeps yesterday's guidance for NORTHLANDS in place without any need to revise it going into tomorrow's launch — if the server runs escrow-protected paid resources, staying on Legacy through the early access period remains the safer default until Cfx.re actually confirms an Enhanced escrow date.
