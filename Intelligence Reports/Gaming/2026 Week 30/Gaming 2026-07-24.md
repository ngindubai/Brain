## 1. Buildia launches on Steam
**Source:** Steam / aggregated release trackers | https://gg.deals/game/buildia/

Buildia, a voxel base-building sandbox from solo developer Ali Turgut, released today on Steam. Players build structures using elemental blocks (lava, ice, forest) in a creative, precision-focused mode with a deliberately serene, minimalist interface. Niche and small-scale, but a clean data point for the base-building/sandbox sub-genre's continued output this month.

## 2. Sol One revealed — survival/automation hybrid
**Source:** OpenCritic | https://opencritic.com/news/34943/new-open-world-survival-crafting-game-on-steam-is-satisfactory-meets-the-planet-crafter

Sol One was revealed this week, pitched as Satisfactory meets The Planet Crafter with a dash of Subnautica: open-world exploration, automation and base building on an alien world, dropping players in alone. Early Access launch is set for 30 July on Steam. Modest scale, but adds to the crowded survival/base-building pipeline alongside Category 6 and The Dead We Knew, both still weeks out.

## 3. Star Wars Zero Company reveals voice cast
**Source:** PC Gamer | https://www.pcgamer.com/games/strategy/star-wars-zero-company-reveals-cast-including-fan-favorite-actors-from-clone-wars-animated-series/

BitReactor's squad-tactics strategy game (ex-Firaxis XCOM developers) revealed its cast ahead of a San Diego Comic-Con panel on 25 July: Jonathan Freeman and Erica Luttrell voice protagonist Hawks, with Dee Bradley Baker, Kevin Michael Richardson and Clone Wars alum Matt Lanter in supporting roles. The game ships 27 August for PS5, Xbox Series X|S and PC at $50, built around permadeath squad tactics. Not RTS proper, but the closest thing to fresh strategy-genre news today.

## 4. Rockstar Game Workers Union holds first meeting with management
**Source:** GamesRadar+ / TechPowerUp | https://www.gamesradar.com/games/grand-theft-auto/after-claiming-gta-6-devs-have-crunch-built-into-their-contracts-union-says-first-meeting-with-rockstar-management-was-a-step-in-the-right-direction/

The Rockstar Game Workers Union — now the largest game studio union in Europe — confirmed its first formal meeting with Rockstar management, reported in the last 24 hours. The union says it secured a "roadmap for a fast negotiation process" and is next pushing to agree a bargaining unit (the workers it will formally represent). This is the first concrete movement on the labour thread since the IWGB tribunal date was set, and lands four months out from GTA VI's 19 November launch — a period Rockstar can't afford disruption in.

## 5. GTA Online's weekly event rolls out the Kortz Center reward response
**Source:** GTA Boom / TheXboxHub | https://www.gtaboom.com/gta-online-july-pegassi-ignus-pursuit-update-f4d9

This week's GTA Online event (23–29 July) is the concrete form of Rockstar's response to last week's Kortz Center Heist payout backlash: a new Pegassi Ignus Pursuit law enforcement supercar, a rotated heist target, 5x GTA$/RP on HSW Races, quadruple Associate/Bodyguard pay, and a $400,000 bonus for a deathless heist run. No new backlash or reversal — this is Rockstar making good on the cash-reward increase flagged yesterday, not a fresh dispute.

## What this means for Orwell

Nothing here touches NORTHLANDS operationally — standing guidance holds: stay on FiveM Legacy through Enhanced's early access while the Asset Escrow gap stands, since escrow-protected paid resources still can't be relied on. The one thread worth watching is Rockstar's labour relations: a union representing the largest studio workforce in Europe four months from GTA VI launch is a stability signal for the whole GTA ecosystem FiveM depends on, though it's too early to call impact on Enhanced's development pace. The base-building/survival micro-releases (Buildia, Sol One) are noise, not signal, for the portfolio.
