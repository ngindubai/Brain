## 1. Nightmare Frontier exits early access into full 1.0 release today

**Source:** [Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3310950/Nightmare_Frontier/), search-indexed Games Press coverage

Nightmare Frontier, developed by Ice Code Games (the studio behind Hard West 2) and published by Slitherine, launched into its full 1.0 release today on PC after a year in early access. It's a tactical extraction looter set in an alternate late-19th-century America where a mysterious force has transformed an entire city into a nightmare: players lead bands of scavengers into the streets on high-risk, high-reward turn-based expeditions, recovering loot, recruiting survivors and deciding when to extract versus push further. The full release folds in a year's worth of community-driven refinements. It's a smaller launch than the two survival/society titles covered in this brief over the past two days, but sits squarely in the extraction/survival crossover this beat tracks, and is worth a first-impressions look once early post-launch reviews settle.

## 2. FiveM for GTA V Enhanced, day two: community frustration builds over patch authorship and the still-missing native Rockstar integration

**Source:** search-indexed RockstarINTEL and Sportskeeda coverage of the FiveM Enhanced update and community reaction

A day after Tuesday's early access launch, the mood has shifted from launch-day novelty to visible frustration. Prominent community figure Tez2 pointed out that roughly 90% of the improvements in the latest patch notes came from community contributions rather than from Cfx.re itself, despite Cfx.re now operating under Rockstar's ownership, feeding a broader complaint that Rockstar's own resourcing of the project looks thin relative to community effort. Compounding that: native GTA V Enhanced support from Rockstar directly (distinct from the third-party Cfx.re-built FiveM client covered in yesterday's report) was first announced back in February and has sat on "coming soon" for close to six months with no firm date. Asset Escrow remains absent from the FiveM Enhanced early access build, exactly as flagged yesterday, so the standing NORTHLANDS guidance, stay on Legacy through the early access window if the server runs escrow-protected paid resources, holds without any need to revise it. Worth revisiting once Cfx.re responds publicly to the community-authorship criticism, since that's a reputational thread as much as a technical one.
