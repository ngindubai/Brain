## 1. FiveM for GTAV Enhanced: Asset Escrow won't be ready for the 21 July Early Access launch

**Source:** [GamingHQ](https://gaminghq.eu/2026/07/16/fivem-gta-5-enhanced-early-access-july-21-performance-upgrade/), [GTA BOOM](https://www.gtaboom.com/gta-v-roleplay-is-about-to-get-a-major-upgrade-on-july-21-cd6d), [RockstarINTEL](https://rockstarintel.com/fivem-for-gta-v-enhanced-releases-next-week/)

Follow-up coverage of yesterday's Development Update #3 surfaced a detail with direct bearing on NORTHLANDS: Asset Escrow — the mechanism that protects and distributes paid scripts and resources — will not ship with the 21 July Early Access launch of FiveM for GTAV Enhanced. Cfx.re has said as much itself, and coverage is explicit that larger server operations running paid resources will likely prefer to stay on GTA V Legacy FiveM until Asset Escrow support arrives in a subsequent update. The two versions will run through entirely separate launchers going forward, so this isn't a soft transition — it's a genuine fork for as long as escrow support is missing. Other confirmed specs from the update: sync tick rate up to 120Hz (from 30-40), OneSync scaling toward the current 2,048-player cap and beyond, up to 50% memory savings on the server component, and full backward compatibility for existing Lua/JS/C# scripts. With four days to launch, this is the one action item worth flagging now rather than at go-live: if NORTHLANDS runs paid resources under escrow protection, staying on Legacy past 21 July is the safer default until Cfx.re confirms an Asset Escrow date for Enhanced.
