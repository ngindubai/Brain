## 1. GTA Online: The Kortz Center Heist and Title Update 1.73 go live
**Source:** [GTAbase, "GTA Online 'Kortz Center Heist' 1.73 Patch Notes, New Cars"](https://www.gtabase.com/articles/grand-theft-auto-v/title-updates/gta-online-summer-2026-dlc-update-1-73-patch-notes-features), updated 13-14 July 2026; [Gamerant, "GTA Online: Kortz Center Heist Release Date & Launch Times FAQ"](https://gamerant.com/gta-online-kortz-center-heist-release-date-faq/).

Title Update 1.73 unlocked today at roughly 10am UK / 09:00 UTC on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, PC and last-gen consoles. The centrepiece is The Kortz Center Heist, a multi-stage museum robbery in Pacific Bluffs playable solo or as a 4-player crew, run with fixer Raf De Angelis and the mysterious Mr Faber. It is Rockstar's first purpose-built new heist since Cayo Perico in December 2020, with multiple approaches, custom loadouts and several escape routes.

Entry requires a Mansion property upgraded with the new Art Studio extension; players who logged in during the pre-launch window got a $1,000,000 discount on that upgrade, a free Annihilator Stealth helicopter and Turreted Limo, and a Kortz Center Sculpture. The update also adds the Grotti Veleno GT supercar, free to GTA+ members from today. Coverage broadly frames this as the last major GTA Online content drop before GTA VI on 19 November, with speculation about story wrap-ups and character cameos. Rockstar had not published full official patch notes on its Newswire/Support site at time of writing — outlets are running on the pre-release breakdown; a fuller support-site changelog is expected to follow launch, as it has for prior title updates (1.70–1.72).

Community sentiment is mixed on cost: full heist entry (Mansion plus Art Studio) runs north of $12M pre-discount, and some players are questioning replay value for a heist a chunk of the base may only run a handful of times before GTA VI absorbs attention this autumn.

## 2. Urban Strife leaves Early Access with its 1.0 release
**Source:** [BigBossBattle, "Tactical Survival Game Urban Strife To Release From Early Access With Major Update"](https://bigbossbattle.com/tactical-survival-game-urban-strife-to-release-from-early-access-with-major-update/); [TurnBasedLovers, "The Spiritual Successor to Jagged Alliance 2 Finally Gets a 1.0 Release Date"](https://turnbasedlovers.com/news/the-spiritual-successor-to-jagged-alliance-2-finally-gets-a-1-0-release-date/).

Urban Strife, developed by White Pond Games and published by MicroProse, exits roughly 18 months of Early Access today with its full 1.0 launch on Steam. It's a turn-based tactical survival RPG set in a devastated American South, combining Jagged Alliance-style squad combat (action points, overwatch, body-part targeting, simulated ballistics) with shelter management, faction politics and a population of survivors to feed, heal and defend.

The 1.0 build delivers the complete story campaign, including the previously missing final act — the arrival of the "Atlanta Horde" and a siege that determines the fate of the player's shelter. It's a solid, if niche, proof point that the survival-tactics hybrid genre (adjacent to base-building and colony-sim audiences) still has room for a full narrative release rather than endless early access.

## 3. Strategic Command: World War I – Battles for Ypres DLC releases
**Source:** [CombatSim.com, "Strategic Command WWI – Battles for Ypres Release Date"](http://www.combatsim.com/2026/07/strategic-command-wwi-battles-for-ypres-release-date.htm/); [Matrix Games news](https://www.matrixgames.com/news/strategic-command-wwi-battles-for-ypres-dlc-announcement-beta).

Matrix Games' divisional-level WWI wargame gets its Battles for Ypres DLC today. It adds six campaigns spanning 1914–1918, from the initial defence of First Ypres through to the final Allied push in Flanders, playable as either the Allies or Germans. New mechanics include gas attacks, creeping barrages, and the gradual introduction of tanks and aircraft as the war modernises across the scenarios. Announced 10 July, this is a straightforward niche-audience wargame release — solid content for the Strategic Command base but not a broader industry signal.

## 4. Ubisoft Barcelona walks out over layoffs
**Source:** [GameDeveloper.com, "Ubisoft Barcelona to go on strike over proposed layoffs"](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/ubisoft-barcelona-to-go-on-strike-over-proposed-layoffs); social-media confirmation of a three-day strike window of 14–16 July.

Ubisoft Barcelona staff are on a three-day strike starting today, escalating a labour dispute that began in late June over 51 proposed layoffs. The CGT-affiliated Video Game Union Coordination (CSVI) is pushing for the layoffs to be cancelled outright, a five-year no-mass-layoff guarantee, honouring stalled promotions and pay reviews, and reinstating the studio's previous 60%-remote work policy. Ubisoft is restructuring the studio to focus solely on Rainbow Six going forward, cutting it out of the open-world work it had been doing on Assassin's Creed Black Flag: Resynced — a title that has already sold over 2 million copies. It's another data point in a brutal year for the industry: alongside the Xbox studio sell-off/layoffs on 6 July and the id Software cuts that followed, 2026 is on track to be one of the worst years on record for developer job security.
