## Story 1: Project ROME — Rockstar's Own Modding Platform Is Coming With GTA VI

**Source:** Evolution Host | https://evolution-host.com/blog/everything-we-know-about-gta-rome.php | Kotaku (Cfx.re acquisition) | https://kotaku.com/grand-theft-auto-5-gta-6-red-dead-redemption-fivem-mod-1850728525
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]]

Project ROME — believed to stand for "Rockstar Online Modding Engine" — is Rockstar's own first-party modding and multiplayer platform, reportedly built for GTA VI. Expected features: custom multiplayer game modes with scripting support, user-hosted or Rockstar-hosted persistent servers, visual scripting tools, creator monetisation, and built-in moderation/security. This isn't speculation about a hypothetical third party — Rockstar acquired Cfx.re (the company behind FiveM and RedM) back in August 2023, and Cfx.re is now officially part of Rockstar Games, not an independent modding operation Rockstar merely tolerates.

The open question is whether ROME replaces FiveM/RedM outright or evolves as an official successor that current framework maintainers (QBox, ox ecosystem, etc.) migrate into. Recent hiring activity (a "Senior Product Manager, Creator Platform" role posted August 2025) and the January 2026 discovery of a dedicated FiveM Steam page both point to active, ongoing development rather than a shelved concept. No firm rollout date beyond "likely phased, at or after GTA VI's November 19 launch."

**NORTHLANDS flag (high priority):** This is bigger than the GTA VI launch date itself. If Rockstar builds creator monetisation and moderation directly into ROME, that's a fundamentally different commercial and legal environment than the current, informally-tolerated FiveM modding scene NORTHLANDS operates in — one with official server hosting options, built-in monetisation Rockstar takes a cut of, and presumably tighter content rules. NORTHLANDS should treat "what does ROME's monetisation and TOS actually require" as an open research question worth tracking now, well before November.

## Story 2: GTA V Enhanced Free Upgrade Live Since June 18 — FiveM Support "Coming Months" Away, No Forced Migration

**Source:** GameInformer (search snippet) | https://gameinformer.com/2026/06/17/rockstar-announces-free-ps5-and-xbox-series-xs-upgrade-for-grand-theft-auto-v | Zoov.dev bulletin | https://zoov.dev/kb/bulletin/gta-v-enhanced-gen9-fivem-support
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]]

Owners of PS4 or digital Xbox One versions of GTA V have been able to upgrade free to PS5/Xbox Series X/S versions since June 18. GTA V Enhanced itself launched on PC back on March 4, 2026. Cfx.re has confirmed FiveM support for the Enhanced version is coming "in the coming months," starting as early access.

Critically for server operators: this is not a forced transition. Legacy and Enhanced will run as separate, non-cross-play server versions, and server owners choose if/when to migrate. Cfx.re's own guidance is to plan separate rollout windows, testing servers, and resource rebuilds rather than rushing an upgrade.

**NORTHLANDS action:** No urgent change needed, but the free console upgrade wave means more of the existing GTA V player base will be sitting on Enhanced-capable hardware well before FiveM's Enhanced support even goes live. Worth deciding now whether NORTHLANDS times its eventual Enhanced migration to coincide with early access, or waits for a stable release — the zoov.dev guidance explicitly recommends the latter for RP-focused servers with built resource libraries.

## Story 4: Industry Backdrop — Godot Bans AI-Authored Contributions, IO Interactive Loses Funding

**Source:** PC Gamer RSS | https://www.pcgamer.com/rss/
**Related vault notes:** none

Two smaller industry items worth a line each.
