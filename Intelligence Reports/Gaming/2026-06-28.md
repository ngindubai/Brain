## Story 1: GTA 6 confirmed November 19 console release — pre-orders live, $80 price point, no disc

GTA 6 pre-orders opened June 25, 2026:
- Standard Edition: $79.99 (PS5, Xbox Series X/S)
- Ultimate Edition: $99.99 (extra in-game content)
- Release date: November 19, 2026 — delayed from the original May 26 window announced earlier this year

The $79.99 price point has attracted significant community backlash. The no-disc controversy is larger: physical edition boxes at launch will not contain a disc. Rockstar confirmed discs will arrive "months later," with one insider pointing to December. Physical buyers are paying $79.99 for a code in a box at launch. Community reaction has been sharply negative on both points.

Third trailer: as of June 28, still not released. The current marketing posture from Rockstar is minimal — one Newswire post about pre-orders, no new gameplay footage. The marketing push is expected to intensify significantly in August-October ahead of the November date.

**PC release**: Rockstar has confirmed no PC release date, no storefront, no system requirements as of June 28. PC is expected to follow console release by at minimum six months — earliest mid-2027, more likely Q3-Q4 2027 based on Rockstar's historical console-first strategy. This is a directly material fact for NORTHLANDS: the FiveM player base is PC. GTA 6 on console does not pull PC players immediately.

**NORTHLANDS impact:** The November 19 date sets the framework for the next five months. Between now and November 19, GTA V is in peak re-engagement mode — players revisiting the game before the sequel, franchise-adjacent content driving YouTube and Twitch traffic, new audiences entering GTA V RP servers. After November 19, console players migrate to GTA 6 but PC FiveM players have nowhere to go for at minimum 12-18 months. The NORTHLANDS strategic window is now through approximately Q1 2027 for the PC-based FiveM audience specifically.

## Story 2: FiveM is now the only legal GTA V multiplayer mod platform — migration opportunity
**Source:** GamesRadar | https://www.gamesradar.com/games/grand-theft-auto/after-9-years-of-work-team-behind-gta-5-multiplayer-mod-that-rivalled-rockstar-owned-fivem-says-take-two-is-shutting-the-project-down-thank-you-for-being-part-of-this-journey/ | GTA Boom | https://www.gtaboom.com/the-last-gta-v-multiplayer-alternative-just-got-a-cease-and-desist-3970 | Screen Rant | https://screenrant.com/gta-6-gta-online-ragemp-shutdown/ | Vertexmods | https://vertexmods.com/blog/project-rome-gta6-fivem-future
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]]

Take-Two Interactive has completed its legal sweep of GTA V multiplayer alternatives:

**alt:V**: received a cease-and-desist from Take-Two. Full shutdown deadline: July 6, 2026 — eight days from today. The alt:V team, which had nine years of development behind the project, posted a farewell message: "Thank you for being part of this journey." The closure means hundreds of alt:V servers and their player communities need a new home. FiveM is the only destination.

**RAGE:MP**: all 288 active RAGE:MP servers must close by August 31, 2026. That is approximately 60 days from today, meaning RAGE:MP server communities are actively planning their migration right now.

**FiveM**: the sole remaining platform. Rockstar acquired CFX.re (the FiveM team) in 2023 and has since formalised FiveM as the authorised GTA V multiplayer modding solution in its Platform License Agreement. The Cfx Marketplace launched January 2026 sells curated FiveM and RedM assets. Rockstar sets the rules, curates the content, and takes a marketplace cut.

**Framework landscape for 2026:**

QBox is the recommended framework for any new FiveM server in 2026. QBox is the actively maintained evolution of QBCore — rebuilt on the Ox ecosystem (ox_lib, ox_inventory, oxmysql), running at 0.42ms total CPU usage and ~30MiB memory, and maintaining 97.99% compatibility with existing QBCore resources. Original QBCore development has largely stalled. For any NORTHLANDS infrastructure decision, QBox is the modern baseline.

**Project ROME**: rumoured by community sources to be a Rockstar-built modding SDK integrated into GTA VI's core architecture — essentially an official, Rockstar-controlled version of FiveM for GTA 6. No official announcement. Given the November 19 console-only release and uncertain PC timeline, Project ROME is not an immediate operational concern. FiveM on GTA V will remain the primary modding platform for at minimum 12-18 months.

**NORTHLANDS action items:**

1. **Capture alt:V migration**: alt:V communities shut down July 6. NORTHLANDS should be visible in alt:V Discord servers, Reddit, and CFX forum threads discussing migration destinations. The window is the next 8 days, then the 2-4 weeks post-shutdown as communities settle into new servers.

2. **Capture RAGE:MP migration**: 288 servers closing by August 31. Same playbook — presence in migration discussions now, active recruitment of displaced communities in July-August.

3. **Assess QBox migration**: if NORTHLANDS runs on original QBCore, the migration path to QBox is documented and resource-compatible at 97.99%. Migrating before the GTA 6 hype peak would ensure the server is on the most performant and actively maintained stack when traffic spikes.
