## Story 1: GTA VI Pre-Orders Live — November 19 Launch, $79.99, No Real Disc

**Source:** Business Wire | https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260624539426/en/Rockstar-Games-Announces-Pre-Orders-for-Grand-Theft-Auto-VI | Fortune | https://fortune.com/2026/06/25/gta-6-release-date-price-no-disc-rockstar-games/
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]]

Grand Theft Auto VI pre-orders launched June 25 at midnight local time, with a confirmed release date of November 19, 2026 on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S. Price: $79.99 standard, $99.99 for the Ultimate Edition (includes exclusive vehicles, weapons, apparel, and action items). All pre-orders and purchases before November 20 include the Vintage Vice City Pack. Digital pre-orders get a free month of GTA+.

The physical edition controversy: physical versions ship on November 12 (one week before digital launch) but contain only a download code, not a disc. Rockstar has effectively ended physical media for GTA without announcing it. The backlash has been significant — PC Gamer flagged similar industry friction around Xbox's "not on Game Pass" Call of Duty ad, suggesting publisher-consumer trust is strained across the sector right now.

Rockstar partnered with Sony PlayStation for the marketing campaign. Cover art dropped June 18, pre-orders June 25. The Sony exclusivity on marketing (not gameplay) suggests PlayStation gets some window before Xbox marketing launches.

**NORTHLANDS flag (high priority):** GTA VI launches November 19, 2026 — five months away. This is the most significant platform-risk event for NORTHLANDS since FiveM launched. The current FiveM ecosystem is built on GTA V (2013 base game). GTA VI will siphon players from GTA V for a sustained period post-launch, as happened when GTAO received major updates historically.

However, the FiveM community response will be different this time. GTA VI's online mode will take months to establish modding support. The FiveM/CFX team will need time to build a compatible framework for GTA VI. During that period — likely November 2026 through mid-2027 — GTA V FiveM servers may actually see retention issues as casual players migrate, while dedicated roleplay communities may stay on GTA V because the GTA VI RP ecosystem will not be mature.

**Action for NORTHLANDS:** Assess current server population trends. Plan a GTA VI migration strategy: will NORTHLANDS eventually move to GTA VI, or position as the premium GTA V roleplay experience during the transition period? A well-run GTA V server with stable content can actually attract quality roleplay players who are tired of the chaotic post-launch GTA VI environment.

## Story 2: FiveM / QBox Framework — Current State, No Breaking June Update

**Source:** CFX.re community (web search) | https://forum.cfx.re/c/cfxre-announcements/66 | https://forum.cfx.re/t/qbox-project-wip/4917962
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]]

No breaking QBox or FiveM framework update was confirmed for June 2026 specifically. The current state of the FiveM framework landscape:

**QBox** is the active, community-maintained successor to QBCore. It uses `qbx_core` (replacing `qb-core`) and is built on the ox ecosystem: `ox_lib`, `ox_inventory`, `oxmysql`. For servers launching new in 2026, QBox is the recommended choice — QBCore development has largely stalled. QBox runs Lua 5.4 natively, integrates the ox stack, and has a rapidly growing script library.

**Framework stability in June:** No major breaking changes reported. The QBox project thread on CFX.re shows active development but no release that would force migration or require immediate NORTHLANDS action.

**Anticipation mode:** The FiveM development community is in a holding pattern watching the GTA VI situation. CFX.re (Rockstar's parent company of FiveM since 2023) will need to make decisions about GTA VI framework support timelines. No official announcement has been made. The critical question for NORTHLANDS: when will there be a FiveM equivalent for GTA VI, and what will QBox compatibility look like?
