## Story 1: GTA VI pre-orders open June 25; launch November 19, 2026 for PS5/Xbox Series X|S at $79.99

**Source:** Rockstar Games (via web search) | https://www.rockstargames.com/newswire/article/517oa135328155/grand-theft-auto-vi-pre-orders-begin-on-june-25
**Additional source:** BusinessWire | https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260624539426/en/Rockstar-Games-Announces-Pre-Orders-for-Grand-Theft-Auto-VI
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]] (NORTHLANDS)

Rockstar revealed the GTA VI cover art on June 18, and pre-orders opened on June 25 at midnight local time. The game launches November 19, 2026 on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S at $79.99 standard edition. A Grand Theft Auto VI: Ultimate Edition is available at $99.99, including exclusive vehicles, weapons, and apparel. Physical editions (download code only, no disc) are available from November 12.

All pre-orders and purchases before November 20 include the Vintage Vice City Pack plus one free month of GTA+. Rockstar has partnered with Sony Interactive Entertainment's PlayStation as the platform marketing partner.

**NORTHLANDS FiveM impact — DIRECT FLAG:**

GTA VI launches in seven months (November 19). FiveM is a multiplayer modification for GTA V; GTA VI is a separate title and FiveM is not officially compatible with it at launch (Cfx.re will need to develop a FiveM-equivalent for GTA VI, which is typically a 6-18 month post-launch process). This means:

1. **Player migration:** A significant proportion of the GTA V / FiveM player base will spend time on GTA VI from November. NORTHLANDS can expect population dips in the November-January period as players explore the new game.
2. **FiveM lifespan:** GTA V FiveM will not disappear overnight — the established role-play and custom server ecosystem has years of residual value. GTA V maintained a strong player base for over a decade. But the peak of FiveM's server population growth is likely behind it once GTA VI launches.
3. **Counter-programming:** The window between now and November is the time to build NORTHLANDS content, community, and events that create enough server-specific loyalty to retain players through the GTA VI launch period. Content that GTA VI cannot replicate immediately (established community, custom lore, persistent economy progression) is the retention lever.
4. **GTA VI FiveM:** When Cfx.re does develop a FiveM-equivalent for GTA VI (likely 2027), NORTHLANDS would need to evaluate migration. Plan for this as a 12-18 month horizon decision.

## Story 2: PC memory prices forecast +40-50% Q3, +30-40% Q4; no recovery until 2028

**Source:** PC Gamer | https://www.pcgamer.com/hardware/memory-prices-are-predicted-to-rise-as-much-as-50-percent-in-q3-and-it-only-gets-worse-from-there/
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]] (NORTHLANDS)

Consumer RAM prices are forecast to increase 40-50% in Q3 2026 and a further 30-40% in Q4, driven by AI data centres consuming the same DRAM supply that serves consumer PC hardware. Meaningful price recovery is not expected until 2028.

**NORTHLANDS implication:** The FiveM player hardware upgrade cycle will slow. New players considering building or upgrading a PC to run GTA V at playable settings on a FiveM server will face a materially higher cost. This is a headwind to organic player on-boarding. The practical response is ensuring NORTHLANDS performs well on a wider range of hardware specs — testing and communicating minimum requirements for good server performance, and avoiding resource-heavy scripts that raise the minimum playable spec.

## Story 3: QBox framework status — active maintained FiveM successor; no breaking June update

**Source:** QBox project (web search) | https://www.qbox.re/
**Additional source:** Cfx.re forum | https://forum.cfx.re/t/qbox-project-wip/4917962
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]] (NORTHLANDS)

No breaking QBox framework update was released in June 2026. The framework status:

- QBox (qbx_core) remains the actively maintained successor to QBCore. QBCore development has largely stalled; QBox is the recommended foundation for new servers launched in 2026.
- The QBox stack uses modern Lua 5.4, native ox ecosystem integration (ox_lib, ox_inventory, oxmysql), and reports CPU performance of 0.42ms total and ~30MiB memory usage — well within server spec.
- No June 2026 patch releases flagged in forum threads as of this report.

If NORTHLANDS is running QBCore rather than QBox, migration to QBox is now the community consensus best practice for long-term server health. The migration involves replacing qb-core with qbx_core and updating dependent resources. This should be evaluated as a planned maintenance task rather than an emergency — but it becomes more urgent as the QBCore script ecosystem gradually deprecates in favour of QBox/ox-compatible scripts.
