## Story 1: Manor Lords ships Update 7, a major pathfinding and locomotion overhaul

**Source:** PCGamesN | https://www.pcgamesn.com/manor-lords/update-7 | Steam news hub | https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/1363080/view/698769997400375323
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]]

Manor Lords' Update 7 went live yesterday, 10 July, and is still the freshest base-building news landing today. It's a technical-focused patch rather than a content drop: a reworked "island" pathfinding system rejects impossible routes (a worker on a riverbank with no bridge no longer tries to path through it), refactored character locomotion and collision, an overhaul of worker task logic, full gamepad support, redesigned Game Setup and loading screens, and a long list of bug fixes covering stuck workers, broken AI and construction/save issues. Developer Greg Styczeń signalled the next update cycle shifts back to content.

This is exactly the base-building design-pattern beat this brief tracks: a solo/small-team-built city-builder investing a full patch cycle in pathfinding and simulation robustness before returning to content is a useful reference point, not something with any direct Orwell action attached.

## Story 2: Palworld 1.0 weekend numbers land — strong but short of 2024 peak, reviews split on tone

**Source:** Forbes | https://www.forbes.com/sites/paultassi/2026/07/10/palworld-10-is-putting-up-enormous-steam-player-numbers-at-launch/ | GamesRadar+ | https://www.gamesradar.com/games/survival/palworld-1-0-sees-the-survival-games-biggest-steam-player-spike-since-its-record-breaking-early-access-launch-and-96-percent-positive-reviews-better-than-even-pokemon/
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]]

The first real data point on yesterday's 1.0 launch: Palworld peaked at 472,769 concurrent Steam players around 11pm JST on 10 July, roughly 10x its pre-launch daily average of ~50,000, briefly crossing 500,000 around 11am ET. That's a genuinely strong spike but well short of its January 2024 early-access all-time peak of ~2.02m, consistent with the pre-launch read that Game Pass and console availability would spread demand rather than concentrate it on Steam. Steam user reviews are running around 96% positive. Critical reception is more split: IGN, PC Invasion, PCGamesN and GameSpot praised the combat and tone, while Rock Paper Shotgun and PC Gamer criticised the game's shock-humour treatment of animal cruelty and sweatshop-labour themes as over-committing to the bit.

Purely a survival/base-building genre reference point, no NORTHLANDS relevance. Closes out the loop opened yesterday.

## Story 3: Subnautica 2 ships its first Early Access content update

**Source:** GameRant | https://gamerant.com/subnautica-2-today-patch-notes/
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]]

Unknown Worlds shipped Subnautica 2's first community-driven Early Access patch on 8 July, still fresh enough into the weekend cycle to flag today: two new biomes (Coral Gardens, Axum Ruins), Biomod capacity raised from four to six slots with new scannable passives, a co-op fix so audio logs no longer interrupt other players, new wreck routes, oxygen puzzles, and above-water sprinting. The studio confirmed more than 5m copies sold within a month of Early Access launch. Another genuine survival/base-building data point, logged for genre-tracking purposes rather than any direct relevance.
