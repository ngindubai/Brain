## Story 1: Company of Heroes 3 gets a standalone roguelike wave-defense expansion, "Final Stand," 29 July

**Source:** Strategy and Wargaming | https://strategyandwargaming.com/2026/07/05/strategy-and-wargaming-news-5-july-2026-slitherine-takes-over-blood-bowl-new-company-of-heroes-3-dlc-dawn-of-war-iv-multiplayer-match-battleplan-delayed-new-total-war-warhammer-iii-dlc-teased/
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]]

Relic's Company of Heroes 3 is getting a standalone roguelike expansion called Final Stand, launching 29 July. It's a wave-defense mode (single-player and co-op) across 12 rounds, 8 difficulty levels, 5 purpose-built battlegrounds and 36 boss units, drawing explicit inspiration from Dawn of War II: The Last Stand and Age of Empires IV's Crucible mode. Not directly Orwell-relevant, but worth noting as another established RTS franchise leaning into roguelike wave-defense as a retention mechanic — a pattern that keeps recurring across the genre this year.

## Story 2: Battleplan delayed to Q3 2026

**Source:** Strategy and Wargaming (same roundup as above)
**Related vault notes:** none directly

The tactical strategy game Battleplan has slipped from its original July release to Q3 2026. The developer cites extra time needed for battle-plan drafting tools, precision controls, army-management functionality, bug fixes and general polish. Standard pre-release delay, logged for the wargaming shelf.

## Story 3: Dawn of War IV shows a 1v1 multiplayer match; mixed reception on polish

**Source:** Strategy and Wargaming (same roundup)

## Story 4: ARK: Survival Ascended ships Genesis Part 1, adds the Burrowbuck creature

**Source:** Steam Community patch notes | https://steamcommunity.com/app/1962700/allnews/ (via search aggregation)
**Related vault notes:** [[Products]]

ARK: Survival Ascended's Genesis Part 1 update landed 3 July, bringing back legacy Genesis creatures, items, structures and environments, headlined by the new Burrowbuck — a fast, evasive utility creature that can dig hidden underground dens and open temporary gateway tunnels for rapid travel. Relevant as a base-building/survival design reference point rather than for any direct Orwell tie; the underground-den/gateway-tunnel mechanic is a mildly interesting base-defence pattern if NORTHLANDS ever wants a reference for hidden-cache or fast-travel systems.
