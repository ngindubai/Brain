## Story 1: Anthropic explores building its own AI chip, in talks with Samsung as manufacturing partner

**Source:** The Information (original report) | https://www.theinformation.com/articles/anthropic-talks-samsung-manufacture-custom-ai-chip — corroborated by TechCrunch (2 July) | https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/02/anthropic-is-discussing-a-new-custom-chip-with-samsung/ and UPI (3 July) | https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2026/07/03/Anthropic-Samsung-Electronics/7811783128641/

Anthropic has begun early-stage work on a custom AI chip and held talks with Samsung Electronics as a potential manufacturing partner, following the same path OpenAI took with its Broadcom-designed "Jalapeño" inference chip, unveiled 24 June. Three sources told The Information the discussions are exploratory: Anthropic hasn't yet decided what the chip should be optimised for, how powerful it needs to be, or how it slots into a server rack. No prototype exists and no manufacturing timeline has been set. The chip under discussion would reportedly use Samsung's 2-nanometre process and advanced packaging.

The Samsung connection isn't new — Samsung, alongside SK Hynix and Micron, was a strategic investor in Anthropic's $65 billion Series H round in May, explicitly as an infrastructure partner rather than a pure financial one. What's new is the personnel signal: Anthropic recently hired Clive Chan, the second engineer ever to join OpenAI's dedicated custom-chip team, who spent two and a half years building the Jalapeño accelerator before moving. Hiring the person who just shipped a rival's chip program is a stronger tell of intent than the Samsung talks alone.

Why it matters: every major AI lab is now trying to reduce dependence on Nvidia, either for cost, supply security, or workload-specific efficiency. Anthropic doing this is unremarkable in isolation — OpenAI, Google and Amazon are all further along their own silicon programmes — but it confirms the compute-independence race has widened to include Anthropic specifically, the lab Orwell depends on for Claude Code and the Max plan.

Signal for Orwell: nothing actionable today — this is a multi-year infrastructure bet with no near-term effect on API pricing, availability, or model roadmap. Filed as background: it's a mild positive signal for Anthropic's long-term compute security, which matters if Orwell's build tooling stays anchored to Claude Code for years rather than months.

## arXiv watch

- **Service Agent Control in Customer Operations** — routes service requests by complexity and uses conflict-aware communication to catch operational errors before they hit backend writes. Directly relevant to any Orwell agent handling customer-facing operations without human review. https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01426
- **CausalSteward: An Agentic Divide-Conquer-Combine Copilot for Causal Discovery** — human-in-the-loop framework that partitions high-dimensional causal analysis across collaborating agents. A usable pattern reference for [[Agent Org]] designs that need staged human checkpoints rather than full autonomy. https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01936
- **Adoption and Ecosystem Health: Open-Source Multi-Agent Frameworks Analysis** — argues contributor density and cross-framework engagement are better ecosystem-health signals than GitHub stars alone. Directly relevant to how the still-pending OpenClaw evaluation should be scored, not just "it has the most stars." https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02453
- **Agent4cs: Multi-agent System for Code Summarization** — specialised agents for summarisation, keyword extraction and QA analyse large codebases bottom-up. Relevant pattern for any future Orwell tooling that needs to document or summarise client codebases at scale. https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01425
- **Cache Merging as a Convergent Replicated State for Multi-Agent Latent Reasoning** — a method ("CanonicalMerge") for combining agent KV-caches that gives consistent results regardless of input order. Technical, but relevant to the cost-governance thread: cache reuse across agents is one lever for cutting redundant compute. https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01308
