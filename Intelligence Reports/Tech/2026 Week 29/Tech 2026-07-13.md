## Story 1: Asian AI-chip stock selloff has a second read beyond the markets angle: buildout timing risk

**Source:** CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/12/stock-market-today-live-updates.html (also covered in today's [[Markets]] brief, Story 1)
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Agent Org]]

SK Hynix fell around 5% in Seoul and Samsung Electronics dropped more than 6% at Monday's open, with SoftBank, Tokyo Electron, Advantest and Renesas all down too, on the weekend's renewed US-Iran strikes and Iran's re-declaration that the Strait of Hormuz is closed. [[Markets]] has the full trading picture; the angle worth adding here is infrastructure-specific: SK Hynix and Samsung are the two firms supplying the HBM memory that every frontier AI training cluster depends on, and both are also mid-buildout on US fab capacity (SK Hynix just closed a record $26.5bn US IPO on 10 July with pressure to build new US fabs attached). A sustained regional risk premium that raises financing costs or delays fab timelines for either company is a slower-moving but more structural threat to AI compute supply than a single day's share-price move suggests. One session isn't a trend; worth checking whether this reverses once TSMC's delayed June revenue print (due today, covered in [[Markets]] Story 3) lands.
