## Story 1: Alibaba ran 28.8 million Claude queries through 25,000 fake accounts to train Qwen
**Source:** CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/24/anthropic-alibaba-distillation-campaign.html | The Next Web | https://thenextweb.com/news/anthropic-accuses-alibaba-distillation-claude-qwen | Tom's Hardware | https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/anthropic-claims-that-chinas-alibaba-illicitly-distilled-its-models-from-april-to-june-2026-says-effort-involved-25-000-fake-accounts-and-28-8-million-exchanges-on-claude
**Related vault notes:** [[Agent Org]], [[Products]], [[Agentic AI Keyword Forecast]]

In a letter dated 10 June and sent to US Senators Tim Scott and Elizabeth Warren, Anthropic accused Alibaba and its Qwen AI lab of running the largest known distillation attack on a US AI company to date. Between 22 April and 5 June 2026, operators linked to Alibaba generated 28.8 million exchanges with Claude through approximately 25,000 fraudulent accounts. The campaign specifically targeted Claude's most commercially valuable capabilities: software engineering, multi-step reasoning, and agentic task completion.

Distillation, also called model extraction, works like this. An attacker creates a large number of accounts, runs millions of carefully designed queries against a frontier model, collects the outputs, and uses those outputs as training data for a smaller, cheaper competing model. The attacker does not obtain the target model's weights or source code. What they obtain is a high-quality synthetic training dataset generated at the expense of the original developer's compute and R&D investment. The resulting model cannot fully replicate the original, but it can approximate its capabilities on the most-queried tasks at a fraction of the training cost. Building a frontier model costs billions. This technique costs server time and coordination.

This is not Anthropic's first accusation of this kind. Earlier in 2026 the company accused DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax of collectively running 16 million Claude exchanges through roughly 24,000 fraudulent accounts. The Alibaba campaign is nearly twice that scale and targets a single company, suggesting systematic escalation. Anthropic warned in its letter that the campaign is designed to help Alibaba's Qwen model approach the capabilities of Anthropic's Mythos Preview model, which the US government has already flagged as a national security risk.

For Orwell: this directly affects the model access picture. If Anthropic responds to distillation attacks by tightening API access controls, rate limits, or account verification requirements, that affects the cost and reliability of the agent stack.
