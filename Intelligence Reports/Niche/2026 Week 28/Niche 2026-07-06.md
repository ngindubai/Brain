## 2. Pet Transport
**Primary source:** IPATA.org, IATA LAR 52nd Edition documentation  
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[pet-transport]], [[Agent Org]]

### IATA LAR 52nd Edition: Brachycephalic Breed Rules Now Enforcement-Live
**Source:** IATA | https://www.iata.org/contentassets/80b3908be0ca4ebe8d359292c46cdac2/20260101-lar-addendum-i-v1.0-final_ext.pdf ; blog.gettransport.com | https://blog.gettransport.com/news/live-animal-transport-regulations-2026/

The IATA Live Animals Regulations 52nd Edition came into force 1 January 2026 but its practical impact on operators is intensifying now as airlines complete their compliance rollouts. The headline change for pet transport: brachycephalic dog and cat breeds (bulldogs, pugs, Persian cats, and related snub-nosed breeds) are no longer permitted in cargo holds, except for confirmed service animals. This is a hard prohibition, not a recommendation. For IPATA members who have been handling brachycephalic cargo shipments, this triggers an immediate service redesign -- the only lawful option for most owners is in-cabin travel, which has its own size restrictions, or specialist veterinary ground transport.

The container rules are also tightening. The CR1 container (previously used loosely across species) now requires strict species-specific compliance with new visual guides. The 52nd Edition mandates that snub-nosed breeds which do travel require containers 10% larger than standard sizing. Ventilation and absorbent bedding requirements are explicitly codified. The formerly ambiguous CR2 exceptions have been reworded for clarity, with horse transport now defined by weight criteria rather than breed type, and enclosure specifications added.

Electronic documentation is being developed to replace paper-based compliance tracking -- a significant operational change for members still running manual paperwork. The new Live Animals Working Group is the ongoing forum for refining these standards.

### IATA In-Cabin Pet Travel Initiative
**Source:** IATA | https://www.iata.org/en/publications/newsletters/iata-knowledge-hub/making-in-cabin-pet-travel-easier-iata-new-initiatives/

IATA is developing a standardised in-cabin pet travel journey map and guidance document in collaboration with airlines, pet shippers, and other stakeholders. This signals a policy shift: rather than pushing all pets into cargo, the industry is trying to formalise in-cabin travel. For IPATA members, this is both opportunity (higher-margin, lower-stress service) and threat (airlines may eventually handle in-cabin bookings directly without a third-party agent).

### IPATA: Scam Landscape and Membership Position
**Source:** IPATA | https://www.ipata.org/

IPATA continues to flag the scam epidemic -- fraudulent companies using the IPATA name to defraud pet owners. The association itself does not ship animals; any company with "IPATA" in its name is operating illegally. With 485+ members across 90+ countries, the legitimate network is large but the scam noise is eroding consumer trust in the sector, which creates a content and differentiation opportunity for member-verified operators.

## 3. Funeral Repatriation
**Primary source:** NAFD.org.uk, gov.uk, commonslibrary.parliament.uk  
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[funeral-repatriation]]

### Statutory Regulation: Government Engagement Continues, No Announcement
**Source:** NAFD | https://www.nafd.org.uk/2024/10/15/nafd-welcomes-fuller-inquiry-call-for-statutory-regulation-of-the-funeral-sector/ ; Parliament | https://www.parallelparliament.co.uk/debate/2026-03-09/commons/commons-chamber/funeral-directors-regulation ; House of Commons Library | https://commonslibrary.parliament.uk/research-briefings/cbp-10475/

The David Fuller Inquiry's phase 2 report (published 2025) called for an independent statutory regulatory scheme for funeral directors in England "as a matter of urgency." Three government departments are now engaged: the Ministry of Justice, the Department for Business and Trade, and the Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government. The cross-departmental involvement reflects the complexity of where funeral regulation sits constitutionally -- it spans end-of-life care, trading standards, and property law simultaneously.

The March 2026 Commons debate on funeral directors regulation kept the issue live in Parliament but produced no legislation. The sector remains in a pre-statutory period where NAFD and SAIF self-regulate via voluntary codes. The significant development this year is that both bodies are aligning on a common core code structure -- NAFD and SAIF plan to share the same underlying code framework, presenting a unified face to government. A new UK Funeral Director Code was published in January 2026, signalling the sector is pre-building for mandatory adoption.

For repatriation specifically: there is no separate repatriation regulatory framework in England and Wales. Repatriation sits inside the broader funeral director licensing question. Scotland is further ahead, with Scottish members actively preparing under NAFD guidance for the incoming Scottish regulatory regime.

### What This Means for Repatriation Operators
The regulatory gap creates both risk and opportunity. Until statutory licensing is in force, there is no barrier to entry for repatriation services -- any firm can offer them. The risk is that when regulation arrives, compliance costs will hit smaller or less-organised operators hardest. Operators who pre-build to the NAFD/SAIF code standard now will have documentation to show an incoming regulator. No specific repatriation policy changes this week.

## 4. Close Protection
**Primary source:** gov.uk SIA, secureonsitesecurity.co.uk, proforcesecurity.co.uk  
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[closeprotectionhure-com]]

### Mandatory Refresher Training: April Deadline Passed, Renewals Now Affected
**Source:** gov.uk | https://www.gov.uk/government/news/sia-announces-new-refresher-training-for-close-protection-operatives ; secureonsitesecurity.co.uk | https://www.secureonsitesecurity.co.uk/sia-strategic-review-2026/

From 1 April 2026, all close protection operatives renewing their SIA licence must complete a refresher qualification. The training has been available since October 2025 -- the six-month window is closed. Any CP operative who has not yet completed their refresher and whose licence falls due for renewal is now legally required to do so before renewal is approved. This is the most operationally significant regulatory change to close protection licensing since the original top-up awards were introduced in 2022.

The refresher covers: physical intervention, conducting searches, protecting vulnerable individuals, counter-terror threat awareness, and first aid. From April 2026, a Level 3 First Aid at Work qualification is the formal prerequisite for CP renewals -- a step up from earlier requirements. Importantly, CP licence holders who also cover door supervision and security guarding roles must complete refresher elements relevant to all three activities, since a CP badge automatically covers those lower-tier roles.

### SIA 2026 Strategic Review: Final Qualification Specifications Imminent
**Source:** secureonsitesecurity.co.uk | https://www.secureonsitesecurity.co.uk/sia-strategic-review-2026/

The SIA's major strategic review of all licence-linked qualifications is at a critical juncture. Draft specifications went out for public consultation in early spring 2026. Final specifications are expected in summer 2026 -- meaning the published results could arrive within weeks of this report. The review covers close protection alongside door supervision, security guarding, CCTV, and other regulated roles, and is described as shifting from "tick-box licensing" to "competency-led" qualifications.

The new content areas being embedded: counter-terror modules, drone awareness, social media auditor scenarios, spiking awareness and victim support, English language testing, and enhanced trainer standards. Training providers will redesign courses from autumn 2026 through spring 2027, with new mandatory qualifications going live for new applicants from spring 2027. Existing licence holders with a valid badge are not immediately affected by the 2027 changes, but their next renewal after spring 2027 will require the new qualification.

### Demand Context
Summer is traditionally elevated for close protection work -- corporate travel, high-net-worth family holidays, and events. The refresher training requirement is creating friction in the supply pool: operatives who have not yet completed it cannot renew, which tightens available licensed capacity during peak season. This is a short-term supply constraint, not a long-term one.

## 5. Bus Hire
**Primary source:** busandcoachbuyer.com, bus-news.com

### DVSA ATF Lane MOT Charge Cap Rises to £90 -- Effective This Week
**Source:** Bus and Coach Buyer | https://www.busandcoachbuyer.com/category/news/coach/

From 6 July 2026, the DVSA maximum charge for ATF (Authorised Testing Facility) lane MOT inspections for buses and coaches rises from £70 to £90. This is a 28.5% increase and takes effect this week. For operators running large fleets, the annual compliance cost increases materially. Smaller hire operators -- who may run 5-20 vehicles -- will absorb this directly into margins or pass it on via rate increases. Bus hire pricing is already under margin pressure from fuel, insurance, and driver costs; this adds another fixed-cost line. Expect some operators to consolidate testing schedules or seek ATF facilities with competitive pricing below the new cap.

### Ember Launches Electric Coach Hire: Transparent Pricing Model
**Source:** bus-news.com | https://bus-news.com/ember-launches-new-electric-coach-hire-service/

Ember -- already operating the UK's first all-electric intercity coach routes in Scotland -- has launched a dedicated coach hire service. The pricing model is unusually transparent for the sector: £300 base positioning fee, £60 per driving/boarding hour, £20 per waiting hour. Example prices: approximately £510 for a school sports trip, £780 for a distillery tour, £2,460 for a multi-day Highland journey. Fleet is fully electric, with 5G Wi-Fi, USB charging at every seat, and real-time tracking for customers. Ember plans to reach 100 electric coaches by the end of 2026.

The significance here is not just environmental: Ember is attacking the traditional coach hire experience on price transparency and digital booking. The sector has historically operated on opaque quote-based pricing; Ember's published rate card sets a consumer expectation benchmark that other operators will face pressure to match or explain why they won't.

### Market Consolidation: Three Acquisitions This Week
**Source:** Bus and Coach Buyer | https://www.busandcoachbuyer.com/category/news/coach/

Consolidation continued at pace in late June / early July:

- **ComfortDelGro acquires P&J Ellis** -- the London-based 26-coach executive hire firm, operating for over 42 years. ComfortDelGro is building a London executive hire footprint.
- **Lee's Coaches acquired by Alpro Capital** -- the acquisition announcement flagged more deals to follow, suggesting Alpro is building a coach group.
- **MM Band Services joins Pieter Smit Group** -- a luxury Van Hool sleeper bus operator merging with the European entertainment logistics firm.

Three acquisitions in a single week signals that private capital is treating UK coach hire as a consolidation opportunity. Smaller independent operators are being absorbed. This is the same pattern seen in pest control -- professionalised management layers, capital-backed rollups.

### Other Notable Developments
- **Carousel Buses launches airline flyer ticket**: High Wycombe operator simplified airport access products, signalling demand for airport coach connections (tourism demand signal).
- **New Wales north-south coach service**: Transport for Wales funded a new coach service linking north and south Wales through supplementary budget allocations.
