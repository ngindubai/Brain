### New World Screwworm: $105M Federal Response Signals Imminent Threat

The dominant story in US pest control this week is not a treatment or a tech product -- it is a border emergency. The USDA has confirmed New World screwworm (NWS) presence in Texas and has deployed $105 million across 40 projects under its Grand Challenge programme, announced 16 June 2026. Texas A&M AgriLife received a portion of those funds to evaluate electron beam technology as a non-radioactive alternative to cobalt-60 for sterilising NWS pupae.

New World screwworm (*Cochliomyia hominivorax*) is a flesh-eating fly that was eradicated from the US in 1966 and has been held at bay in Central America through a sterile insect technique (SIT) barrier. The USDA confirmed its presence back in the US this year. The larvae burrow into living tissue of warm-blooded animals -- livestock, pets, and in rare cases humans -- making it both an animal health catastrophe and a pest management matter. The Grand Challenge's four priorities are: enhanced sterile fly production; novel traps and lures; therapeutics and treatments; and preparedness tools including repellents and ecological modelling.

For the US pest control industry, this represents an emerging service line. PMPs in Florida, Texas, and the south-western states will increasingly face client enquiries about screwworm risk, and the USDA's $105M signal means surveillance infrastructure will expand rapidly. Texas A&M's electron-beam sterilisation research is particularly significant: if cobalt-60 reliance can be reduced, SIT production can scale faster, which is the only proven long-term eradication tool.

The USDA received 226 applications requesting $664M before selecting the 40 funded projects -- demand for expertise in this area is clearly outpacing supply. This is a multi-year story.

### IATA 52nd Edition Live Animals Regulations: In Force Since 1 January 2026

IPATA's own news page has not been updated since November 2025, so there are no new IPATA announcements this week. The dominant regulatory story for the sector has already landed: IATA's 52nd edition Live Animals Regulations (LAR) came into force on 1 January 2026 and the industry is now six months into compliance.

Key changes in the 52nd edition that remain live issues for pet shippers:

**Brachycephalic breed standards.** The 2026 LAR establishes formal dimension and handling requirements for brachycephalic (snub-nosed) breeds for the first time. Previously, airlines applied their own breed lists and restrictions with no consistent container standard. The new rules set minimum crate size parameters specific to these breeds, meaning IPATA members must now verify crate dimensions against LAR specs rather than relying on general IATA container tables. This also affects acceptance documentation.

**CR1 container misuse crackdown.** CR1 containers are designed for wild and exotic species. The 52nd edition adds visual guides and species-specific language to stop CR1 containers being used for domestic pets by uninformed shippers. IPATA members in markets with less-experienced handlers should expect more ground rejection of improperly labelled containers.

**Stocking density and ventilation.** New detailed requirements for density and ventilation, primarily targeting day-old chick shipments, but the framework shift signals tighter oversight of all live cargo.

**Digital compliance.** IATA is developing digital processes and e-air waybill integration for live animal shipments, which are currently still paper-heavy. This is a 2026-2027 workstream rather than a current requirement, but IPATA members should monitor: early-adopter airlines may begin requiring digital documentation sooner than the general industry timeline.

### Summer Embargoes: Seasonal Demand Signal

With temperatures across the US, Gulf, and much of Europe running high in late June and through July/August, airline temperature embargo seasons are now active. The widely-cited safety band is 45°F to 85°F (7°C to 29°C) for cargo/checked-baggage pet transport. Routes through hubs in Dubai, Doha, Houston, and Phoenix are at highest risk of embargo during this window.

For IPATA members and the pet-transport-facing SEO site, this is the highest-volume search period of the year: people booking summer relocations and holiday pet travel hit the research phase in June. The content gap is typically "can I fly my dog in summer" and "which airlines accept pets in summer 2026" -- questions that have route-specific answers that change weekly.

### In-Cabin Pet Travel: IATA Working Group

IATA, in collaboration with airlines and pet shippers, is developing a formal in-cabin pet travel journey map and associated guidance. This is in active development as of the IATA Annual General Meeting (Rio de Janeiro, June 2026). No publication date confirmed but it signals that in-cabin standards -- currently highly fragmented across carriers -- will eventually align. For IPATA members, this is a medium-term planning signal.

## 3. Funeral Repatriation

### No NAFD News This Week

The NAFD news page shows no content published in June 2026. The most recent entries date to December 2024. This is unusual and may reflect a site maintenance issue or a deliberate content pause ahead of the Ministry of Justice announcement. The underlying regulatory situation is however moving.

### Regulatory Endgame: MoJ Report Expected Summer 2026

The background situation that has been building for two years is approaching a decision point. The Fuller Inquiry examined care standards in the funeral sector following a high-profile scandal. Its recommendations are being worked through a Ministry of Justice programme board involving the Department for Business and Trade and MHCLG.

As of December 2025, the status of the 75 Fuller Inquiry recommendations was: 11 accepted in full, 43 accepted in principle, 21 under consideration. The eleven phase-2 recommendations relating to statutory regulation remain under consideration -- that is the unresolved core.

The Government has stated that "some form of regulation of the funeral sector is required" but has not confirmed the mechanism. The MoJ final regulatory report was expected in summer 2026. Given that Parliament is in recess from late July, if the report does not land in July, it slips to autumn. This is a watch-and-wait situation.

### Unified Industry Code Now Live: March 2026

What has happened concretely: NAFD and SAIF (the Society of Allied and Independent Funeral Directors) adopted a unified core code in March 2026, modelled on Scotland's statutory code. This means the two main trade bodies -- between them representing the majority of funeral businesses in England and Wales -- now operate under the same baseline standards framework. The government has noted this favourably as evidence the sector can self-regulate, but statutory oversight of the non-member tail is the outstanding issue.

For the funeral-repatriation SEO site, mandatory trade association membership has been raised in Parliament as one possible mechanism for statutory oversight. If that passes, every funeral director handling repatriation would need to be a member of NAFD or SAIF, creating a significant structural change in the addressable market.

### Repatriation Specifics

No changes to repatriation logistics requirements this week. The established requirements remain:
- Zinc-lined (hermetically sealed) coffin mandated by international airline regulations and most national laws
- Repatriation to the UK from Europe: typically £3,000-£7,000; from further: up to £12,000 or more
- Documentation sequence is the primary cause of delays, not logistics
- NAFD-accredited directors remain the recommended route for avoiding paperwork errors

## 4. Close Protection

### April 2026 Refresher Mandate: Now Live, Compliance Clock Running

The most significant near-term regulatory change for UK close protection operatives is already in force. From 1 April 2026, CP operatives must complete an SIA-approved refresher training qualification before renewing their licence. The refresher has been available since 1 October 2025.

The four mandatory areas:
1. Physical intervention
2. Conducting searches
3. Protecting people in vulnerable situations
4. Terror threat awareness

Operatives must also present a current first aid qualification alongside the refresher. The rationale given by the SIA is "skills fade" in safety-critical competencies identified through subject-matter expert consultation.

The practical compliance situation: any CP operative whose licence renewal falls due from 1 April 2026 onward needs the refresher in hand before applying. Licences are typically three years, so the population of operatives hitting renewal now is those first licensed or last renewed in early 2023. The SIA's six-month lead time (October 2025 to April 2026) mirrors the pattern used for door supervision and security guarding refreshers.

This also applies to operatives holding CP licences who work primarily as door supervisors or security guards -- the licence integration provisions pull them in.

### Summer 2026: SIA Qualification Review Final Specs Incoming

The SIA launched a major strategic review of licence-linked qualifications in November 2025. The review covers:
- Core content for physical intervention, searching, and counter-terrorism
- Whether there is "the right amount of content in key specialist areas such as close protection" -- the SIA's own language, meaning CP qualification requirements may increase
- Emerging issues: social media auditors, new technologies
- English language assessment methods
- Training provider quality standards

Timeline:
- Engagement and consultation: November 2025 -- spring 2026
- **Final specification publication: summer 2026** -- imminent
- Industry preparation period: autumn 2026 -- spring 2027
- **New qualifications mandatory: spring 2027**

The final specs are expected any time now. For the close protection hire site, this is a double content signal: the immediate refresher compliance story (actionable now) and the medium-term qualification change story (spring 2027 deadline).

### Threat Landscape Context

The terror threat awareness component of the refresher reflects the SIA's ongoing integration of CT considerations into frontline security training. The inclusion of "protecting people in vulnerable situations" is notable -- it broadens the CP skill profile beyond the traditional principal-protection focus toward public duty scenarios, which is consistent with the growing use of CP-trained operatives in event security and dignitary escort roles beyond private client work.

## 5. Coach and Bus Hire
**Source:** Coach & Bus Week (cbwmagazine.com), CPT (cpt-uk.org), EVM UK
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[global-bus-hire]]

### Summer VAT Cut: Demand Stimulus, Not Direct Revenue Change

Chancellor Reeves announced the "Great British Summer Savings" scheme on 25 June 2026: VAT on qualifying tourism, leisure, and hospitality services cut from 20% to 5% for a ten-week window from 25 June to 1 September 2026. Coach and bus passenger transport is already zero-rated, so the cut does not alter ticket economics directly.

The indirect effect is the point. Lower cost at attractions (theme parks, zoos, museums, children's cinema and theatre, children's meals) reduces total day-out cost, stimulating group travel demand. IPSOS and CPT research cited by trade press indicates that cost is the primary barrier to group excursion bookings. Operators with leisure hire, school trip, and day excursion programmes should expect a demand uptick through August.

TOMS (Tour Operators' Margin Scheme) note: operators packaging coach hire with meals or admissions should review their TOMS calculations with a VAT adviser during the June-August window, as the 5% rate on included components changes the margin calculation.

### Free Child Bus Travel: August Demand Spike Confirmed

Children aged 5-15 travel free on local bus services in England throughout August 2026. This is confirmed government policy. For operators on contracted local routes, the funding mechanism sits with the local transport authority -- operators who have not already contacted their LTA should do so immediately to confirm reimbursement arrangements.

The secondary effect for private hire: free local bus travel during August will drive up family leisure mobility generally, reinforcing the group-trip demand signal above.

### Government Coach Planning Mandate: April 2026

From 2 April 2026, every local authority in England is required to recognise and plan for coach services in their transport strategies. The specific obligations:
- Assess current and potential coach demand in their area
- Coordinate with highway authorities on coach-related infrastructure
- Establish adequate parking, pick-up, and drop-off facilities
- Allow coaches access to bus lanes (except where this slows local buses)
- Explore funding to stimulate new or expanded scheduled coach services

The policy cites research valuing a single 49-seat coach visiting daily at approximately £1M annually to local economies. For the bus hire SEO site, this is significant: councils are now mandated to facilitate coach access, which removes a historic operational friction point for operators running private hire into town centres and visitor attractions.

The broader "Better Connected" transport strategy integrating tap-and-go payment and Google Maps tracking for coach services signals a digitisation direction that will affect how passengers discover and book private hire.

### Driver Shortage: 12.4% Coach Gap at Peak Demand

The Confederation of Passenger Transport (CPT) has formally called on Government to act on driver shortages ahead of the summer peak. Current numbers: 3.4% shortage for bus drivers; 12.4% for coach drivers. That is more than 4,000 unfilled positions nationally.

CPT is pushing two specific reforms:
1. **Remove the 50km route restriction for 18-19-year-old drivers.** Currently, drivers under 20 cannot operate regular services over 50km, excluding them from the majority of coach driving work.
2. **Allow training before provisional licence issuance.** New recruits currently wait weeks to months for provisional licences before any training can begin. CPT wants the theory and off-road CPC modules to start immediately on hiring, preventing candidate loss to other sectors during the wait.

For bus hire operators, the 12.4% coach driver shortage at the start of the peak summer season is a pricing signal. Supply is constrained; demand is being stimulated by the VAT cut and free child travel. Operators who have fleet and drivers will be able to command premium rates through August. Operators who cannot staff their fleet will lose bookings they cannot fulfil.

### London Bus Strikes: East London, June 11-14

A four-day strike by Unite-represented drivers at East London Bus & Coach Company (Stagecoach) ran 11-14 June 2026, with approximately 300 drivers walking out from the Bow garage. The dispute centred on driver fatigue from long-distance shifts without adequate breaks. Routes 8, 25, 205, 425, N8, and N25 were partially or fully affected. The strikes followed walk-outs in March, April, and May -- this is an ongoing industrial dispute, not a one-off.

Minister for Roads and Buses Simon Lightwood MP attended a meeting with coach industry leaders at London's Victoria Coach on 15 June, the day after the strikes ended. Coach Day / Better Transport Week (17 June) followed shortly after.
