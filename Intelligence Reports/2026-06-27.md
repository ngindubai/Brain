## Story 2: ARD — the new open standard for AI agent discovery, signed by Google, Microsoft, Nvidia and others
**Source:** MarketingProfs AI Update | https://www.marketingprofs.com/opinions/2026/55130/ai-update-june-26-2026-ai-news-and-views-from-the-past-week
**Related vault notes:** [[Agent Org]], [[Agentic AI Keyword Forecast]], [[Agent Skills Ecosystem]]

A consortium of major technology companies released the Agentic Resource Discovery (ARD) specification this week. Signatories include Google, Microsoft, GitHub, Hugging Face, Nvidia, Salesforce, and Snowflake. ARD is an open specification that enables AI agents to locate, verify, and connect with tools, APIs, MCP servers, and other agents at runtime.

The mechanism works as follows: organisations publish machine-readable catalogs on their own domains describing their available capabilities, tools, and agent endpoints. Registries index these catalogs. AI agents can then query registries at runtime to discover what capabilities are available and connect to them dynamically, without requiring preconfigured integrations. The spec includes publisher verification mechanisms and standardised metadata for establishing trusted connections.

The significance of ARD is that it provides the missing directory layer for multi-agent systems. The current state of agentic AI requires developers to manually configure which tools and agents their systems can access, which limits scalability and creates integration bottlenecks. ARD makes this discovery automatic: an orchestrator agent could, in principle, query an ARD registry, find the right specialist agent or tool for a subtask, verify it, and invoke it, all at runtime without human configuration.

For the keyword and content angle: ARD lands directly in [[Agentic AI Keyword Forecast]] Cluster A (agent plumbing and protocols), alongside MCP and A2A. It is the newest and lowest-competition term in that cluster. "Agentic resource discovery", "ARD specification", and "AI agent discovery protocol" are all near-zero competition right now. This is exactly the window the keyword forecast describes: a term that is technically significant, backed by major vendors, and not yet claimed by anyone in the SERP.

For Orwell's [[Agent Org]] architecture: ARD is the protocol layer that would enable the humanless company's agents to discover and invoke each other's capabilities dynamically. When the George/JARVIS voice agent needs to hand off to a data-retrieval agent or a CRM-update agent, ARD is the discovery mechanism that makes that handoff automatic rather than hardcoded. MCP handles the individual tool connections; ARD handles the directory that lets agents find what to connect to. Together they are foundational infrastructure for the agentic vision.

## Story 3: Gemini 2.5 Pro with Deep Think — benchmark reality check and where it matters
**Source:** Medium/AI-Insights | https://medium.com/adi-insights-innovations-collective/ai-update-monday-june-22-2026-ad347993f9ad | Google DeepMind | https://deepmind.google/models/gemini/
**Related vault notes:** [[Agent Org]], [[Products]]

Gemini 2.5 Pro with Deep Think launched on 22 June. The benchmark picture is now clear enough to characterise without hype. Google leads on science and maths: GPQA Diamond 82.4% (vs Fable 5's 79.1% and GPT-5.5's 76.3%), MMLU-Pro 89.8% (highest of any public model), and HumanEval+ 94.1% (highest ever recorded on coding syntax). However it trails Fable 5 on the benchmarks that matter most for agentic work: SWE-bench Verified 76.4% vs Fable 5's 88.6%, and on the Artificial Analysis GDPval-AA agentic work evaluation, Gemini models underperform their general intelligence index scores.

The 2 million token context window is a genuine differentiator. No other frontier model offers this at production cost. For tasks requiring analysis of an entire codebase, a year of meeting transcripts, or a complete regulatory document set in a single prompt, Gemini 2.5 Pro is the only option. Pricing is approximately $2.50 input / $15 output per million tokens standard, with Deep Think at approximately 4x the standard rate — cheaper than Claude Opus 4.8 or GPT-5.5 at high volume.

The practical decision framework: use Gemini 2.5 Pro for high-volume workloads, long-document analysis, video understanding, and science or maths tasks; use Claude Opus 4.8 for software engineering quality, agentic coding, and regulated-domain work where hallucination reduction is critical; use GPT-5.5 for autonomous computer-use agentic tasks (Terminal-Bench lead). No single model wins every category.

Note on the Gemini family: the search results confirm Google has since shipped Gemini 3 (1501 LMArena Elo, GPQA Diamond 94.3% on Gemini 3.1 Pro) and Gemini 3.5 Flash (agentic multimodal). The 2.5 Pro Deep Think launch on 22 June was a significant but not the latest Google release; the 3.x family is already rolling out. Google's model cadence is accelerating.

For Orwell: the current Claude Opus 4.8 and Sonnet 4.6 stack is the right choice for the coding and agentic work that drives Orwell's builds. Gemini 2.5 Pro is worth testing for the [[Comparison Network]] content pipeline where large-document ingestion (mortgage documents, financial data) is a key use case and cost efficiency at volume matters. The 2M context window could be relevant for the 172-article knowledge-hub corpus on MortgageCompare.ae.

## Story 4: OpenAI advertising goes live — $100M ARR in under six weeks, 900M users
**Source:** OpenAI Blog | https://openai.com/index/our-approach-to-advertising-and-expanding-access/ | OpenAI | https://openai.com/index/accelerating-the-next-phase-ai/
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Comparison Network]], [[Agentic AI Keyword Forecast]]

OpenAI confirmed this week that its ad pilot has reached over $100 million in ARR in under six weeks since launch. The ad platform sits below answers in ChatGPT for the free and Go tiers, triggered by commercially-intent queries. OpenAI reports that approximately one-fifth of all ChatGPT queries already express direct commercial intent. ChatGPT now serves 900 million weekly active users with $25 billion in annualised revenue and 50 million paying subscribers. Enterprise accounts for over 40% of revenue and is on track to reach parity with consumer by end of 2026.

The advertising model has three stated principles: answer independence (ads do not influence answers), conversation privacy (conversations are not sold to advertisers), and user control (personalisation can be disabled). Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise tiers have no ads. The format is sponsored placements at the bottom of answers, separate from and clearly labelled distinct from the answer itself.

The implications for search and SEO are significant. OpenAI is building what is effectively a conversational commerce platform with advertising economics layered onto it. For the [[SEO Portfolio]] and [[Comparison Network]], this is both a threat and an opportunity. Threat: if users resolve commercial queries in ChatGPT rather than clicking through to comparison sites, traffic to MortgageCompare.ae and similar properties could erode. Opportunity: the one-fifth commercial-intent figure confirms that agentic and AI-first interfaces are now a major commercial channel, and businesses that optimise for LLM visibility (GEO) alongside traditional SEO will have an advantage as this shift plays out.

The arXiv paper noted in yesterday's report (Wikipedia editing shaping LLM outputs) connects here: if ChatGPT's answers in commercial verticals are influenced by what it has been trained on, and if LLM-visible authoritative content influences those answers, then investing in being the authoritative source that LLMs cite is the right GEO play. The [[SEO Portfolio]] keyword strategy should have a GEO dimension.

There is also a market signal embedded in OpenAI's ads announcement worth noting. $100M ARR in six weeks from advertising on a product that was previously ad-free means CPMs or equivalent metrics are very high. Commercial-intent AI queries are monetising at rates comparable to search advertising. That confirms the value of the channel for any brand that can appear there.

## Story 5: Apple embeds quiet AI throughout iOS 27 — the invisible-AI model
**Source:** MarketingProfs AI Update | https://www.marketingprofs.com/opinions/2026/55130/ai-update-june-26-2026-ai-news-and-views-from-the-past-week
**Related vault notes:** [[Agent Org]], [[Products]]

Apple's iOS 27, announced at Tim Cook's final WWDC (8 June, now shipping), takes a deliberately different approach to AI than the conversational-assistant model of ChatGPT and Claude. Instead of a chat interface, Apple is embedding AI throughout the OS as quiet background capability: automatic restaurant bill splitting, password replacement after security breaches, intelligent message suggestions, calendar creation from natural language, smarter Shortcuts automation, contextual information during customer service calls, grouped smart home notifications, and AI-organised Safari tabs.

The design philosophy is significant: AI as infrastructure rather than AI as product. The user does not see a model name or an AI interface. They see their apps working better. This is the consumer normalisation path for agentic AI: the assistant disappears, the outcome remains. It is the same design logic that the best enterprise agent deployments will eventually follow — the agent becomes invisible, the workflow improves.

The Siri rebuild runs on a custom 1.2 trillion parameter Gemini model licensed from Google at approximately $1 billion per year. Apple also launched an Extensions system that lets users choose between ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude as the AI model powering Apple Intelligence features, with Gemini as the default. This ends OpenAI's previous ChatGPT exclusivity inside the iPhone.

For Orwell: the invisible-AI design pattern is directly relevant to the humanless company thesis. Clients do not want to manage AI agents; they want their business to run. The George/JARVIS positioning and the KlientFlo build should learn from Apple's framing: the AI does not need to be named or visible, it needs to produce the outcome. The Extensions model also confirms that multi-model access via Apple's system-level integration will expose Claude to hundreds of millions of iPhone users who may not know they're using it.

## Story 6: AI agents shift from chat to delegation — Codex at 17% of active ChatGPT users
**Source:** MarketingProfs AI Update | https://www.marketingprofs.com/opinions/2026/55130/ai-update-june-26-2026-ai-news-and-views-from-the-past-week
**Related vault notes:** [[Agent Org]], [[Agentic AI Keyword Forecast]]

OpenAI researchers reported this week that organisational use of Codex (their agentic work platform) has risen from virtually zero in mid-2025 to approximately 17% of active ChatGPT and Codex users by mid-2026. Non-developers are the fastest-growing segment. Users are increasingly assigning tasks estimated to save hours of human effort, including coding, file management, scheduling, web browsing, and administrative work. The tasks being delegated are multi-step, hours-long work, not single questions.

This is the clearest data point yet that the agentic AI transition is underway at consumer scale. The people using ChatGPT for agentic delegation are not technical users building pipelines; they are non-developers who have discovered that they can hand a task to an AI and come back later. That is exactly the behaviour pattern the Orwell humanless company model is built for — except aimed at business processes rather than individual productivity tasks.

The 17% figure matters for the [[Agentic AI Keyword Forecast]]. Search terms around "AI agents for business", "delegate tasks to AI", and "AI automation for [function]" should be rising on the back of this adoption curve. Non-developer buyer intent is the exact profile of the Orwell Lab commercial target.

1. **ASR hallucinations and rare entity errors** (arXiv:2606.24889, cs.CL) — Automatic speech recognition systems hallucinate rare entities and domain-specific terms even at low overall error rates. Directly relevant to voice agent builds (George/JARVIS) under [[Products]]. Any voice-based agentic system needs a dedicated rare-entity correction layer.
