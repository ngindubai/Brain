## Story 1: Critchlow's warning — SEO teams may not own the outcomes GEO is judged on

**Source:** Search Engine Journal, "AI Search Is Exposing SEO's Risk Of Losing Ownership Of GEO Outcomes" (9 July) | https://www.searchenginejournal.com/ai-search-is-exposing-seos-risk-of-losing-geo-outcomes/581805/
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Comparison Network]], [[Agent Org]]

Tom Critchlow's argument, reported by Roger Montti, is blunt: GEO (generative engine optimization — how brands show up inside AI answers) behaves much more like brand marketing than it behaves like SEO, and the people who actually move the needle on GEO outcomes — brand builders, product teams, PR, editorial — are, in most organisations, not SEO professionals. His line: "The people that drive SEO outcomes are not SEO professionals, by and large." He pushes further by asking whether an SEO team does any of the things that actually drive AI-search visibility — brand reputation, product quality, press coverage, distinctive editorial — and answers that in most organisations it doesn't.

The mechanism is that AI engines like ChatGPT and AI Overviews draw heavily on aggregate brand signal — how often and how favourably a brand is discussed across the web, not just how well a specific page is optimised or how many links point at it. That's a slower-moving, harder-to-engineer signal than classic technical or on-page SEO, and it sits organisationally outside where SEO teams typically operate. Critchlow's warning is that this creates a structural risk: when GEO underperforms, leadership may reassign responsibility for it to brand or product teams rather than fund the SEO function to fix it, because the SEO function was never the lever that controlled it.

This is worth taking seriously for Orwell specifically, not just as industry commentary. The whole thesis of [[Agent Org]] is an agent in every department role — which means the brand, PR, product and editorial functions Critchlow says actually drive GEO outcomes are exactly the roles Orwell is trying to staff with agents rather than treating SEO as a single silo. That's arguably an advantage: a humanless company built with departmental agents from the start doesn't have the same "SEO team in a box, disconnected from brand" structural problem that a traditional agency or in-house team has. But it does mean AI-visibility performance for the SEO Portfolio and Comparison Network sites shouldn't be graded purely against SEO-team inputs (content volume, backlinks, technical fixes) — it needs a brand/PR/product lens layered on, or the grading will keep missing why a technically sound site is invisible in AI answers.

The piece doesn't offer a fix beyond "expand what SEO means" — which is thin as practical guidance, but the diagnosis is sharper than most of the GEO commentary running through this report over the past fortnight, and it reframes several open threads (the used-vs-cited framework, the self-promotional-content-risk flag) as symptoms of the same root cause: AI visibility is downstream of brand and product signal that most SEO workflows don't touch.

## Story 2: OpenAI launches GPT-Live — ChatGPT gets full-duplex voice with live web search, citation handling undisclosed

**Source:** Search Engine Journal, "OpenAI GPT-Live Brings Search Into ChatGPT Voice" (9 July) | https://searchengineland.com/openai-gpt-live-brings-search-into-chatgpt-voice/481843
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Comparison Network]]

OpenAI has begun rolling out GPT-Live, a new voice-model generation for ChatGPT Voice, globally across tiers (GPT-Live-1 for Go/Plus/Pro, GPT-Live-1 mini for Free users). It's full-duplex — simultaneous audio in and out, with better turn-taking than prior voice modes — and mid-conversation it can hand a question off to a frontier model (GPT-5.5 at launch) to run a live web search and bring the answer back into the spoken conversation, along with visual cards for weather, stocks and sports. OpenAI says over 150 million people already use ChatGPT's voice features weekly, so this isn't a niche surface.

The detail that matters for search visibility is the one OpenAI hasn't disclosed: whether a spoken GPT-Live answer names its sources, shows them on a card, or drops them entirely. That's the difference between a voice search inside ChatGPT sending a listener to a site and a voice search quietly consuming a site's content with zero referral trail — the audio equivalent of the "used vs cited" gap this report flagged on 7-8 July, except with no visible link at all if the citation is omitted, since there's no SERP to inspect.

For the portfolio, this is a watch item rather than an action item today. Voice-native AI search at this scale (150 million weekly voice users, now with live web search baked in) is a new consumption surface for exactly the kind of transactional, local-intent queries the SEO Portfolio and Comparison Network sites are built to catch — "who does pet transport to the UAE," "compare UAE mortgage rates" — and if GPT-Live turns out to cite sparingly in spoken answers, that's another referral channel quietly closing without any dashboard currently built to detect it.

## Story 3: Google claims all-time Search usage record during World Cup — usage record, not a traffic record for publishers

**Source:** Search Engine Journal, "Google Says Search Hit All-Time Usage High During World Cup" (9 July) | https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-says-search-hit-all-time-usage-high-during-world-cup/581796/
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]]

Google's Nick Fox (SVP, Knowledge & Information) posted that Google Search "broke all prior usage records and saw its highest usage in history" in the moments after Argentina's stoppage-time winner against Egypt in the World Cup Round of 16. No figures, no methodology, no definition of "usage" were shared — Google made an near-identical claim after the 2022 World Cup final with the same lack of data.

Matt G. Southern's SEJ writeup makes the useful distinction: a record in Google-side query volume says nothing about clicks reaching publisher sites, and with bot traffic estimated at over 53% of all web traffic in 2025, even the query-volume figure itself is hard to take at face value without Google explaining how it filters automated requests. The story is a reminder, not a data point — Google is very willing to publicize usage superlatives that make Search look indispensable at the exact moment independent data keeps showing AI Overviews and AI-native competitors eating into click-through on informational queries. Nothing here changes portfolio strategy; it's useful context for why "search usage is at an all-time high" and "publisher traffic from search is declining" are both true simultaneously and not in tension.

## Story 5: Google Ads tightens the Smart Bidding conversation — naming clarified now, a real behaviour change lands 17 August

**Source:** Search Engine Journal / Search Engine Roundtable coverage of Google's Smart Bidding update (9 July), cross-referenced with Google Ads Developer Blog | https://www.searchenginejournal.com/category/news/
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

Two Google Ads items landed today. First, a naming-only change: "Maximize conversions with a Target CPA" and "Maximize conversion value with a Target ROAS" are being relabelled "Target CPA" and "Target ROAS" — cosmetic, no account action needed. Second, and more consequential: from 17 August 2026, budget-limited campaigns running Target CPA or Target ROAS will start delivering results closer to the actual figures advertisers type in, closing a long-standing gap where campaigns routinely blew past targets (a £20 CPA target quietly delivering £12; a 500% ROAS target hitting 700%+). Google is giving a six-week runway with a Bid Target Adjustment Tool now live in-account (since 6 July) to help advertisers reset expectations before enforcement.

Separately, Google Ads expanded its Search Campaigns for Travel beta to cover "Things to Do" and "Events," widening eligibility beyond core travel/hotel advertisers. Neither story is core-SEO, but any Comparison Network or CRM Systems paid-media spend running Target CPA/ROAS on Google Ads should flag 17 August as a date to re-baseline budgets before performance shifts — campaigns currently over-delivering against target will likely tighten, which changes volume assumptions for any lead-gen forecasting built on current Smart Bidding behaviour.
