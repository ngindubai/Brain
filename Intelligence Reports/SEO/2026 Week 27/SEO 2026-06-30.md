## Story 1: Google June 2026 Spam Update — Complete, Confirmed, Second of the Year

**Source:** Search Engine Land | https://searchengineland.com/google-june-2026-spam-update-done-rolling-out-481063 | Search Engine Journal | https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-begins-rolling-out-the-june-2026-spam-update/580424/
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Agent Org]]

Google's June 2026 spam update started June 24 at approximately noon EDT and completed on June 26 at around 2pm ET. Rollout period: two days. This is the second spam update of 2026 (the first ran earlier in the year). Spam updates target Google's automated spam detection systems, specifically SpamBrain — its AI-based spam prevention system — rather than the ranking signals that core updates adjust.

Key distinction for the [[SEO Portfolio]]: spam updates penalise rule violations (cloaking, link spam, auto-generated garbage content, scaled content abuse), not quality signals. A site hit by a spam update has a different recovery path than a core update victim. For recovery: review Google's spam policies, fix the violations, wait. Google warns that reassessment can take months even after corrections.

The update was described by Search Engine Roundtable sources as "starting earlier than it was announced and feeling more widespread than a typical Google spam update." This suggests SpamBrain's sensitivity thresholds were adjusted, not just a clean-up of known offenders. Sites using AI content generation at scale without human review are most exposed.

**Portfolio exposure assessment:**
- **Pet transport / funeral repatriation / close protection:** Programmatic SEO with thin templates is the classic SpamBrain target if content does not demonstrate genuine E-E-A-T differentiation. Review any pages that are near-identical across entity variants.
- **Global Bus Hire / Pest Control USA:** Same concern. Volume-generated content needs locality signals, expertise signals, and genuine utility to survive spam detection. If pages exist that differ only by swapping a location name, those are flagged.
- **Comparison network (MortgageCompare, MoneyCompare, InsureCompare):** Financial product comparison is YMYL — Your Money/Your Life — so standards are already elevated. Main risk is thin financial product pages without licensed entity backing or expert review signals.

No confirmed reports of spam update traffic losses to portfolio-adjacent sites in this cycle. The update's two-day rollout and apparent multi-language scope makes it broader than average. Monitor Search Console for any manual action notifications through July 3.

**Source:** Digital Applied | https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/google-june-2026-ranking-update-volatility-seo-analysis | Semrush Sensor web search
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]]

Separately from the confirmed spam update, community reports surfaced around June 19 of a ranking movement targeting black-hat tactics. Semrush Sensor and Mozcast registered largely calm readings because the volatility was concentrated in EU traffic segments and specific e-commerce verticals not captured in their US-weighted keyword samples.

This is a recurring measurement problem: SERP volatility tools measure a fixed sample of high-volume US-centric keywords. Volatility that is geographically concentrated, vertically concentrated, or confined to AI Overview surfaces rather than blue-link positions stays invisible to aggregate sensors by design. The SEO community has increasingly split between "the sensor says calm, therefore nothing happened" and "my traffic says otherwise."

For [[SEO Portfolio]] sites with EU geographic exposure (particularly funeral repatriation and close protection — both with UK/EU audiences), this unconfirmed movement is worth monitoring in GSC data for week-on-week changes in impressions and clicks from European traffic. A sensor reading of 4/10 nationally can mask a 9/10 event in a single country or vertical.

**Action:** Pull GSC data for UK/EU country segments for pet transport, funeral repatriation, and close protection sites for the week of June 16-23. Look for impression drops rather than ranking drops — AI Overviews often collapse impressions before ranks move.

## Story 3: Google's Official GEO Guide — The New Optimisation Standard

**Source:** Google Search Central | https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/ai-optimization-guide | Digital Applied | https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/google-official-seo-docs-generative-ai-optimization-june-2026
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Agent Org]]

On June 5, 2026, Google published its official guidance on optimising for generative AI features in Google Search. The core message: "Optimising for generative AI search is optimising for the search experience, and thus still SEO." Google explicitly says AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation) and GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) are not separate disciplines — they extend from the same fundamentals.

Critically, Google called out specific GEO tactics as unnecessary or misleading:
- **llms.txt files:** Not required, not read by Google's generative AI features
- **Content chunking** specifically for AI: Not needed
- **AI-specific schema markup:** Not a factor
- **Content rewriting for AI consumption:** Not a ranking signal

This is a significant market correction. An entire cottage industry of "GEO specialists" has been selling optimisation packages targeting these exact tactics. Google is explicitly calling them out as ineffective. Any SEO vendor pitching a specific guaranteed GEO improvement number is, per Google's own documentation, making a structurally impossible claim.

What does matter for AI Overview citation: traditional E-E-A-T signals (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness), clear factual statements that AI can extract and cite, structured data that helps Google understand page content, and being a recognised entity in the knowledge graph.

The Ahrefs study (June 15) adds empirical weight: 97% of llms.txt files across 137,000 domains receive virtually no bot traffic. The file format is functionally dead as a Google optimisation signal.

**Orwell SEO implication:** The [[SEO Portfolio]] sites should not be wasting resource on llms.txt, AI-specific schema, or content chunking. Focus is: genuine topical authority, entity recognition, clear Q&A content that AI can extract, and authoritative source signals (citations from legitimate industry sources, trade bodies, government pages).

## Story 4: AI Overviews — The CTR Crisis and the Citation Opportunity

**Source:** Ahrefs | https://ahrefs.com/blog/seo-trends/ | EnFuse Solutions | https://www.enfuse-solutions.com/how-googles-ai-overviews-are-changing-seo-in-2026/ | Marie Haynes | https://www.mariehaynes.com/new-aio-and-aimode-info-in-gsc/
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Comparison Network]]

The CTR data on AI Overviews is now consistent across multiple research studies (Semrush, Ahrefs, independent researchers): queries triggering AI Overviews produce a 30-50% reduction in organic CTR for the top three blue-link positions compared to equivalent queries without AIO. This is not theoretical — it is measured traffic loss from the same rankings.

However, the counterfactual matters. The alternative is not always "traffic that would have come anyway." AI Overviews often appear on navigational and informational queries where zero-click was already the outcome. The net-new loss is concentrated on mid-funnel informational queries where users would previously have clicked through to read the answer.

For [[SEO Portfolio]] sites, the highest-risk query types are informational ("how much does pet transport cost," "what happens when someone dies abroad") — exactly the query types that programmatic SEO often targets. These are now AI Overview territory. The strategic response is to make the [[SEO Portfolio]] sites the citation sources within AIOs, not the click-through destinations for queries they answer.

Being cited within an AI Overview drives:
- Higher brand trust perception
- Increased branded search volume (users who see your brand in AIO then search for it directly)
- Higher conversion rates from the traffic that does arrive (pre-qualified)

Marie Haynes reported that GSC now has AI Overview impression and click tracking — the measurement tooling is available. This allows direct attribution of AIO appearances to traffic.

Google is also testing top stories within the AI Overview section — reported rolling out to some searchers as of late June. This creates a news-flavoured AIO surface where freshly published, on-topic content can appear alongside the generative answer. For comparison network sites, publishing regular market data updates (mortgage rate changes, comparison benchmarks) creates AIO citation eligibility.

**Action:** Set up AIO tracking in GSC for all [[SEO Portfolio]] domains. Identify which query clusters are triggering AIOs for portfolio-relevant terms. Prioritise building entity and citation signals for those clusters.

## Story 5: Marie Haynes — Open Knowledge Format and Agent-Readiness

**Source:** Marie Haynes | https://www.mariehaynes.com/okf/ | https://www.mariehaynes.com/build-an-okf-brain-like-mine/ | https://www.mariehaynes.com/lighthouse-report-for-agents/
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Agent Org]]

Marie Haynes' June 2026 output is the most forward-looking SEO intelligence available this week. Her March 2026 piece on Google-Agent as "the biggest mindset shift in SEO history" set the frame; her June pieces build on it.

The Open Knowledge Format (OKF), introduced in mid-June by Google, is a standardised way for AI agents to access knowledge via simple directories of Markdown files. This is directly analogous to what Gareth has built in this Brain repo. Google's agents will crawl and read structured Markdown knowledge bases as a primary source layer. Sites that organise their knowledge in OKF-compatible structures become agent-readable by design.

Chrome's Lighthouse tool now includes an "agent readiness" report — evaluating whether a website's structure, speed, and content organisation makes it discoverable and usable by AI agents, not just human crawlers. The test suite checks for structured data completeness, page speed thresholds, clear heading hierarchies, and entity disambiguation.

Haynes' "Rise in Crawled — currently not indexed" post (May 2026) flagged a pattern: Google is crawling and evaluating more content than it indexes for traditional search, specifically to populate its agent and generative AI knowledge layer. Being crawled but not indexed is no longer purely a technical failure — it may mean your content is being used in AIO without appearing in blue-link results.

**Orwell implication:** The [[SEO Portfolio]] sites should run Lighthouse agent-readiness checks. Any site failing structural organisation tests is at risk of not being picked up by Google's agent crawlers. This is a new dimension of technical SEO that existing audit processes may not cover.
