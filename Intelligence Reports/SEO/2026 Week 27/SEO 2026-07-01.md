## Story 1: Google's June 2026 spam update is done — no drama, but AI Overview spam enforcement is the real story going forward
**Source:** Search Engine Roundtable / Search Engine Journal | https://www.seroundtable.com/google-june-2026-spam-update-out-41568.html
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Comparison Network]]

Confirms and closes yesterday's open loop. The update rolled out fast — started around noon ET on 24 June, fully complete by roughly 2pm ET on 26 June, a two-day rollout that felt more widespread than a typical spam action despite the short window. No confirmed hit on any portfolio property; this is now closed as a monitoring item, but keep GSC manual actions and coverage reports open through 3 July as a formality.

The more important context sits underneath the update itself: this spam policy round explicitly extended to cover "inauthentic mentions" and "scaled content abuse" inside AI Overviews and AI Mode, not just classic organic results. That is a direct line to the programmatic sites. Pet Transport, Funeral Repatriation, Close Protection Hire, Global Bus Hire and Pest Control USA all run on templated, scaled content patterns by design — that is the entire point of programmatic SEO. Google is now on record saying scaled content abuse inside AI surfaces is a policy target, not just classic SERP spam. None of the five sites were built to game rankings, but the templated structure is exactly the shape Google's classifiers are trained to scrutinise. Worth a manual spot-check: pull ten random programmatic pages per site and read them as a human, not as a template output, to confirm each one stands alone as useful.

For the comparison network the read is more comfortable — MortgageCompare, MoneyCompare, InsureCompare, CompanyFormation and LegalConsultants are editorially thin in places but not templated in the scaled-abuse sense, so lower exposure here.

## Story 2: Google Search Console ships Generative AI performance reports — UK-only, no click data yet, and already has a logged bug
**Source:** Search Engine Land / Google Search Central Blog | https://searchengineland.com/google-search-console-ai-performance-reports-and-controls-to-block-your-content-in-ai-responses-479298
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Comparison Network]], [[Agent Org]]

This is the direct answer to yesterday's open loop on AI Overview citation tracking, and it moves that thread from "open, no action taken" to "open, action available but incomplete." Google launched dedicated Search Console reports on 3 June showing impressions and pages that appeared in AI Overviews, AI Mode, and generative Discover features. The report breaks down by country, device and time, which is useful, but there are two real limits right now.

First, no click data and no query-level detail — you can see that a page appeared in an AI surface, not what query triggered it or whether anyone clicked through. That materially limits how actionable the report is for prioritising which pages to optimise for citation.

Second, and more relevant today: Google itself has now logged a data logging bug affecting the Discover and Generative AI in Discover performance reports. Anything pulled from that specific report right now should be treated as unreliable until Google confirms a fix.

Third, and this is the practical blocker for Gareth specifically: rollout is currently limited to a subset of UK-based site owners. Unless one of the portfolio properties happens to be in that test cohort, there is nothing to check yet. Action for this week: check GSC on each property (comparison network sites are more likely candidates given UK/GCC crossover in account settings) to see if the new report has appeared. If none have it, this loop stays open but downgrades to "watch for wider rollout" rather than "set up now."

## Story 3: Lighthouse quietly adds an llms.txt check to its agent-readiness audit
**Source:** Web search aggregation (SEO industry coverage, multiple sources) | https://nohacks.co/blog/okf-website-knowledge-graph
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Comparison Network]], [[Agent Org]]

Direct update to yesterday's new "Lighthouse agent-readiness audit" thread. Google's Chrome team has added an `llms.txt` presence check into Lighthouse's agent-readiness auditing. This is a concrete, testable signal, not a speculative GEO trend piece — Lighthouse is a tool Gareth's stack can run today, locally or in CI, against every portfolio and comparison-network domain.

None of the ten portfolio and comparison properties currently ship an `llms.txt` file, as far as this report can confirm without direct access to the sites. That is worth verifying directly rather than assumed. The action is low-cost: `llms.txt` is a simple plain-text file at domain root describing site purpose and key pages for LLM crawlers, similar in spirit to `robots.txt`. Given Orwell's whole thesis is agent-native operation, having the portfolio fail an agent-readiness audit that Google's own tooling now runs is a bad look internally even before it matters externally. Suggest folding this into the next portfolio maintenance pass: run Lighthouse against all ten domains, log which fail the agent-readiness check, ship `llms.txt` to all of them in one batch.

## Story 4: Open Knowledge Format (OKF) — status check, still v0.1, still not a ranking factor
**Source:** Web search aggregation (Search Engine Roundtable, seo-kreativ.de, StartupHub.ai) | https://www.seo-kreativ.de/en/blog/open-knowledge-format-okf/
**Related vault notes:** [[Agent Org]], [[SEO Portfolio]]

Direct update to yesterday's new OKF thread. No new developments since the 12 June v0.1 publication — Google is still explicit that this is "a starting point, not a finished standard" and not a search ranking mechanism. Structurally it is a directory of Markdown files with thin YAML frontmatter, one file per concept/metric/table/API/runbook, cross-linked with ordinary Markdown links, designed so multiple AI agents can read one canonical knowledge bundle instead of each re-parsing a wiki independently.

This is worth flagging for a reason that has nothing to do with SEO rankings and everything to do with Agent Org: the structure Google is describing for OKF — Markdown files, YAML frontmatter, wikilink-style cross-references — is functionally close to how this Brain vault itself is organised. There may be a genuine crossover opportunity to publish an OKF-compatible knowledge bundle for the portfolio brands (service coverage, pricing logic, FAQ content) that both AI agents and Orwell's own internal agent stack can consume. This is a Research note candidate, not an action item yet — no urgency, Google itself is still calling it pre-standard.

## Story 5: June 19 "black hat" ranking movement — clarified, not EU-concentrated, narrow targeting confirmed
**Source:** Search Engine Roundtable | https://www.seroundtable.com/google-search-ranking-hits-black-hats-41541.html
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]]

Correction and close on yesterday's open loop, which had this logged as "EU-concentrated, sensor-invisible." Fuller reporting shows the concentration was actually in black-hat SEO forum chatter (BlackHatWorld, similar communities), not a geography — the EU framing from yesterday appears to have been an early, partial read. Reported impact was steep where it landed: individual webmasters reporting 25-50% traffic drops starting late 19-20 June, informational sites hit hardest, commercial-intent queries more stable. All major third-party trackers — MozCast, Semrush Sensor, Sistrix, Algoroo, AccuRanker — showed normal readings throughout, and Google has not confirmed any update.

The self-selection caveat matters: people who get hit post about it, people who don't move stay silent, so the 25-50% figures describe the affected tail, not an average. Read on this for the portfolio: sites built on legitimate, non-manipulative programmatic patterns (which is the whole portfolio) sit outside the apparent target profile. Downgrade this thread to closed-monitoring rather than active — no further portfolio-specific action needed unless a specific site shows unexplained movement in GSC.
