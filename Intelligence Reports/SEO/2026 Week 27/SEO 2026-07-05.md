## Story 1: Fabrice Canel retires from Microsoft after nearly 30 years leading Bing indexing and IndexNow

**Source:** Search Engine Land, "Fabrice Canel retires from Microsoft Bing after legendary career" | https://searchengineland.com/fabrice-canel-retires-from-microsoft-bing-after-legendary-career-481397 — Search Engine Roundtable, "Fabrice Canel Retires From Microsoft After Almost 30 Years Of Service" (via web search) | https://www.seroundtable.com/fabrice-canel-retires-41602.html

Canel, Bing's Principal Program Manager for indexing — crawling, URL discovery, content selection and processing — retired from Microsoft effective 1 July 2026 via the company's Voluntary Retirement Program, closing out nearly 30 years there. He created IndexNow (the push-based indexing protocol Bing and other engines use to get URLs crawled faster than waiting on discovery) and helped build Bing Webmaster Tools into what it is today.

Why it's worth a line rather than a shrug: IndexNow is one of the few pieces of technical SEO infrastructure that meaningfully improved indexing speed across engines beyond Google, and it exists because one person pushed for it inside Microsoft for years. No indication yet of who inherits the indexing/crawling remit at Bing, which is the thing actually worth watching — a leadership gap in that team is the kind of thing that shows up months later as slower IndexNow support or stalled Bing Webmaster Tools development, not immediately.
