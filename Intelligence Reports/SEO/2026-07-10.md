## Story 1: Google Business Profile reviews bug — "You have no reviews yet" — with a partial fix already out
**Source:** Search Engine Roundtable (via search-indexed summary; original 403'd on direct fetch) | https://www.seroundtable.com/google-you-have-no-reviews-yet-41662.html

Business owners logging into Google Business Profile today are hitting a display bug: clicking through to read reviews shows "you have no reviews yet," even on listings with hundreds of reviews (one reported case: 916 reviews visible on the live listing, zero visible in the owner's review-reply dashboard). The bug affects the ability to reply to reviews, not just view them.

Google has responded on the same thread: a related, longer-running issue — reviews going missing after a fake-review dispute was filed — has now been resolved, with Google saying affected reviews should reappear within a day or two. That is a genuine update to the reviews-issue open loop tracked since early July: partial resolution on the dispute-hiding bug, but a fresh display fault (the "no reviews yet" message) has opened up alongside it. Net effect: the underlying open loop is not closed, it has mutated.

This matters wherever the portfolio depends on a Google Business Profile presence for trust signals or reply management — pest control USA and close protection hire both lean on local trust proof, and any GBP-dependent lead gen loses the ability to respond to reviews while the bug is live. Nothing to action beyond checking GBP dashboards before assuming a review went unanswered because of neglect rather than a Google-side fault. [[SEO Portfolio]]

## Story 2: Ahrefs adds a Microsoft Copilot index to Brand Radar, restructures AI-visibility pricing
**Source:** Ahrefs Changelog | https://ahrefs.com/blog/new-features/

Dated exactly today: Ahrefs' Brand Radar tool — which tracks brand mentions and citations across AI answer engines — added a Microsoft Copilot index (starting with a reported 12 million prompts from July) alongside its existing ChatGPT, Perplexity, AI Overviews and AI Mode coverage. Alongside the new index, Ahrefs restructured pricing: individual AI-platform indexes now run $199/month each, with a new "All Platforms" add-on bundling all five sources at $699/month. Existing subscribers keep current pricing for at least a year.

This is a small but telling data point on where the AI-visibility tooling market is heading: vendors are now treating per-platform AI citation tracking as a paid, metered product rather than a bundled feature — which raises the real cost of doing GEO measurement properly across ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini/AI Overviews, AI Mode and Perplexity simultaneously. For a solo operation running SEO and comparison-site portfolios across multiple niches, that's a budget line to weigh: full five-platform coverage is now roughly $700/month before considering seat costs, versus picking one or two platforms most relevant to a given vertical (Copilot's relevance to UAE finance-adjacent queries, e.g. mortgage/insurance comparison searches surfaced via Bing/Copilot, may justify a narrower buy). ## Story 3: Google Merchant Center Next quietly drops the "Next" — now just Merchant Center
**Source:** Search Engine Roundtable (via search-indexed summary) | https://www.seroundtable.com/google-merchant-center-next-41658.html

Google confirmed today that Merchant Center Next — the newer interface that has been gradually replacing classic Merchant Center since 2024 — is dropping the "Next" branding entirely. It becomes simply "Google Merchant Center." Google was explicit that this is cosmetic: accounts, product feeds, campaigns, login flow and bookmarks are unaffected, and no action is required from account holders. The "Next" label will be phased out of help docs, emails and the interface itself over the coming weeks.

Low relevance to the current portfolio directly — none of the lead-gen or comparison sites run Shopping/product-feed campaigns today — but worth a line for anyone tracking Google's naming conventions ahead of a future e-commerce or affiliate-comparison play (the comparison network's affiliate-style listings could plausibly intersect with Merchant Center feeds down the line). Filed for completeness rather than urgency. [[Comparison Network]]
