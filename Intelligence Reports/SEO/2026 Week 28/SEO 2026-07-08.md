## Story 1: "Used or cited" — a working framework for the two ways brands actually appear in AI search

**Source:** Search Engine Land, "Used or cited: The two ways brands appear in AI search" (7 July) | https://searchengineland.com/used-cited-brands-appear-ai-search-481664
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Comparison Network]]

The piece names and separates two mechanisms that prior reporting (including yesterday's ChatGPT-referral study and Ahrefs' self-promotion experiment) had been describing without a clean vocabulary: **usage**, where an AI engine ingests brand information and folds it into a response without naming the source, versus **citation**, where the system explicitly references the brand via a link or clickable reference. The data backing the distinction is worth holding onto directly: AI Overview summaries drop blue-link click-through from 15% to 8% (Pew Research), AI-referred traffic converts at 11.4% versus 5.3% for organic search (Similarweb), and — the sharpest number — ChatGPT retrieves almost identical volumes of cited (~16.57) and uncited (~16.58) URLs per response, meaning being pulled into the model's answer and being credited for it are close to a coin flip regardless of content quality.

The practical implication lines up with yesterday's Ahrefs finding rather than contradicting it: ranking well on Google still correlates with AI citation (76.1% of AI Overview citations rank in Google's top 10 per an earlier Ahrefs study), so traditional SEO fundamentals aren't obsolete, but they're necessary rather than sufficient — a page can be fully "used" by a model's answer and get zero citation credit or referral traffic regardless of how well it ranks. For the SEO Portfolio and Comparison Network sites, the actionable distinction is that "am I being used" and "am I being cited" need separate tracking rather than one AI-visibility number; a site that's heavily used but rarely cited is feeding competitor answers for free.

## Story 2: Google Search Console adds native reporting for Instagram, TikTok, X and YouTube performance

**Source:** Search Engine Land, "Google Search Console gains reporting on social and video platforms" (7 July) | https://searchengineland.com/google-search-console-gains-reporting-on-social-and-video-platforms-481696
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Products]]

Google Search Console introduced "platform properties," letting site owners verify a social or video platform account and see how it performs in Google Search — clicks, impressions, post-level data, traffic trends and milestone tracking — across Instagram, TikTok, X and YouTube, once each is verified as its own property. This closes a real gap: previously there was no way to see how content on platforms Google doesn't operate performed in Google's own search results without third-party tooling. Rollout is gradual and requires per-platform verification, so it's not immediately usable everywhere. Relevant mainly to any portfolio or client property running an active social/video presence tied to organic strategy — worth a note for future GSC audits rather than action today.

## Story 3: ChatGPT Ads adds audience-list targeting, extending its ad-platform build-out

**Source:** Search Engine Land, "ChatGPT Ads rolling out audience lists" (7 July) | https://searchengineland.com/chatgpt-ads-rolling-out-audience-lists-481712
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

OpenAI is rolling out an "Audiences" tool letting advertisers upload raw or hashed email/phone lists as targeting filters on ChatGPT Ads — standard first-party-data targeting infrastructure, but notable for how quickly it's arriving after yesterday's story on ChatGPT's auto-generated ad creative feature. Two ad-platform capabilities landing within 24 hours of each other is a clearer signal than either alone that OpenAI is building ChatGPT Ads toward parity with Meta/Google ad tooling rather than treating it as an experimental bolt-on. No action needed today, but worth keeping on the radar if any portfolio paid-media spend ever considers an AI-platform placement test.
