## Story 1: A working framework for justifying GEO spend without perfect attribution

**Source:** Search Engine Land | https://searchengineland.com/geo-investment-attribution-482108
**Related vault notes:** [[SEO Portfolio]], [[Comparison Network]]

Dale Bertrand's piece lands on a genuinely useful operating rule: "if a number doesn't translate into dollars, it's a channel metric, not a business metric." His argument is that GEO (generative engine optimisation, getting cited and recommended inside AI answers) will never have clean, click-level attribution the way paid search did, so teams that wait for perfect measurement before investing will simply lose the window to competitors who fund it on directional evidence instead.

The practical method: build "fuzzy math" estimates that connect AI-citation presence to pipeline, using signals like branded search growth and mention rate on revenue-driving queries, then translate that into a dollar figure a CFO can act on. His worked example: a 10% mention rate on a query type, multiplied by call volume, average contract value, and win rate, produces a defensible influenced-revenue number even without last-click attribution.

This is directly usable for the portfolio and for KlientFlo-style client pitches: none of the programmatic SEO sites or the comparison network have clean GEO attribution either, and this gives a concrete way to build a dollar-denominated case for continued investment (or for selling GEO work to clients) without pretending to a precision that doesn't exist in this channel yet.

## Story 2: Agentic commerce, not ChatGPT ads, is the shift that matters for product-based sites

**Source:** Search Engine Land | https://searchengineland.com/agentic-commerce-chatgpt-ads-482010
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]], [[SEO Portfolio]]

The counter-argument to the ChatGPT-ads hype cycle: ChatGPT's ad platform has flat user growth since September 2025 and a reported $21bn operating loss in 2025, and lacks Google's distribution advantages, while referral traffic from ChatGPT to the wider web still grew 206% in 2025, reflecting existing users going deeper rather than genuine audience growth. The bigger structural shift the piece flags is agentic commerce: AI agents (Google's Universal Cart, Amazon's Alexa for Shopping) completing purchases directly, which routes around ad creative entirely and instead reads structured product-data feeds.

The actionable implication: visibility to purchasing agents depends on clean product feeds, correct structured data, and API integrations, not on bidding strategy. For the comparison network specifically (mortgage, money, insurance comparisons), this is the same pattern as GEO citation, the sites that win future agent-mediated referral traffic will be the ones with the cleanest, most machine-readable data layer, not the ones spending on ads. Worth an audit of structured data completeness across the comparison network sites as a low-cost, high-leverage action item.
