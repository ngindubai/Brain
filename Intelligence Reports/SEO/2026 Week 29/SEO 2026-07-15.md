## Story 1: Google Ads requires passkeys for sensitive actions, effective today

**Source:** Search Engine Roundtable | https://www.seroundtable.com/google-ads-passkey-sensitive-actions-41286.html ; PPC Land | https://ppc.land/google-ads-will-require-passkeys-for-sensitive-actions-from-july-15/
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]], [[SEO Portfolio]]

Starting today, Google Ads requires a passkey (not a password) to complete a defined list of sensitive actions: adding new users, changing billing information, updating account links, and changing user access. Google flagged this by email weeks ago, but today is the enforcement date.

Two operational snags worth knowing before anyone tries to make an account change and gets blocked. First, a newly created passkey takes one to two days to pair with the Ads system, so it is not instant. Second, a new passkey can carry a seven-day security delay before it is trusted for sensitive actions, meaning someone setting one up today could still be locked out of billing or user-access changes until roughly 22 July. Devices need a screen lock (Face ID, Touch ID, or PIN) to register a passkey at all.

This is account hygiene, not ranking risk, but it is the kind of thing that quietly breaks someone's morning. Any Orwell agent or contractor with billing or user-management rights on Google Ads accounts (comparison network paid campaigns are the obvious exposure — mortgagecompare-ae, moneycompare-ae, insurecompare-ae) should have a passkey registered now rather than mid-emergency. Check who currently has access before assuming it is sorted.

## Story 2: AI Overviews can now generate images directly

**Source:** Search Engine Land | https://searchengineland.com/google-ai-overviews-now-lets-you-create-image-482163

Dated 14 July, carried today because no report ran yesterday. Google has wired its Nano Banana image model into AI Overviews, so a query that would previously have surfaced an image pack or sent a user to Google Images can now generate a bespoke image on the results page itself. That is one more click Google keeps in-house rather than sending to a publisher or an image search results page.

Combined with the Image Search redesign below, the direction is consistent: Google wants image discovery to happen inside AI-generated surfaces it controls end to end, not through a results page that hands traffic elsewhere. Nothing here is actionable for the portfolio today, but it is one more data point that visual content increasingly earns exposure through being ingested and remixed by Google's models rather than clicked through to source.

## Story 3: Google Image Search drops the search box for an AI-curated gallery

**Source:** Search Engine Land | https://searchengineland.com/google-image-search-drops-clean-search-box-and-adds-gallery-of-images-482153

Also 14 July. To mark Image Search's 25th anniversary, Google rebuilt the images.google.com homepage: the plain search box is gone, replaced with an interactive gallery of live, personalised image collections plus save-and-browse features. It is the most significant visual overhaul of that product in years.

Paired with Story 2, this reads as Google repositioning Image Search as a discovery and generation surface rather than a pure lookup tool. For portfolio sites with strong image assets (pet transport, close protection, bus hire all have obvious photo content), there is no immediate action, but it is worth watching whether image-pack referral traffic shifts as the new gallery format beds in over the coming weeks.

## Story 4: Visual layout and "centerpiece annotation" as a ranking signal

**Source:** Search Engine Land | https://searchengineland.com/visual-semantics-topical-authority-482254

Koray Tuğberk GÜBÜR's 14 July piece argues page layout and visual structure meaningfully influence rankings, not just text. The core claim: Google classifies documents partly through "centerpiece annotation", identifying which visual and functional element on a page is the actual subject, and topical authority assessments run through that lens as much as through copy.

Take this as one analyst's argument rather than a confirmed Google mechanism, Google has not corroborated centerpiece annotation as a public ranking factor. But it lines up with the broader pattern of Google's systems reading pages as rendered documents, not raw HTML. For the SEO portfolio's templated programmatic pages, the practical read is: a page that is visually one thing (a directory listing, a comparison table) but tries to rank for a different intent (a guide, a review) may be sending mixed signals regardless of the text. Worth a light audit on the comparison network pages, not urgent.

## Story 5: OpenAI's ChatGPT ad revenue target reported to be wildly out of step with the market

**Source:** Search Engine Land | https://searchengineland.com/openai-chatgpt-ads-100-billion-revenue-target-482365

14 July. eMarketer estimates the entire US chatbot advertising market will reach only $5.41 billion by 2030, against reports that OpenAI is targeting $100 billion in ChatGPT ad revenue alone. That is roughly an eighteen-fold gap between one company's internal target and a third-party estimate of the whole market.

This matters for the AI-referral and GEO conversation because it is a concrete signal that AI-chat advertising monetisation is nowhere near proven at scale, whatever the usage numbers say. If ChatGPT ad load increases materially to chase that target, it changes the user experience calculus for AI-referral traffic quality, worth revisiting when there is an actual ad rollout to assess rather than a target number.

## Story 6: GBP "no reviews yet" dashboard bug — status correction

**Source:** Search Engine Journal | https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-business-profiles-showing-empty-review-dashboards/582015/ ; Google Business Profile Help Community threads (multiple, dated 10-12 July)

Monday's report noted Google said the dashboard "no reviews yet" bug was fixed 10 July with a day or two for full reappearance, and flagged that window had passed as worth checking. It has not fully cleared. Community threads dated as late as 12 July show profiles still showing full review counts on the public listing (one at 881 reviews, another at 209) while the management dashboard still reports "no reviews yet." Workarounds — clicking the Unreplied tab, replying via email notification — work for some profiles and not others. No confirmed today-dated update from Google on this.

**Portfolio exposure:** any portfolio or comparison-network listing that leans on review counts for local trust signals should be manually checked in the dashboard rather than assumed fixed, until Google posts a clean resolution.
