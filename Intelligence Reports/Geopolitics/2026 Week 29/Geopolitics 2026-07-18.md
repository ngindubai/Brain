## Story 1: Iran war escalates to direct infrastructure strikes on both sides — Kuwait's power grid hit, Chah Bahar surveillance tower destroyed, and a second chokepoint now in play

**Source:** [The War Zone](https://www.twz.com/news-features/u-s-iran-fight-heats-up-with-mutual-strikes-on-infrastructure-targets), [ABC News](https://abcnews.com/International/live-updates/iran-live-updates-kuwait-reports-attacks-after-latest/?id=134704698), [Middle East Eye](https://www.middleeasteye.net/countries/iran), [FDD/Long War Journal](https://www.longwarjournal.org/archives/2026/07/iran-reportedly-instructs-houthis-to-close-bab-al-mandeb-strait-if-us-strikes-power-grid.php)
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]], [[Ventures]], [[Markets]]

The US completed a seventh consecutive night of strikes on Iran, and for the first time both sides moved deliberately onto infrastructure rather than military assets alone: CENTCOM confirmed destroying the Chah Bahar Shahid Kalantari Port surveillance tower — part of the IRGC's maritime tracking network for the Strait of Hormuz — and hit bridges in the southern port city of Bandar Khamir. Iran responded with its heaviest wave of strikes on Kuwait in weeks, damaging a power-generation and desalination station and wounding four Kuwaiti navy personnel; Qatar's foreign ministry separately condemned Iranian strikes on Jordan, Bahrain and Kuwait. Layered on top, Reuters sources report Iran's leadership has discussed using the Houthis to close the Bab el-Mandeb Strait — roughly 7% of global oil output transits it — if the US strikes Iran's domestic power grid, with missiles and drones reportedly already staged near the waterway awaiting an IRGC order. That would put a second major chokepoint at risk alongside Hormuz for the first time this war. Framing divergence: Gulf states and Washington describe this as Iran escalating against civilian infrastructure and adjacent countries that aren't party to the conflict; Iranian channels continue to frame US infrastructure strikes as the provocation. Both readings agree on the facts on the ground — infrastructure, not frontlines, is now the target set on both sides — which is itself the most important development of the week.

## Story 2: Gaza has its deadliest day in weeks — 14 killed including a drone strike on a funeral procession, reviving the ceasefire-fragility thread

**Source:** [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/17/israeli-attacks-on-gaza-kill-14-including-mourners-attending-funeral), [Middle East Monitor](https://www.middleeasteye.net/countries/iran), [Middle East Monitor](https://www.middleeastmonitor.com/20260717-israeli-drone-strike-on-funeral-gathering-in-central-gaza-kills-8-injures-20/)
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

Israeli strikes across Gaza killed at least 14 Palestinians into Friday, more than half of them in a single drone strike on a funeral procession in the Nuseirat refugee camp — mourners had gathered outside the Ahmad Yassin Mosque for the funeral of a Palestinian killed earlier the same day. Al-Awda Hospital received eight bodies and treated 20 wounded; the Israeli military confirmed the strike, saying it targeted a "terrorist cell" and acknowledged awareness of claims that uninvolved civilians were harmed. That brought the toll to 25 killed over 72 hours. This report picks the story up as the freshest available development on a thread the "Gaza ceasefire fragility" open loop was dropped from yesterday's brief for lack of movement — it revives that loop rather than reopening a resolved one. No new Israeli or international reaction has landed as of today; worth a same-day check tomorrow on whether this becomes a diplomatic flashpoint given it's the sharpest single-incident toll in weeks.

## Story 3: Kyiv protests Fedorov's ouster as Russia keeps striking civilian areas; EU-Ukraine Defense Industrial Partnership launches

**Source:** [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/), search-indexed Ukraine coverage
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Thousands rallied in Kyiv today against President Zelensky's dismissal of Defence Minister Mykhailo Fedorov, the criticism flagged as an open question in yesterday's brief now visibly spilling onto the streets rather than staying confined to lawmakers and veterans. Russian forces continued striking civilian areas overnight and today — guided bombs hit Zaporizhzhia, killing three and wounding three more, and an FPV drone strike killed three elderly women in Kharkiv region's Kindrashivka community. Separately, and more constructively, the EU and Ukraine formally launched a new Defense Industrial Partnership today aimed at combining Kyiv's battlefield expertise with EU manufacturing capacity to scale weapons production — a concrete institutional step rather than a statement of intent. The combination — public anger at a wartime leadership reshuffle, continued civilian casualties, and a genuine industrial-cooperation step forward on the same day — is worth tracking as three separate signals rather than one story: political cohesion, humanitarian cost and materiel capacity are all moving simultaneously and not in the same direction.

## Story 4: Taiwan's VP delivers a public rebuke of Beijing over the PNG closure, even as Xi's September Washington visit stays on the calendar

**Source:** [Taipei Times](https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/taiwan/archives/2026/07/18/2003860954), [South China Morning Post](https://www.scmp.com/)
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Taiwan Vice President Hsiao Bi-khim told reporters in Taipei today that China is "suppressing us everywhere — suppressing our chances to survive and to expand our international space," her sharpest public language yet on Papua New Guinea's decision to close Taipei's representative office, which Beijing publicly welcomed. Taiwan now has formal diplomatic relations with just 12 countries, three of them in the Pacific. The same day, SCMP reporting confirms Xi Jinping's planned September visit to Washington remains on the calendar, following Trump's own state visit to Beijing in May, with a G20/APEC meeting between the two leaders also expected later this year — meaning the US-China diplomatic track is proceeding in parallel with, not instead of, Beijing's pressure campaign on Taiwan's remaining relationships. That divergence — high-level US-China engagement continuing normally while Taiwan's diplomatic space keeps shrinking — is the more interesting signal than either fact alone, and sits against TSMC's deepening US capex commitment (covered in [[Markets]]) as the economic counterweight to Taiwan's eroding political position.

## Story 5: Sudan — UN humanitarian chief speaks directly with RSF commander as El Obeid siege puts half a million civilians at grave atrocity risk

**Source:** [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/1167871), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/)
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

UN Under-Secretary-General for Humanitarian Affairs Tom Fletcher held a direct call today with RSF commander General Muhammad Hamdan Dagalo ("Hemedti") to press for safe humanitarian access and safe civilian movement amid the escalating siege of El Obeid — a rare instance of the UN's top humanitarian official engaging a warring party's commander directly rather than through intermediaries, which signals how acute the UN judges the risk to have become. Drone strikes continue to damage schools, displacement sites and fuel infrastructure in the city; UN rights chief Volker Türk has separately said drone strikes killed over 1,000 Sudanese civilians in the first five months of 2026 alone, an 81% increase in drone attacks year-on-year. Around half a million civilians are assessed to be at grave risk of large-scale atrocities if the siege isn't broken. This sits alongside the Chinese-made drone interceptions flagged earlier this week as part of the same pattern — foreign-manufactured hardware showing up on both sides of a conflict now driving one of the world's most severe, and least-covered, humanitarian crises.
