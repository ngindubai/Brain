## Story 1: Trump reinstates the Hormuz blockade as a toll regime; Iran mocks the price

**Source:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/13/trump-iran-hormuz-strait-charge-reimburse.html) (search-indexed), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/07/13/us-strikes-iran-naval-blockade-trump-reversal-hormuz-fees/), [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/2026/07/13/trump-reimposes-us-blockade-and-demands-20-hormuz-shipping-fee), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/07/13/nx-s1-5891746/us-iran-strait-of-hormuz-updates), [TASS](https://tass.com/world/2150027)
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]], [[Ventures]]

Trump announced the US Navy will reinstate a full blockade of Iranian ports and coastline, effective 4pm ET today (14 July), branding the US "THE GUARDIAN OF THE HORMUZ STRAIT" and demanding a 20% toll on cargo transiting the strait in exchange for naval protection. Transit to non-Iranian destinations won't be impeded and humanitarian shipments are exempted, subject to inspection. Iranian Foreign Minister Araghchi responded by agreeing in principle that safe passage should be paid for, then turned the logic back on Washington: "Iran has always been the GUARDIAN of the Strait and will remain so FOREVER... 20% is of course too much. We will be fair" — effectively asserting Iran's own right to collect the fee. This is a genuine departure from the shooting-war framing of the past 72 hours: Washington is now trying to institutionalise a toll booth rather than just clear a channel by force. Divergence to flag: US framing treats the blockade as protective and self-financing; Chinese state-linked commentary (via earlier reporting this week) calls the same posture "dangerous and irresponsible," reflecting Beijing's exposure as the largest buyer of Iranian crude. Shipping remains near-collapsed — roughly 34 vessels transited yesterday against a pre-crisis norm of ~88/day. Brent settled the session up 4.2% to $79.22, WTI at $77.84.

## Story 2: Houthis break a four-year truce, fire on Saudi Arabia

**Source:** [SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/news/world/middle-east/article/3360444/yemens-houthis-fire-missiles-saudi-arabia-biggest-flare-years), [Japan Times](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/07/14/world/houthis-missiles-saudi-arabia/), [Washington Times](https://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2026/jul/13/houthis-yemen-strike-saudi-arabias-abha-airport-missiles-drones-sharp/), [Haaretz](https://www.haaretz.com/middle-east-news/2026-07-13/ty-article/saudi-arabia-strikes-houthi-airport-in-yemen-to-prevent-landing-of-iranian-plane/0000019f-5ba4-d0b9-ab9f-7bfcfc560000)

The Saudi-backed Yemeni government struck Sanaa International Airport, stating the aim was to stop an Iranian aircraft carrying a Houthi delegation — returning from Ali Khamenei's funeral in Tehran — from landing. The Houthis retaliated by firing missiles and drones at Abha International Airport in Saudi Arabia's south, intercepted by the Saudi-led coalition. This is the first Houthi attack on Saudi soil since the informal March 2022 truce, and it did not originate from the Hormuz/Israel-Iran track directly — it's the Iran funeral logistics dispute pulling Yemen back into the active conflict map. Al Jazeera and Gulf-based reporting frame this as a coalition-management failure as much as an Iran-proxy escalation; Saudi state messaging (via the coalition spokesperson on X) frames it purely as defensive interception, omitting the airport-strike provocation. Watch whether this reopens the Red Sea shipping-lane risk that had been quiet for over a year, compounding Hormuz-driven insurance costs.

## Story 3: Sudan — UN investigators warn El Obeid is following the El Fasher script

**Source:** [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/1167897), [Save the Children via UN reporting], [UNFPA flash update](https://www.unfpa.org/resources/flash-update-situation-el-obeid-north-kordofan-sudan-2-july-2026)

UN Human Rights Council-appointed investigators issued a fresh warning today that the RSF is replicating its El Fasher siege tactics around El Obeid — encirclement, infrastructure strikes, access restriction — and that El Obeid "must not become the next crime scene." Save the Children now puts displacement at over 11,000 people, including 5,500+ children, fleeing the city in the past two weeks alone, against a UN estimate that up to 500,000 civilians remain at risk if the siege intensifies. The RSF continues to hold all approach routes except the eastern corridor. No Western government has moved beyond the European Parliament's non-binding call (adopted last week) for an RSF terrorist designation — there is a gap between rhetorical alarm and any operational response, which is itself the story.

## Story 4: Beijing runs a deniable sovereignty campaign against the Philippines

**Source:** [Taipei Times](https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/taiwan/archives/2026/07/13/2003860660), [Manila Times](https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/07/10/news/ph-rejects-chinese-scholars-claim-over-batanes-dares-beijing-to-file-case-before-intl-court/2382624), [Rappler](https://www.rappler.com/philippines/department-foreign-affairs-statement-china-scholars-claim-batanes/), [Philippine Information Agency](https://pia.gov.ph/news/batanes-governor-reaffirms-ph-sovereignty-amid-chinese-scholars-claims/)

Batanes' provincial governor issued a fresh reaffirmation of Philippine sovereignty over the islands within the last day, the latest rung in an escalating back-and-forth: Chinese academics at a Jinan University symposium claimed Batanes as a "natural extension" of Taiwan and therefore China; Manila's Defence Secretary called it "baseless" and "ludicrous"; Beijing's Foreign Ministry has pointedly declined to either endorse or disavow the scholars, saying only that it doesn't "comment on the views of the academic community." That non-denial is the tell — it mirrors the plausible-deniability pattern Beijing has run on Ryukyu and Senkaku claims before state media picked them up formally. This sits inside the broader three-phase island-chain push flagged yesterday (record 110+ PLA/coast guard vessels around the First Island Chain, the Luzon-overflying SLBM test). No de-escalation signal from either side today.

## Story 5: Europe — Coalition of the Willing delivers a format, sanctions package stays stuck

**Source:** [Ukrinform](https://www.ukrinform.net/rubric-polytics/4143552-zelensky-to-attend-antiballistic-missile-coalition-coalition-of-willing-meetings-in-paris.html), [RBC-Ukraine](https://newsukraine.rbc.ua/news/macron-rallies-allies-in-paris-to-boost-ukrainian-1783878518.html), [Ukrainska Pravda](https://www.pravda.com.ua/eng/news/2026/07/13/8043841/)

Following yesterday's 37-nation Paris summit, the Coalition of the Willing translated talk into a named initiative: FREYJA, a new multinational integrated air/missile-defence programme with manufacturing spread across several European states, plus commitments on multinational force training and winter energy-sector support for Ukraine. Zelensky and 25 Ukrainian troops then marched in today's Bastille Day parade alongside coalition forces — a symbolic continuity signal rather than a new material commitment. Separately, the 21st EU sanctions package remains unadopted; foreign ministers could not reach consensus on 13-14 July, and the automatic-reversion clause on the Russian oil price cap now hinges on a 15 July deadline — miss it, and the cap reverts upward toward $58/barrel rather than holding at $47.60. That deadline, not today's talks, is now the thing to watch.

## Story 6: Colombia's president locks his successor out of a military inauguration

**Source:** [UPI](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2026/07/13/latam-colombia-petro-bars-Espriella-military-base-inauguration/5521783958093/), [Breakthrough News](https://breakthroughnews.org/2026/07/12/petro-and-de-la-espriella-teams-suspend-talks-as-colombias-political-tensions-deepen/)

Outgoing President Gustavo Petro has formally barred president-elect Abelardo de la Espriella from holding his 7 August inauguration oath at a military base, invoking his authority as commander-in-chief until the handover. Petro posted that military and police installations "are under my command... therefore, until that moment I am the supreme commander of the military forces." This is a harder institutional move than last week's transition-process boycott — it uses actual command authority rather than just political noise, and it's the first direct use of military-facility control as leverage in the standoff. International observers and electoral bodies have validated de la Espriella's win and found no fraud; most analysts still expect an on-schedule handover, but the margin for a genuine constitutional crisis has widened, not narrowed, since yesterday.

## Story 7: Graham's death turns a stalled Russia sanctions bill into a fast-moving one

**Source:** [The Hill](https://thehill.com/homenews/senate/5964754-russia-sanctions-bill-graham-legacy/), [RFE/RL](https://www.rferl.org/a/senate-trump-russia-sanctions-ukraine-graham/33801207.html), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-13/trump-to-support-graham-led-russia-sanctions-bill), [Congress.gov S.1241](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/1241)

Senator Lindsey Graham died Saturday night after returning from Ukraine, days after reaching agreement with the Trump administration and Senators Blumenthal, Shaheen and Wicker on an updated Russia sanctions bill — the Sanctioning Russia Act — that had sat stalled for over a year. His death has triggered a genuine bipartisan push: Schumer is pressing Thune to bring it to the floor "immediately," the bill now carries 85 cosponsors, and Trump has publicly backed it. The updated draft narrows the original's threat of up to 500% tariffs on countries (including China) still buying Russian oil and gas, but the scope reduction hasn't been finalised. No floor vote has happened yet as of writing. If this passes with China-linked secondary-tariff language intact even in narrowed form, it's directly relevant to chip and compute supply-chain risk.

## Story 8: India and the EU open a Trade and Technology Council round in Brussels

**Source:** [Tribune India](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/commerce-and-industry-minister/piyush-goyal-to-lead-business-delegation-to-europe-from-july-13-aims-to-boost-trade-investment-ties), [ProKerala](https://www.prokerala.com/news/articles/a1783721.html)

The third India-EU Trade and Technology Council ministerial opened in Brussels today (runs 14-15 July), co-chaired by Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal, External Affairs Minister Jaishankar and IT Minister Jitin Prasada, following on from the recently concluded India-EU FTA. WTO reform is on tomorrow's agenda. This is a middle-power realignment marker worth tracking rather than a single-day event: India is positioning itself as a technology and trade counterweight in the same window that Washington is tightening chip licensing on China and the EU is trying (and failing today) to tighten the sanctions net on Russia. No concrete deliverable emerged today; the meeting itself is the signal.
