## Story 1: US-Iran technical talks convene in Islamabad today, Washington attaches hard conditions

**Source:** Modern Diplomacy | https://moderndiplomacy.eu/2026/07/10/can-july-11-prevent-a-wider-middle-east-war/ | Times of Israel liveblog | https://www.timesofisrael.com/liveblog-july-11-2026/
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]], [[Ventures]]

A new round of US-Iran technical talks is underway today in Islamabad, building on the 14-page memorandum both sides signed 17 June, with Pakistan hosting and brokering as it did for the original April ceasefire (PM Shehbaz Sharif and army chief Field Marshal Asim Munir on the Pakistani side; Iran's foreign minister Abbas Araghchi attending). The agenda covers Iran's nuclear programme, sanctions relief, frozen assets and freedom of navigation through Hormuz. Washington is reportedly demanding Iran issue a public statement renouncing further attacks on shipping and forswearing any "tolls" on transiting vessels, plus a handover of "nuclear dust" — radioactive residue — before any deal proceeds, citing satellite imagery suggesting reconstruction activity at nuclear sites.

No adversary-state framing of today's specific round could be sourced despite targeted searches; that's logged as a genuine gap rather than silence being read as fact. The Western framing here is of the US holding a hard diplomatic line rather than rushing to close a deal. This is the single most consequential dated item this weekend: the outcome, or lack of one, is the direct input into Monday's oil-price reaction covered in today's Markets report, and by extension into UAE fiscal exposure and the mortgage-comparison network's rate sensitivity.

## Story 2: QatarEnergy pauses Ras Laffan LNG expansion after tanker attack

**Source:** NBC News live coverage (Bloomberg-sourced) | https://www.nbcnews.com/world/iran/live-blog/live-updates-iran-attacks-gulf-us-strikes-tehran-ships-hormuz-oil-rcna353439
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]], [[Ventures]]

Following the Iranian strike on a Qatari-linked LNG tanker in Hormuz earlier this week, QatarEnergy CEO Saad al-Kaabi has halted plans to ramp production back up at Ras Laffan, the world's largest LNG facility. The exact date of the decision is fuzzy in the sourcing ("earlier this week"), so this is logged as a recent-but-not-confirmed-today development rather than a today-dated event, but it's the first direct evidence the Hormuz crisis is now hitting gas-export capacity rather than just tanker traffic and oil pricing. Worth watching alongside the Islamabad talks: a gas-export pause at the world's largest LNG facility is a harder economic fact than a shipping-risk premium, and it sits directly upstream of the UAE's own energy-corridor exposure.

## Story 3: Senator Graham says the White House has agreed to back a new Russia sanctions bill

**Source:** Kyiv Independent | https://kyivindependent.com/us-senator-claims-white-house-backing-for-russia-sanctions-bill/ | RFE/RL | https://www.rferl.org/a/senate-trump-russia-sanctions-ukraine-graham/33801207.html
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

Senator Lindsey Graham announced on 10 July that the White House has agreed to support the long-stalled Graham-Blumenthal Russia sanctions bill, which has 85 co-sponsors and would empower the president to sanction countries still buying discounted Russian oil. A Senate vote is expected once the chamber returns to Washington. This is a genuinely new thread, not previously logged, flagged here a day after Graham's announcement because it's the first concrete legislative movement on secondary sanctions in months. If passed, this is a mechanism that could reach grey-market Russian-oil buyers directly, worth tracking for knock-on effects on Gulf oil-trading intermediaries and shipping insurers already stretched thin by the Hormuz situation.

## Story 4: EU confirms 13 July adoption date for its 21st Russia sanctions package

**Source:** Ukrainska Pravda | https://www.pravda.com.ua/eng/news/2026/07/09/8043181/ | Consilium | https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/policies/sanctions-against-russia/timeline-sanctions-against-russia/
**Related vault notes:** none directly

The EU Foreign Affairs Council is now set to adopt its 21st sanctions package on 13 July, covering asset freezes on roughly 90 banks and transaction bans on 30-plus entities. The oil price-cap renewal deadline sits two days later, on 15 July. A proposed Schengen entry ban on former Russian combatants is being softened after French and Italian objections, and fish-related sanctions provisions have been dropped. This is a new hard date rather than a today event, distinct from the already-tracked Ukraine Patriot-licensing thread, and it intersects directly with the oil-price watch: a lapsed price cap landing in the same fortnight as a live Hormuz crisis would compound rather than offset global crude pressure.

## Story 5: China moves to ease Nvidia H200 chip access for Alibaba, ByteDance and DeepSeek

**Source:** Bloomberg via Yahoo Finance | https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/ai/articles/china-plans-let-top-ai-150506111.html | Tom's Hardware | https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/semiconductors/nvidia-prepares-h200-shipments-to-china-as-chip-war-lines-blur
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Chinese regulators will reportedly let the country's top AI firms apply to buy a capped allotment of Nvidia H200 chips, under 200,000 units total, less than half of what those firms originally sought, easing Beijing's self-imposed ban that had been aimed at forcing domestic chip self-reliance. Nvidia shares rose about 1% on the report. Dated 8 July and not previously logged, this is a genuine and notable divergence from the Hormuz picture: chip-access friction between the US and China is easing at the same moment military friction in the Gulf is escalating, two separate axes of the same broader compute-and-energy landscape moving in opposite directions. Directly relevant to GPU compute-cost planning.
