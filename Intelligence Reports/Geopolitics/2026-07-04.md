## Story 1: Khamenei's Funeral — Four Days of Contradictory Scheduling, and a Threat Against His Son That Still Hasn't Been Answered

**Source:** Al Jazeera | https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/7/3/iran-war-live-tehran-slams-us-ahead-of-huge-funeral-for-ali-khamenei | India TV News | https://www.indiatvnews.com/news/world/will-mojtaba-khamenei-attend-ali-khamenei-s-funeral-questions-persist-as-iranian-officials-stay-mum-2026-07-02-1046941 | RT | https://www.rt.com/news/642514-iran-khamenei-funeral-warning/ | Republic World | https://www.republicworld.com/world-news/israeli-threats-surveillance-risks-mojtaba-khamenei-to-skip-his-father-ali-khamenei-s-funeral-over-security-concerns-2026-07-03-131023
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]], [[Ventures]]

Today's dated fact, per Al Jazeera and multiple wire-adjacent outlets: Khamenei's coffin was moved to Tehran's Grand Mosalla for public viewing today, 4 July, with Iranian state media broadcasting the first images of the casket and President Pezeshkian calling for large public turnout to project unity. Delegations from more than 30 countries and religious representatives from over 90 are expected across the coming days, with confirmed attendance from Pakistan, China, India, Turkey and Russia — a genuinely wide diplomatic turnout that reads as a data point on where Tehran's post-war alignment actually sits, distinct from Western framing of an isolated regime.

But the scheduling confusion flagged in prior reports hasn't resolved — it's deepened. India TV, reporting on the same event, describes ceremonies running 5–9 July, not today. That is now at least three different day-by-day sequences reported by credible outlets across four consecutive days of coverage (Wikipedia's own page, Iran International, Western wire-adjacent liveblogs, and now Indian press). This is not a trivial detail: it means even the logistics of Iran's most significant state event since Khomeini's 1989 burial are not being communicated coherently, which is itself informative about the state of internal command and control during a leadership transition happening mid-war.

The dangerous thread has not moved. Israeli Defence Minister Israel Katz's statement that Mojtaba Khamenei — the son now serving as Supreme Leader — is "marked for death" still stands unanswered by any Israeli walk-back, and Mojtaba is still reported to be skipping his own father's funeral over the security risk it creates. Iran's Foreign Minister Araghchi's "immediate powerful response" warning and military commander Ali Abdollahi's "harsh retaliation" language remain the only Iranian response on record. RT's coverage (read defensively, as Kremlin-adjacent state media) frames this purely as Iran warning the US and Israel off any attack during the funeral — a framing that omits the Katz threat entirely, which is itself a useful data point on what Russian state media chooses not to report about Israeli conduct. No new escalation occurred today, but nothing has been walked back either, and the underlying structural risk — a frozen diplomatic channel during the exact week these threats are live — is unchanged from yesterday.

## Story 2: Hormuz — No Confirmed Parliamentary Outcome Yet, Toll Dispute Still Unresolved

**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]], [[Ventures]]

Iran's parliamentary vote on ratifying the June MoU — reported as scheduled for "this week" in prior reports — still has no confirmed outcome as of today's search. The absence of any headline reporting a rejection is suggestive (a rejection would almost certainly be reported immediately, given how closely this is being watched) but not proof either way; it's equally plausible the vote was quietly deferred to avoid a public rupture during the funeral period, which would be a rational hardliner calculation given the same politicians now need to look unified for the ninety-country diplomatic audience arriving this week. Iran's military "forceful response" order against non-Tehran-approved shipping routes remains in force, unchanged since it was first reported.

TWZ's tracking shows the practical shipping picture stuck in the same holding pattern as prior reports: an IMO-coordinated plan to evacuate ships still stranded from earlier in the crisis has been paused, not cancelled, after an Iranian drone strike on a vessel — meaning the "return to normal routing" case remains unproven even as headline crossing numbers slowly recover. Middle East Eye's regional-sourcing angle adds one new detail worth flagging: reported US concern that Israel might attempt to assassinate Iran's Foreign Minister specifically to disrupt the Doha-adjacent talks — an allegation, not a confirmed plan, but one that fits the same pattern as the Katz threat against Mojtaba Khamenei: this week's rhetoric on the Israeli side is running hotter than the "channel frozen for the funeral, resumes after" framing implies.

## Story 3: A Front This Report Hasn't Been Tracking — Israel Claims Permanent Control South of Lebanon's Litani River

**Source:** Middle East Eye | https://www.middleeasteye.net/live-blog/live-blog-update/israel-says-it-will-control-bridges-and-area-south-lebanons-litani-river | NBC News | https://www.nbcnews.com/world/lebanon/israel-captures-key-lebanon-site-crossing-litani-river-rcna347732 | The New Arab | https://www.newarab.com/news/israel-strikes-lebanons-bridges-threatens-key-border-crossing
**Related vault notes:** none previously — new thread for this report series

Genuinely new to this report series, not new to the world: a separate Israel-Lebanon war has been running since roughly March 2026, distinct from the Iran conflict, and it has now reached a point where Israel's defence minister says the IDF will permanently control the remaining bridges over the Litani River and the territory south of it — a de facto redrawing of the operative Lebanese border, not a temporary buffer-zone claim. Israeli forces reportedly captured Beaufort Ridge, a militarily significant high ground, after crossing the river, and multiple Litani bridges (Qasmiyeh, Dalfa, Qaqqaia, Khardali, Tir Faliseh) have been destroyed to sever the south from the rest of the country. Netanyahu is quoted vowing to expand Israel's grip further, calling this the deepest incursion into Lebanon in 26 years.

This has been running for months without appearing in this report's prior editions — a genuine coverage gap, flagged here rather than glossed over. Whether it deserves standing coverage going forward depends on whether it starts intersecting with the Iran/Hormuz picture (a second active Israeli front while the Khamenei succession and Hormuz dispute are both live is a meaningfully different regional risk picture than either conflict in isolation) — worth a decision on scope rather than defaulting to either full silence or full coverage.

## Story 4: Ukraine — Kyiv Death Toll Climbs to 30 as Ukraine's Own Long-Range Campaign Reportedly Rattles Russia

**Source:** NPR | https://www.npr.org/2026/07/03/g-s1-131920/up-first-newsletter-america-250-heat-wave-ukraine-russia-democratic-party | Al Jazeera | https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/3/russian-advance-collapses-in-ukraine-as-anxiety-rises-in-moscow | Al Jazeera | https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/2/the-crisis-is-deep-the-view-from-russia-as-fuel-shortages-worsen
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Thursday's mass Russian missile-and-drone strike on Kyiv (nearly 600 munitions per Zelenskyy, targeting what Ukraine says were residential buildings, an ambulance station, a research institute and a hotel) has had its confirmed death toll revised upward through the week: 22 initially, then 27, now 30 confirmed dead with more than 90 injured as rescue crews kept finding bodies under a collapsed apartment building in the Darnytskyi district. Russia's framing — that this was proportionate retaliation for Ukrainian strikes on "civilian infrastructure" — remains directly contradicted by Ukraine's own account, that its strikes have targeted refineries and military-linked logistics sites, not civilian targets. That factual divergence is unresolved and, per standing practice, not something open-source reporting alone can adjudicate.

The less-covered side of this story: per Al Jazeera and the Washington Post (both Western outlets, read with that lens), Zelenskyy's 25 June-announced 40-day campaign of long-range strikes against Russian fuel and logistics infrastructure — a "Logistical Lockdown" strategy — has visibly escalated (attacks up from 210 in May to 303 in June) and is generating genuine public anxiety inside Russia: long queues at petrol stations, described as common now even in Moscow, with some pumps reportedly dry. The Institute for the Study of War's territorial data backs the framing that this is working militarily, not just symbolically — Russian territorial gains have collapsed from 2,190 sq km in the first half of 2025 to just 622 sq km in the same period this year. Ukraine's overnight strike on the Kstovo refinery (hundreds of miles east of Moscow, deep interior rather than a symbolic near-Kremlin target) fits this same widening pattern. Read together, both sides are inflicting real, escalating pain on the other's civilian-adjacent infrastructure and population — a genuinely symmetrical story that the casualty-count headlines alone don't convey.

## Story 5: China — Rare Earth Enforcement Tightens, Still No State-Media Framing to Read

**Source:** South China Morning Post (via search) | https://www.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3358238/china-offers-rewards-reporting-rare-earth-export-control-violations | Al Jazeera | https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/22/china-adds-10-us-firms-including-rare-earth-miner-to-export-control-list
**Related vault notes:** [[Agent Org]], [[Ventures]]

New enforcement measures took effect 1 July: China is now offering financial rewards for reporting rare-earth export-control violations, covering unlicensed exports, circumvention attempts and illegal transfer of strategic-mineral processing technology abroad. This tightens rather than escalates the existing framework — any foreign product containing 0.1% or more Chinese-origin rare earths, or made using Chinese processing technology, already requires a license. This follows the G7's agreement to cap Chinese rare-earth imports at 60%, and China's own blacklisting of MP Materials and USA Rare Earth among roughly ten named US firms in the retaliation list flagged in prior reports. The broader stand-down agreed in October 2025 remains formally in effect until November 2026, after which a wider suite of restrictions could be reimposed — meaning today's enforcement-tightening operates inside an existing truce rather than breaking it. Still no Xinhua or Global Times framing found on any of this — the fourth consecutive report with that specific gap, worth treating at this point as either a genuine absence of loud state messaging on the topic, or a search-visibility limitation that direct-language sourcing might resolve.
