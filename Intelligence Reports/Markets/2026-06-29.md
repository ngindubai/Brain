## Story 1: Micron Q3 FY2026 — $41.5B revenue (+346% YoY), 84.9% gross margin, $50B Q4 guidance; Wall Street repositions memory as the AI infrastructure trade

**Source:** CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/25/micron-stock-3q-earnings-memory.html
**Additional source:** StockTitan | https://www.stocktitan.net/news/MU/micron-technology-inc-reports-record-results-for-the-third-quarter-6f50161e5zxh.html
**Additional source:** TechCrunch | https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/28/why-wall-street-thinks-us-memory-maker-micron-is-the-next-nvidia/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Micron Technology reported fiscal Q3 2026 results that were, by any measure, extraordinary: revenue of $41.46 billion against analyst expectations of $36 billion (+15% beat), non-GAAP EPS of $25.11 against $22 expected, gross margin at a company-record 84.9%, and Q4 revenue guidance of approximately $50 billion (Q4 FY2025 was $11.3 billion — this is a 340%+ YoY guidance). Micron's market capitalisation has surpassed Meta, placing it among the top-tier US public companies.

The driver is clear: HBM (High Bandwidth Memory), used in Nvidia H200 and AMD MI300X clusters, is structurally undersupplied. AI training and inference are memory-bandwidth-constrained rather than compute-constrained at current model sizes. Micron is shipping HBM3e at scale and has signed 16 long-term supply agreements with major customers across data centres and automakers, locking in $22 billion in committed revenue.

Wall Street's repositioning of memory as "the next Nvidia trade" reflects a deeper thesis: as AI inference scales, the binding constraint shifts from FLOPS to memory bandwidth. Micron and SK hynix are in a duopoly on HBM supply with Micron gaining share. SK hynix filed for a $29.4 billion Nasdaq IPO this week (see Story 3), bringing the only serious HBM competitor into US capital markets.

**GPU compute venture relevance:** The HBM constraint is one of the primary reasons GPU cluster pricing is elevated. If Micron's $50B Q4 guidance holds, HBM supply is growing faster than previously modelled — which should eventually ease cluster pricing and improve the economics of the GPU compute marketplace thesis. The 16 long-term supply agreements Micron signed also lock in a significant portion of HBM output, which means spot-market HBM remains scarce even as total supply grows.

## Story 2: Broadcom Q2 FY2026 — AI guidance miss triggers $1.3 trillion semiconductor selloff on June 5; recovery since

**Source:** CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/03/broadcom-avgo-earnings-report-q2-2026.html
**Additional source:** Intellectia.ai | https://intellectia.ai/blog/semiconductor-stocks-selloff-june-2026
**Additional source:** Kavout | https://www.kavout.com/market-lens/what-triggered-the-recent-semiconductor-sell-off
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Broadcom reported Q2 FY2026 revenue of $22.19 billion (beat consensus $22.13 billion) and non-GAAP EPS of $2.44 (beat $2.39). However, Q3 AI chip sales guidance of $16 billion came in below the $17.2 billion analyst estimate, and Broadcom did not raise its full-year 2026 AI semiconductor sales forecast.

The market reaction was disproportionate: Broadcom shed 14% on June 4, triggering a sector-wide selloff that erased $1.3 trillion in market capitalisation from global chip stocks on June 5. Nvidia shed over $300 billion in market cap. Marvell cratered 17%. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOXX) fell 10% in a single session — its worst day in this cycle.

The selloff has since partially recovered, particularly after Micron's blowout results on June 24-25. The episode demonstrates the market's sensitivity to AI chip demand signals. A single guidance miss from Broadcom, even with a revenue beat, was sufficient to reset the entire sector's near-term expectations.

For the GPU compute venture: the sell-and-recover pattern in AI chip stocks reflects genuine uncertainty about the cadence of hyperscaler capex. The underlying demand signal remains intact (Micron's $50B guidance proves AI training and inference are accelerating), but the pace of hyperscaler custom silicon adoption may reduce Broadcom's standalone AI revenue growth relative to expectations. The Jalapeño chip (OpenAI + Broadcom) and Google TPU trajectory suggest custom ASIC increasingly captures workloads that would otherwise run on Broadcom's merchant AI chips.

## Story 3: Anthropic and OpenAI confidential IPO filings; race to $1 trillion

**Source:** Fortune | https://fortune.com/2026/06/01/anthropic-confidentially-files-ipo-965-billion-valuation/
**Additional source:** CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/anthropic-open-ai-startup-value.html
**Additional source:** Crunchbase | https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/menlo-ventures-raise-ai-startup-funding-across-stages-anthropic/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Agent Org]]

Both Anthropic and OpenAI have filed confidential IPO prospectuses with Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley as joint bookrunners. Both are targeting late 2026 or early 2027 debuts.

**Anthropic:** $965 billion valuation (eclipsing OpenAI for the first time), $44 billion annualised revenue, first-ever operating profit in Q2 (~$559 million). Raised $65 billion in its most recent round. Anthropic now leads all frontier models on multiple benchmarks (Claude Opus 4.8 at 55.7% on Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index; 67.9 on LLM Stats overall score). Menlo Ventures — Anthropic's lead backer — announced $3 billion in new funds specifically to back AI startups, the firm's largest raise in 50 years.

**OpenAI:** $852 billion valuation, $25 billion annualised revenue, confidential filing June 8. Recently raised $122 billion in a record funding round. Preparing banks for a potential $1 trillion listing.

The combined SpaceX-OpenAI-Anthropic IPO pipeline represents approximately $3.6 trillion in targeted valuation — equivalent to France's GDP. This is the largest cohort of pre-IPO technology companies in history. The question for the market is whether the public appetite exists to absorb these listings at current private valuations after the semiconductor sector's June volatility.

For Orwell: Anthropic's near-$1 trillion valuation validates the AI infrastructure stack that Orwell uses as its primary build tool. The fact that Anthropic is profitable and growing at this scale means the API pricing model is sustainable for Anthropic long-term. OpenAI's $25B revenue figure suggests pricing pressure is coming — two profitable, IPO-bound AI labs competing for enterprise contracts will drive model commoditisation faster than a single dominant player would.

## Story 4: SK hynix $29B Nasdaq IPO; Qualcomm +14% on Modular acquisition

**Source:** SiliconAngle | https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/24/memory-maker-sk-hynix-files-29b-us-ipo-amid-ai-demand/
**Additional source:** SiliconAngle | https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/24/qualcomm-shares-jump-14-modular-acquisition-guidance-upgrade/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

SK hynix, the world's largest HBM memory supplier, filed for a US Nasdaq listing at a projected $29.4 billion valuation. The listing timing is deliberate: SK hynix is capitalising on record AI-driven HBM demand and Micron's earnings as a pricing comp. A US listing gives SK hynix access to the deepest pool of institutional capital for semiconductor investment.

Qualcomm jumped 14% on its acquisition of Modular — an inference software startup — combined with a significant FY2029 guidance upgrade. Qualcomm previewed two upcoming AI chips and raised expectations for non-handset revenue growth substantially. The Modular acquisition is strategically significant: it gives Qualcomm the inference software stack to make its AI chips attractive to enterprise customers who want end-to-end solutions rather than just hardware. This mirrors the Apple Silicon playbook — tightly integrated hardware and software outperforms best-of-breed component combinations.

**Source:** SiliconAngle | https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/24/openai-broadcom-debut-custom-jalapeno-chip-llm-inference/
**Additional source:** Crunchbase | https://news.crunchbase.com/ma/2026-mergers-acquisitions-record-cursor-spcx/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Products]]

OpenAI and Broadcom formally debuted the Jalapeño inference chip. Built following the Broadcom-Google TPU blueprint, Jalapeño is optimised for LLM inference at scale. OpenAI's stated goal is to reduce per-token inference cost by running dedicated silicon rather than Nvidia H100/H200 clusters for inference. If successful, this compresses the cost structure for every API caller, including Orwell — which would improve the economics of running high-volume agent workflows.

The SpaceX acquisition of Cursor/Anysphere at $60 billion is the largest startup acquisition ever recorded and reflects the strategic value of AI-augmented coding workflows. US startup M&A has reached $119.8 billion in 2026 YTD, on pace to exceed 2025's record. The volume of exits at high valuations validates the AI startup investment cycle that produced Anthropic and OpenAI's current valuations.

## Story 7: US-China trade — managed trade agreement forming; Section 301 12.5% forced labour tariffs; China rare earth controls

**Source:** CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/03/us-tariffs-60-economies-dection-301-forced-labor-trade-practices-.html
**Additional source:** China Briefing | https://www.china-briefing.com/news/us-china-relations-in-the-trump-2-0-implications/
**Additional source:** War on the Rocks | https://warontherocks.com/cogs-of-war/the-u-s-navys-subsea-rare-earth-vulnerability/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

The US-China trade relationship has settled into a "managed trade" framework following Trump-Xi meetings. A bilateral trade board is being created to manage non-sensitive goods; China committed to purchasing US aircraft and addressing US concerns on critical minerals. China faces a 24% effective tariff rate (down from earlier highs) with specific goods still near 30%.

The Section 301 forced labour investigation has produced proposed tariffs of 12.5% on China and 59 other economies. This is an escalation layer on top of existing tariffs and adds complexity to supply chain planning for any hardware-dependent business.

The most strategically important development is Beijing's June 2026 export control action placing **MP Materials and USA Rare Earth** — the two most prominent US domestic rare earth processing companies — on China's export control list. This directly targets US attempts to reduce rare earth dependency on Chinese processing. The War on the Rocks analysis notes that the Columbia-class submarine's propulsion, stealth, and strike precision systems depend almost entirely on Chinese-refined rare earths. The export control action is a strategic escalation below the level of tariffs but with longer-term defence industrial consequences.

For the GPU compute venture: rare earth export controls affect the magnets used in electric motors, cooling systems, and some power conversion equipment in data centres. This is an indirect constraint on data centre buildout, not a direct GPU supply issue, but worth tracking as a second-order cost factor.
