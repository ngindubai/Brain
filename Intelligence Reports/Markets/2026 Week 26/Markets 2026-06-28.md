## Story 1: Baseten $1.5B Series F and Groq $650M — inference is the capital destination
**Source:** Crunchbase | https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/biggest-funding-rounds-ai-marketing-robotics-baseten/ | TechCrunch | https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/18/ai-inference-startup-baseten-reportedly-raising-1-5b-months-after-its-last-mega-round/ | Groq | https://groq.com/newsroom/groq-raises-usd650m-to-scale-its-ai-inference-cloud-business | The Next Web | https://thenextweb.com/news/groq-650m-raise-nvidia-pivot
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Agent Org]]

Both rounds closed June 22, 2026, and together define the capital thesis for AI infrastructure in mid-2026: inference at scale is where the commercial value is being created, and investors are paying accordingly.

**Baseten $1.5B Series F** at a blended $13B valuation (two tranches). Led by Altimeter Capital, Conviction, and Spark Capital, with Sands Capital and Wellington Management as co-leads. IVP, Greylock, 01A, Blackbird, Durable Capital Partners, Verified Capital, Battery Ventures, and D.E. Shaw also participated. The business metrics behind the raise are striking: revenue grew 20x year-over-year, inference volume grew 40x, and the platform now processes more than 1 billion inference calls per day across 87 clusters and 18 clouds. The pitch is the inference layer at the app level — as open-source and closed-source models converge in capability and cost, the competitive differentiation shifts to the infrastructure that serves them: reliability, latency, and cost at scale.

**Groq $650M** led by Disruptive and Infinitum, scaling its AI inference cloud toward 200 megawatts of capacity by end-2027. Groq's current trajectory changed in December 2025 when it entered a non-exclusive licensing agreement with Nvidia, and at NVIDIA GTC in early 2026, Nvidia announced its next-generation LPX platform incorporating Groq's Language Processing Unit (LPU) technology. This legitimation by Nvidia — the dominant AI infrastructure vendor — while Groq raises $650M to scale cloud capacity, is the highest-credibility signal the company has received. The LPU architecture is purpose-built for inference (low latency, high throughput, low energy per token), not training. Groq is making the same directional bet as Baseten: inference is the durable commercial market.

The strategic frame: in 2023-2024, the winning AI infrastructure thesis was training compute (Nvidia monopoly). In 2025, the thesis bifurcated — training (still Nvidia) and inference (emerging competition). In 2026, the two Baseten/Groq rounds on the same day are a market signal that inference infrastructure is now a confirmed, fundable, at-scale business category. The training side remains dominated by Nvidia's H100/H200/Blackwell stack and the hyperscalers' custom silicon; the inference side is an open competition.

**For the GPU compute venture [[Ventures]]:** the Baseten and Groq rounds are the comparable set for any inference-positioned GPU infrastructure play. The question the venture needs to answer: is its model training-focused (increasingly locked out by Nvidia moat) or inference-focused (capital-rich, competitive, but requires operational excellence at very high volume)? Groq's path (custom silicon licensed to Nvidia) required deep technical moat. Baseten's path (cloud orchestration layer) required distribution and operational excellence at scale. The $1.5B/$650M in a single week confirms the market will reward either path if the numbers are real.

## Story 2: Semiconductor sector — Nvidia leads, AMD surges, Broadcom misses
**Source:** TipRanks | https://www.tipranks.com/news/nvidia-amd-and-broadcom-stocks-face-off-as-the-2026-ai-chip-battle-intensifies | Kavout | https://www.kavout.com/market-lens/what-triggered-the-recent-semiconductor-sell-off | Intellectia | https://intellectia.ai/blog/chip-stocks-rebound-investment-strategy-june-2026 | Motley Fool | https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/06/03/nvidia-vs-broadcom-which-ai-chip-stock-is-the-bett/ | Stocksdownunder | https://stocksdownunder.com/nvidia-broadcom-micron-ai-chip-stocks-computex/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

**Nvidia**: Fiscal 2026 (year ended January 2026) revenue of $215.9 billion — a new record. Now trading at approximately 25.4x forward earnings, a compressed multiple versus 2024 peak valuations, which reflects the market re-pricing growth expectations from unsustainable to more sustainable levels. The Vera Rubin GPU architecture is the next platform. No specific earnings event this week, but Nvidia remains the benchmark against which all AI chip moves are measured.

**AMD**: Year-to-date return of approximately 130% through early June 2026 (as of last reporting). The open philosophy — supporting multiple model backends, open standards, flexibility over proprietary lock-in — is resonating with hyperscalers looking to reduce single-vendor dependency on Nvidia. AMD chips are being adopted in cloud providers' inference fleets. The 12-month performance (+78% to +130% depending on measurement window) is the clearest market confirmation that the "not just Nvidia" diversification thesis is playing out.

**Broadcom**: Mixed picture. The Q3 2026 AI chip sales guidance of $16 billion fell short of analyst estimates, and Broadcom notably declined to raise its full-year 2026 AI semiconductor forecast — triggering a 14% single-day stock drop on June 4. However, the strategic positioning is strong and requires a longer view. Broadcom inked a $35 billion deal with Blackstone and Apollo to build the AI XPV infrastructure platform: 20 gigawatts of compute capacity over two years — the largest AI infrastructure commitment by any company outside the hyperscalers themselves. That deal is multiyear contracted revenue, not reflected in near-term chip guidance. Broadcom's second pillar is custom ASIC design: Apple, Google, Meta, and Amazon all use Broadcom-designed AI chips within their infrastructure. That business is sticky and growing.

**Sector-level event:** The June 5 semiconductor selloff was sharp — AMD down 10.86%, Intel down 11.28% on the day. Kavout attributed it to Broadcom's guidance miss triggering a sector-wide repricing rather than any fundamental change in AI demand. AMD's subsequent YTD performance confirms it was a market event, not a demand signal.

**Micron**: $50B Q4 revenue guidance on record, results due late September 2026. Memory pricing cycle is in upswing driven by HBM demand for AI training clusters. The Jalapeño engineering samples were delivered June 24; small-scale deployment target is late 2026.

## Story 3: OpenAI and Anthropic IPO tracks — autumn 2026, both targeting $1T+
**Source:** IndMoney | https://www.indmoney.com/blog/us-stocks/spacex-openai-anthropic-ipo-explained | IG UK | https://www.ig.com/uk/trading-strategies/anthropic-how-to-buy-shares-260612 | Zacks | https://www.zacks.com/featured-articles/761/anthropic-ipo | US News | https://money.usnews.com/investing/investing/articles/openai-vs-anthropic-ipo-which-ai-stock-should-investors-buy | Yahoo Finance | https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/article/spacex-openai-and-anthropic-here-are-the-most-anticipated-ipos-in-2026-114439441.html
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Agent Org]], [[Products]]

**Anthropic**: The funding staircase tells the story — $380B valuation (Series G, February 2026), then $965B (Series H-1, May 2026). S-1 filed confidentially June 1, 2026. Targeting $1T+ IPO valuation. Annualised revenue grew from $9B at end-2025 to $44B by May 2026 — a fivefold increase in under six months. The revenue trajectory is driven by enterprise API adoption, government contracts, and the consumer Claude.ai product. Revenue risk to monitor ahead of the roadshow: the Fable ban has removed the frontier product from general access; the Q2 2026 revenue run rate may reflect some enterprise deferrals. Anthropic can reasonably argue these are temporary, but the S-1 will need to address it. Earliest listing window: October 2026. Likely Nasdaq or NYSE.

**OpenAI**: $122B raised at $852B valuation. S-1 also filed confidentially. Revenue from approximately $2B annualised (end-2023) to $20B+ (end-2025). Near-term positive signals for the IPO narrative: GPT-5.6 limited trusted preview generating partner demand, advertising platform at $100M ARR in six weeks, 900M weekly active users, 50M paying subscribers. Structural risk for the IPO: employee stock lockups and the complexity of OpenAI's non-profit/capped-profit corporate structure, which the conversion to a fully public benefit corporation (announced this year) was designed to resolve. Roadshow timing unclear; autumn 2026 consensus.

Goldman Sachs projects total 2026 IPO proceeds could reach approximately $160 billion — a quadrupling from 2025. SpaceX, OpenAI, and Anthropic together target approximately $3.6 trillion in combined market capitalisation. If all three list in 2026, it would be one of the most concentrated IPO value events in history and would reshape the AI equity market fundamentally by providing liquid public-market vehicles for investors who currently have no direct access to frontier AI lab equity.

**For Orwell:** both IPOs make the underlying infrastructure publicly visible and priced. Anthropic listing at $1T+ would mean the market is pricing in years of continued dominance — that is validation of the stack Orwell builds on. More practically, public company status brings quarterly earnings: model pricing trajectory, API cost trends, competitive positioning — all become disclosed quarterly rather than rumoured. That gives better signal for [[Agent Org]] workflow cost modelling.

## Story 4: AI VC mega-cycle — $300B in Q1, AI at 81% of all VC
**Source:** Crunchbase | https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/record-breaking-funding-ai-global-q1-2026/ | Crunchbase | https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/menlo-ventures-raise-ai-startup-funding-across-stages-anthropic/ | Tech Startups | https://techstartups.com/2026/06/24/venture-capital-startup-funding-roundup-june-24-2026/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Q1 2026 set a new global venture funding record at $300 billion. AI captured 81% of deployed VC — the highest concentration in a single technology sector in venture history. By June 2026, the narrative has shifted from "will the AI funding cycle sustain?" to "which layer of the stack wins in the public markets?"

Notable rounds from the week of June 22-26 (beyond Baseten and Groq):

- **Menlo Ventures**: $3B raised across two funds (June 23), the firm's largest capital raise in its 50-year history. Stated focus: AI startups from seed through growth stage across enterprise, healthcare, and consumer. Menlo is an Anthropic early investor; its early stake is now worth multiples of the fund. The $3B raise signals continued LP appetite for top-tier AI VC even at late-cycle prices.
- **AppsFlyer**: $1B. Mobile attribution and analytics — infrastructure for measuring AI-driven advertising performance.
- **General Intuition**: $320M at $2B valuation. AI for robotic complex tasks — physical-world AI applications.
- **Supabase**: $500M at $10.5B valuation. Open-source developer platform and AI app builder infrastructure. The $10.5B valuation for a developer tooling company confirms that the infrastructure layer beneath AI applications is being priced at premium multiples.
- **Flourish**: $500M initial round, backed by Bezos, Lux Capital, and Google Ventures. Brain-inspired AI models — alternative architecture research. Speculative but the $500M initial round with top-tier investors confirms continued appetite for non-transformer AI architecture bets.
- **Assort Health**: $120M Series C. Healthcare AI for patient access automation.

The Tech Startups framing of June 24 capital is the best summary: "production AI" — systems that make real decisions, move money, route patients, govern agents — is the investment theme. Research AI and demo AI are not where late-cycle capital is flowing. This aligns with the Orwell build: operationalised agent workflows that replace human roles are exactly the "production AI" that investors are pricing at premium.

## Story 5: Fed holds, bank stress test passes — macro floor confirmed

The June 16-17 FOMC meeting: federal funds rate held at 3.50-3.75%. No rate cut signalled for the near term. The June economic projections were released alongside the statement. The rate hold removes one macro risk from the mid-year picture — no tightening surprise, and no cut that would upend the discount rate on growth-equity valuations.

June 24: the Federal Reserve's annual bank stress test confirmed all large US banks can weather a severe recession and continue lending to households and businesses. No bank failures, no capital requirement increases triggered. The stress test pass is a clean signal for financial system stability entering the second half of 2026 — relevant to the options/futures macro lens because it removes bank-failure tail risk as a near-term scenario.

The June 18 proposal requiring payment stablecoin issuers to maintain effective customer identification programmes is worth tracking: if UAE stablecoin-based financial products expand (a plausible development given CBUAE's progressive digital asset framework), financial comparison platforms covering these products need compliance-aware positioning.

**M&A context:** the SpaceX/Cursor $60B acquisition (agreed June 16, expected to close Q3 2026) is now confirmed as the largest startup M&A deal in history. The year-to-date M&A total of $119.8 billion already exceeds 2025's full-year record, with Capital One's purchase of Brex ($5.15B) and Qualcomm's acquisition of Modular ($4B) also in the mix. Goldman Sachs' $160B IPO proceeds forecast and the record M&A total together make 2026 the most active liquidity year in AI/tech history.

## Story 6: Chinese AI stocks — Hang Seng Tech -11% YTD, Baidu on Pentagon list
**Source:** Univest | https://univest.in/blogs/china-stock-market-sinks-ai-capital-rotation-june-2026 | Pluang | https://pluang.com/en/news-feed/3-saham-ai-china-yang-perlu-dipertimbangkan-2026 | SEC | https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001329099/000119312526262873/d116298dex991.htm
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

The Hang Seng Tech Index is down 11% year-to-date in 2026. The structural reason: the index is dominated by Chinese internet, e-commerce, and consumer platforms — Alibaba, Tencent, Meituan, JD — not semiconductor or AI chip manufacturers. Global funds are rotating away from Chinese consumer platforms toward US AI infrastructure plays. Only seven of the Hang Seng Tech Index constituents are in positive territory for the year.

**Baidu**: AI now represents 52% of total revenue. ERNIE 5.0 (2.4 trillion parameters, natively omni-modal) was released in late 2025. But June 9, 2026: the US Department of Defense added Baidu to its list of Chinese Military Companies. This designation does not automatically trigger sanctions but creates material compliance risk for US institutional investors — pension funds, government-affiliated investment vehicles, and ETFs with China restrictions may be forced to divest Baidu's US-listed ADRs. Baidu's Nasdaq ADR overhang is a concrete risk for H2 2026.

**Alibaba**: Cloud Intelligence Group posted $1.3 billion in quarterly revenue with triple-digit year-on-year growth. Analysts expect full-year earnings for FY ending March 2027 to rise 40%+. The AI cloud story is real and large, but it is embedded in a conglomerate whose core e-commerce and logistics businesses face macro headwinds from the Iran conflict's effects on regional trade. The Anthropic distillation accusation (Alibaba allegedly ran 28.8M Claude queries through 25,000 fraudulent accounts to train Qwen — see the 2026-06-26 combined report) adds regulatory and reputational risk for US institutional investors.

**JD**: One of only seven Hang Seng Tech constituents in positive YTD territory. AI-driven logistics efficiency is the core thesis; the company is more insulated from the consumer platform sentiment shift than pure-play consumer internet names.

Morgan Stanley called in May 2026 for a "big AI boost" to Chinese stocks. That call has not materialised in the broad index, though individual AI-pure plays have outperformed. The capital rotation thesis — from Chinese consumer platforms to US AI infrastructure — remains the dominant institutional behaviour.
