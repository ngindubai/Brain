## Story 1: Intel's Q2 blowout — beats, doubles EPS estimates, extends into Friday's session

**Source:** TradingKey | https://www.tradingkey.com/analysis/stocks/us-stocks/262050823-intel-earnings-report-q2-2026-intc-ai-data-center-intel-foundry-tradingkey ; CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/23/intel-intc-earnings-report-q2-2026.html
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[GitHub Library]]

Intel reported Q2 2026 revenue of $16.13bn, up 25% year-on-year and 12% above the $14.42bn Street consensus, with non-GAAP EPS of $0.42 against a $0.22 estimate — nearly double. Non-GAAP net income swung to $2.2bn from a $400m loss a year ago, and gross margin jumped 12.1 points to 41.8%. CEO Lip-Bu Tan called it the company's strongest quarterly revenue growth in over fifteen years.

The standout line was Data Center and AI revenue, up 59% to $6.26bn. Foundry — the unit under the most scrutiny since Tan's turnaround plan began — grew 31% to $5.8bn. Guidance for Q3 came in well ahead of Street numbers too: EPS of $0.38 on revenue of $15.8-16.8bn versus consensus of $15.1bn/$0.27.

Shares jumped over 13% after-hours to $112.70 and the move was still running into Friday's regular session. This is a genuine turnaround print, not a beat-and-guide-down: both the AI/data-centre line and the foundry line — the two things markets doubted Intel could deliver simultaneously — grew together.

Signal for the GPU compute venture: Intel's foundry growth is corroborating evidence that AI-driven demand for compute capacity is broad enough to lift a third player, not just Nvidia/AMD/TSMC. That's a demand-side positive for any compute-leasing or reselling model, even one with zero direct Intel exposure — it says the capacity crunch is real and multi-vendor, which is the thesis the venture depends on.

## Story 2: Asia's chip stocks sell off hard today, even as Intel beats

**Source:** TradingKey | https://www.tradingkey.com/analysis/stocks/more/262051035-japan-south-korea-markets-kospi-softbank-sk-hynix-kioxia-tradingkey ; Economy Middle East | https://economymiddleeast.com/news/stock-market-today-japans-nikkei-and-south-koreas-kospi-fall-amid-valuation-driven-ai-sell-off/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Comparison Network]]

Japan's Nikkei 225 fell 2.82% to 64,549.73 and South Korea's KOSPI dropped over 3% to 6,858.40 in Friday trading. Samsung Electronics fell 3.06%, SK Hynix slid 3.91%, Kioxia was down nearly 4%, and SoftBank Group dropped 5.85%.

The proximate trigger is carry-through from Thursday's Wall Street session — the Alphabet/Tesla-led Nasdaq slide (Nasdaq -2.15%) plus the Houthi-driven oil spike — rather than any new AI-demand data point. This is a valuation and risk-off move, not a fundamentals move: memory and HBM suppliers (Samsung, SK Hynix) are being sold on general de-risking even as the same day's Intel print confirms AI/data-centre chip demand is still accelerating.

For the GPU compute venture this is the useful distinction to hold onto: today's Asia weakness is a sentiment/valuation air-pocket in the AI trade, not a demand air-pocket. Memory and packaging supply constraints (HBM, CoWoS) that underpin compute scarcity pricing haven't changed; what's changed is how much multiple the market is willing to pay for that scarcity on any given day.

## Story 3: Oil gives back part of Thursday's Houthi spike, but the Hormuz/Red Sea premium stays

**Source:** OilPrice.com | https://oilprice.com/Energy/Oil-Prices/Brent-Tops-100-as-Houthi-Attacks-Push-Oil-Rally-Into-Triple-Digits.html ; TradingEconomics | https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/brent-crude-oil
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Brent spiked over 7% to roughly $101 on Thursday after Iran-backed Houthi forces claimed strikes on two Saudi tankers in the Red Sea and Kazakhstan suspended crude exports through the Caspian Pipeline Consortium terminal following separate drone attacks. By Friday's open, WTI was trading around $92.50 and Brent had eased toward $91 — giving back a meaningful chunk of Thursday's spike but sitting well above where it started the week.

The market is still pricing the risk of two simultaneous chokepoint disruptions (Hormuz and Bab el-Mandeb), which is why the pullback is partial rather than a full reversal. Nothing has actually resolved: the tanker strikes and the CPC suspension are both live, unclosed situations, and rhetoric out of Tehran continues to keep a floor under the risk premium.

For Orwell this is background rather than actionable — no Gulf-shipping or energy-trading exposure directly — but it's worth watching as a cost input if the compute venture's power/hosting arrangements have any energy-price sensitivity, and as a general volatility driver for any options/futures positioning this week.

## Story 4: Earnings season broadens beyond tech — American Express and Verizon report today, VIX still elevated

**Source:** AlphaStreet | https://news.alphastreet.com/american-express-q2-2026-earnings-preview-july-24-street-expects-4-40-eps/ ; 24/7 Wall St. | https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/07/23/will-american-express-or-verizon-come-out-as-an-earnings-winner/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

American Express and Verizon both report Q2 2026 results before today's open. Street consensus for AmEx is EPS of roughly $4.40 on $19.7bn revenue; for Verizon, consensus points to EPS growth with a 94% probability (per prediction-market pricing) of topping $34.5bn in quarterly revenue. Prediction markets are pricing high beat odds for both (AmEx ~88%, Verizon ~85.5%), reflecting still-resilient consumer spending and network revenue even as the macro mood elsewhere is jumpy.

This lands the same week as Intel's beat and Thursday's Alphabet/Tesla-driven tech slide, and the CBOE VIX closed Thursday at 18.70, up over 12% on the day — a clear signal that hedging demand is up heading into a session with chip earnings, consumer-finance earnings, and a live Middle East risk premium all colliding at once.

Read-through for Orwell: nothing directly actionable, but a genuinely wide earnings dispersion plus an elevated VIX is the kind of week where options/futures positioning (if any is held against the GPU compute venture or elsewhere) should stay hedged rather than directional into the FOMC meeting on 28-29 July and the remaining Big Tech earnings (Microsoft, Meta, Amazon, Apple all still to report).

None of this is a single today-dated catalyst — it's a running confirmation that capital is still pouring into AI infrastructure and tooling regardless of the day-to-day equity volatility described above. Flagging it as context rather than a story in its own right: the funding backdrop for anything Orwell might want to raise against or partner around in the AI-infrastructure space remains extremely permissive.

## What this means for Orwell

Today doesn't change the AI-capex thesis, it tests it: Intel's beat says demand is real and broadening past Nvidia/AMD, while Asia's chip-stock rout says the market will still punish anything AI-adjacent on a risk-off day regardless of fundamentals. That combination — strong demand signal, fragile multiple — is exactly the environment the GPU compute venture needs to underwrite against, not react to day by day. Oil and the elevated VIX are macro noise worth monitoring but not acting on. Nothing here requires a decision from Gareth today; the only thing worth carrying forward actively is watching whether Asia's semiconductor sell-off is a one-day air-pocket or the start of a real de-rating, which would be the first genuinely bad sign for the compute thesis this quarter.
