## Story 1: AMD confirms the Anthropic deal — up to 2GW of MI450s, a $5bn AMD equity stake, stock up ~$85bn over two days

**Source:** [AMD Advancing AI keynote](https://www.amd.com/en/corporate/events/advancing-ai/keynote.html), search-indexed FXLeaders/Stocktwits/Gurufocus/Yahoo Finance coverage
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Dr Lisa Su's keynote this morning at Moscone Center confirmed what the market had been pricing all week: Anthropic will deploy up to 2 gigawatts of AMD Instinct MI450-series GPUs across AMD's Helios rack-scale systems, with the first gigawatt going live in the first half of 2027. As part of the long-term partnership, AMD committed to a strategic equity investment of up to $5bn directly into Anthropic. AMD also confirmed the commercial launch of Zen 6 EPYC "Venice" on TSMC's 2nm node and priced Helios racks at $5-5.5m each. The stock reaction has been the story in itself: AMD gained roughly $85bn in market cap across the two-day event and now trades above $553, taking its market cap past $908bn and its year-to-date gain past 100%. This puts AMD alongside Broadcom as the second chip supplier confirmed across all three leading frontier labs' infrastructure build-outs (Microsoft, OpenAI, Meta and Oracle were already named AMD customers). Wells Fargo reiterated Overweight with a $615 target following the announcement. Worth tracking whether Nvidia responds with its own customer-diversification signal, since this is the clearest evidence yet that frontier labs are actively hedging their compute supplier concentration.

## Story 2: Alphabet beats on revenue but stock drops 5% after-hours on a record $205bn capex guide; Tesla misses on EPS despite 26% revenue growth

**Source:** search-indexed Yahoo Finance/TechBuzz/ZeroHedge coverage of Alphabet and Tesla Q2 earnings
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Alphabet's second-quarter numbers were strong on their face: revenue reached $119.8bn, up 24% year-on-year, with Google Cloud growing 82%. The stock still fell roughly 5% in after-hours trading once CFO Anat Ashkenazi confirmed the company was lifting full-year 2026 capex guidance to a record $205bn, up from prior guidance of roughly $75bn, and flagged that 2027 capex would rise "significantly" again. Tesla's revenue rose 26% but adjusted EPS came in well below Wall Street expectations, and the stock dipped roughly 5% after hours as investors weighed the company's ongoing pivot from EVs toward Cybercab, Megapack 3, Tesla Semi and Optimus production. By this morning, Nasdaq 100 futures had erased most of the overnight capex-driven losses as Asian chipmakers rallied (see Story 3), suggesting the market is treating the capex shock as a one-earnings-call scare rather than a trend change, for now. Intel closes out the week's earnings trilogy tonight after the bell; Wedbush has already warned not to assume a beat automatically rallies the stock given how much AI-infrastructure optimism is already priced in.

## Story 3: Asian chip stocks rally as Samsung and SK Hynix jump on AI buildout bets, cushioning the Alphabet capex shock

**Source:** search-indexed Bloomberg/CNBC coverage of Asian equity markets
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Asian shares advanced broadly today, with MSCI's Asia Pacific equities gauge up around 1% and South Korea's Kospi rising 2.8%. Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix each gained more than 3% on bets that South Korean memory and foundry suppliers will capture a growing share of AI infrastructure spending, directly offsetting the overnight Nasdaq futures hit from Alphabet's capex guidance. This is the regional counterpart to Story 1: the market's read across both AMD's Anthropic deal and today's Asian rally is that AI infrastructure capex is broadening across suppliers rather than concentrating or slowing, the same structural conclusion carried in recent reports, now reinforced from a different geography.

## What this means for Orwell

Today confirms the AI infrastructure buildout is broadening rather than slowing: AMD landing a frontier lab as both a GPU customer and an equity investment target, alongside Samsung and SK Hynix rallying on the same thesis, is a second and third data point behind Micron's numbers this week. The more interesting tension is that Alphabet's capex guide shows the market is now genuinely nervous about how much is being spent versus what it's returning, even while shrugging that nervousness off within twelve hours. Nothing here changes the GPU compute venture's thesis, but it strengthens the case for tracking capacity and pricing trends rather than headline capex numbers alone. On the oil side, today's Houthi tanker strikes turn the Red Sea from a rhetorical threat into an active second chokepoint alongside Hormuz, which matters more for the [[Comparison Network]]'s cost assumptions than any single day's price level: two live disruption threads is a structurally different risk picture from one.
