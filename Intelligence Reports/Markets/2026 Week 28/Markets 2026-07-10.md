## Story 1: SK Hynix's record Nasdaq ADR debut resolves the open loop
**Source:** CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/09/meet-sk-hynix-the-trillion-dollar-chipmaker-debuting-on-us-markets-.html and GlobeNewswire | https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/07/08/3324503/0/en/SK-Hynix-hits-Nasdaq-July-10-SKUU-and-SKDD-are-expected-to-launch-on-July-13.html
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

SK Hynix priced its ADS at $149 and began when-issued trading today under ticker SKHYV, ahead of full SKHY trading from Monday 13 July. The raise lands around $28-29bn, the largest ADR listing in history, bigger than Alibaba's 2014 Nasdaq debut. Institutional demand reportedly ran more than 7x the available shares.

This is the world's number two memory maker and the primary HBM3E supplier feeding Nvidia's AI accelerator stack, listing in the US at the peak of a memory supercycle. The debut lands squarely inside the chip-sector rotation that has hammered Kospi and Taiwan names over the past fortnight (SK Hynix and Samsung between them shed roughly $290bn of value at the worst of it in late June), so the float is a real-time test of whether US investors will pay a premium for HBM exposure that Korean markets have already started to mark down.

For the GPU compute venture: this is the clearest signal yet that memory, not just logic, is now a first-class AI infrastructure trade with its own US-listed vehicle. Watch SKHY's first full session Monday for whether it holds above the $149 print or gets marked down to Korea's discounted read.

## Story 2: Delta kicks off Q2 earnings season, travel demand the tell
**Source:** Alphastreet | https://news.alphastreet.com/delta-air-lines-q2-2026-earnings-preview-july-10-street-expects-1-48-eps/amp/
**Related vault notes:** [[Priorities]]

Delta reported before the bell today, the first major read on consumer and corporate travel demand for Q2. Street consensus sat at $1.48 EPS on $18.78bn revenue, though estimates had been cut sharply over the prior three months (down from $2.01 to as low as $1.43), flagging cost inflation and pricing pressure rather than a demand collapse. Delta has beaten in each of the last four quarters.

Not an AI story directly, but it is the macro backdrop for the options/futures book: airlines are a clean read on whether US consumer spend is actually softening or whether estimate cuts were just conservative positioning ahead of print. A beat here supports the "soft landing" case the Fed's hawkish June dot plot is fighting against; a miss adds weight to rate-cut pricing.

## Story 3: Bernanke joins Anthropic's oversight trust (first flagged today, dated 9 July)
**Source:** Bloomberg | https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-09/former-fed-chairman-ben-bernanke-joins-anthropic-oversight-trust
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Former Fed chair Ben Bernanke has joined Anthropic's Long-Term Benefit Trust, the independent oversight body created in 2023 to keep the company accountable to its public-benefit mission. He's the trust's fourth member, holds no equity, and is being brought in specifically to advise on how frontier AI could affect jobs, markets and financial stability.

This is a one-day-old story not in yesterday's open loops, surfaced here for the first time because of the timing: it lands three weeks after Anthropic's $965bn Series H and amid an active IPO filing, and a former Fed chair joining a governance trust reads as a deliberate signal to regulators and prospective public-market investors ahead of any listing. Worth tracking whether other high-credibility appointments follow as Anthropic and OpenAI both build the governance scaffolding public markets will expect.

## Story 4: Enterprise software "SaaSpocalypse" deepens, Palantir down 40% YTD
**Source:** The Motley Fool | https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/07/09/why-palantir-stock-plunged-34-in-the-first-half-of/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Palantir fell a further 4% on Thursday (9 July), part of a broader repricing across richly-valued enterprise software and cybersecurity names that traders have nicknamed the "SaaSpocalypse". The stock is down roughly 40% year to date despite Q1 revenue up 85% year-on-year to $1.63bn; the selloff is being driven by fear that AI agents erode the seat-based SaaS subscription model these companies are priced on, not by fundamentals.

Relevant to the options book: this is a valuation-multiple story, not a growth story, and it is the mirror image of the SK Hynix and memory-chip re-rating above. Capital is rotating out of "AI-adjacent software" and into "AI-enabling hardware", which is a clean pairs-trade thesis if it keeps running through Q2 earnings season.
