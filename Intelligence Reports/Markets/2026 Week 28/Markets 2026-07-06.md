## Story 1: SK Hynix opens Nasdaq ADR book-building today, targeting a record ~$29.5bn listing

**Source:** CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/24/sk-hynix-nasdaq-adr-listing-south-korea.html | Fortune | https://fortune.com/2026/07/05/sk-hynix-us-stock-listing-nasdaq-29-billion-micron-ai-boom-chips/ | Seoul Economic Daily | https://en.sedaily.com/markets/2026/07/04/sk-hynix-adr-listing-nears-underwriter-fees-seen
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

SK Hynix, the world's second-largest memory chipmaker, opens ADR book-building today, 6 July, with final pricing set for 9 July and Nasdaq debut (ticker SKHY) targeted for Friday 10 July, subject to SEC approval. The company is offering up to 17.79 million new shares, roughly 2.5% of shares outstanding, at a maximum offering size of $29.47bn — a deal Bloomberg has compared to Aramco's $29.4bn 2019 IPO in scale, and which would eclipse Alibaba's $21.8bn 2014 New York debut as the largest ADR listing on record. Underwriters (Bank of America, Citigroup, Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan) are reportedly being offered a fee of around 0.5% of proceeds, low by Wall Street norms but still approaching 200 billion won given the deal's size. Proceeds are earmarked for memory-capacity expansion, including a new Yongin fab and advanced packaging facilities.

This is the second half of the Korea semiconductor catalyst pair this report has been tracking since Friday's index rebound (Kospi +5.8%, Samsung +8.2%, SK Hynix +10.9% on 3 July). The ADR listing is a direct bet by SK Hynix that Wall Street will assign it an AI-cycle valuation premium closer to Nvidia or Broadcom multiples than its Seoul listing currently reflects — a thesis that will be tested in real time this week as book-building runs alongside Samsung's preliminary Q2 print (due tomorrow, consensus ~85.5 trillion won operating profit, an 18-fold year-on-year jump) and Micron's already-reported record Q3 (fiscal March-May) operating profit of $33.3bn. For the GPU compute venture, the relevant read is that AI-cycle memory economics are now strong enough to justify a near-Aramco-scale US listing mid-cycle rather than at a traditional IPO stage — a signal of how much capital the market believes still wants exposure to AI infrastructure hardware, not just the compute layer Nvidia dominates.

**Related vault notes:** none directly — logged for macro/CBUAE tracking

The Federal Reserve releases minutes from the 17 June FOMC meeting on Wednesday 8 July — the first meeting chaired by new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh. At the ECB's Sintra forum on 1 July, Warsh declined to hint at near-term policy but said inflation remains "too high," a notably hawkish framing for a chair some market participants had hoped might lean dovish. Context still holding from prior reports: nine of the Fed's own officials pencilled in either a hike or a hold for this year in June's projections, with only one dissenting toward a cut, meaning Warsh does not have the votes for a cut even if he wanted one. The Fed holds at 3.50-3.75% until the next meeting (28-29 July); CBUAE, pegged to the dollar, holds at 3.65% with no independent move expected before then. Nothing new today, but Wednesday's minutes are the week's real domestic-policy catalyst and worth flagging now so it doesn't arrive as a surprise.

## Story 3: Bitcoin ETF flows turn positive, ending a 10-day, $2.7bn outflow streak

**Source:** Yahoo Finance/99Bitcoins aggregation of Farside/SoSoValue ETF flow data | https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/crypto/articles/bitcoin-etf-news-inflows-hit-105959495.html
**Related vault notes:** none directly — logged for macro/crypto sweep

US spot Bitcoin ETFs took in $221m on Friday 3 July, their largest single-day inflow in roughly two months and the first positive day after a 10-consecutive-session outflow streak that had drained $2.73bn from the funds. Fidelity's FBTC accounted for around three-quarters of the inflow. This coincided with Bitcoin's rebound off a 21-month low below $58,000 in late June back toward the low-$60,000s. The caveat that matters: year-to-date net outflows across all US spot Bitcoin ETF products still sit at roughly $5.4bn, so Friday's reversal is a stabilisation signal, not confirmation that the broader 2026 de-risking out of Bitcoin ETFs (which started the year above $93,000) has ended. Worth a watch item rather than a call either way; the next real test is whether inflows hold through this week's Fed minutes.

## Story 4: Chip-stock sector context — mixed signals persist under the AI-cycle narrative

**Source:** General search sweep (CNBC, Kavout, Motley Fool sector coverage); dates as noted
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

No single dated catalyst today, but the standing sector picture is worth restating cleanly before Wednesday's Fed minutes and Samsung's print collide with it. Micron posted record fiscal Q3 results (operating profit $33.3bn, a 15.4x year-on-year jump) and issued a strong Q4 forecast that briefly pushed its market cap past Meta's. Broadcom's Q3 AI-chip sales guidance of $16bn came in below the $17.2bn analyst estimate, and notably the company did not raise its full-year 2026 AI semiconductor forecast — a soft data point sitting awkwardly next to Micron's blowout, and the kind of divergence within the same sector that matters more for a compute-leasing thesis than any single headline number. Nvidia continues expanding its footprint (35 new European HPC systems announced, a Vera Rubin platform push for scientific computing) while projecting roughly $1 trillion in AI infrastructure demand through 2027. Read together: demand commentary from suppliers remains uniformly bullish, but actual guidance discipline (Broadcom holding its number rather than raising it) is the tell to watch for whether the AI capex cycle is plateauing at the margin.
