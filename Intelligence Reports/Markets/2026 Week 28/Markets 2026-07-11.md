## Story 1: Hormuz shipping "grinds to a halt" even as oil holds steady and Asian equities rally

**Source:** Al Jazeera | https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/10/strait-of-hormuz-shipping-grinds-to-halt-as-us-iran-resume-fighting | Washington Post | https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2026/07/10/stocks-market-iran-war-ai-oil/7c5d264a-7c2d-11f1-b194-f872dd4ec5aa_story.html
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Comparison Network]]

The physical picture in the Strait deteriorated sharply on Friday: only 5 vessels crossed Wednesday-to-Thursday morning versus 45 on Monday, against a normal daily flow of roughly 130. Zero large vessels (10,000+ deadweight tonnes) have used the US-coordinated, Oman-hugging "Southern Highway" route since 7 July — Lloyd's List Intelligence's own language is that traffic has "effectively ground to a halt." Brent nonetheless held flat at $76.58/bbl at Friday's 05:00 GMT reading, up roughly $4/bbl week-on-week but not spiking further, while Asian equities actually rallied Friday (Nikkei +1.8%, Kospi over +5%, Hang Seng +1.9%) on a read that the shipping collapse hasn't yet translated into a sustained price shock.

That gap between the physical disruption and the price/equity reaction is the real story for the options book: either the market is under-pricing a genuine chokepoint failure, or traders are betting the Islamabad talks (see today's Geopolitics report) resolve this before it bites. Analysts cited in the Al Jazeera piece flag Brent could still move $10-15/bbl higher into summer as inventories draw down, with diesel under separate pressure from Middle East refinery outages and Ukrainian drone strikes on Russian refineries. Worth a weekend note purely because Monday's open is the first chance markets get to reprice a full week of vessel-traffic data that's actually worse than the price action currently reflects.

## Story 2: Chip stocks rally hard on Friday — Micron, Broadcom, AMD, Nvidia all up

**Source:** Motley Fool | https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/07/10/should-you-buy-micron-stock-under-1000/ | Yahoo Finance | https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/amd-jumps-7-intel-climbs-140448273.html
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Micron rose 8% Friday after raising its planned US manufacturing and technology investment to more than $250bn through 2035, up from a prior $200bn commitment. Broadcom added 4% on news of an Apple chip-supply partnership expected to exceed $30bn, covering more than 15bn US-made chips. AMD jumped 6% and Nvidia 4% as part of a broad semiconductor and optical-networking rebound following the prior week's DeepSeek-chip-driven selloff.

Direct read for the GPU compute venture: this is US fab onshoring (Micron) and hyperscaler chip-supply diversification (Broadcom/Apple) both getting bid up on the same day the Gulf shipping picture worsened, which reads as capital treating hardware capex commitments as insulated from Middle East risk. Worth tracking whether that decoupling holds if Hormuz deteriorates further.

## Story 3: Anthropic's secondary-market valuation hits $1.2 trillion, pulls ahead of OpenAI

**Source:** Yahoo Finance | https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/anthropic-secondary-market-valuation-hits-114416290.html | Quartz | https://qz.com/anthropic-secondary-market-valuation-1-2-trillion-070926
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Shares of Anthropic trading on secondary platform Caplight now imply a $1.2 trillion valuation, up 550% year-on-year and well above the $965bn Series H that closed in late May. OpenAI's own secondary-implied valuation sits around $908bn by comparison. Caplight's CEO describes near-zero willing sellers, meaning the price is being bid up by scarcity of supply rather than new product or revenue news.

This sits on top of Wednesday's Bernanke-joins-the-oversight-trust story: the valuation gap to OpenAI is widening at the same time Anthropic is visibly building IPO-adjacent governance credibility. Nothing actionable today, but the trend line is now two data points deep in the same direction.

## Story 4: SambaNova closes first tranche of $1bn Series F at $11bn valuation

**Source:** General Atlantic | https://www.generalatlantic.com/media-article/sambanova-completes-first-close-of-1-billion-financing-at-11-billion-valuation/ | Crunchbase News | https://news.crunchbase.com/ai/biggest-funding-rounds-billion-dollar-cyber-ai-keyfactor-sambanova/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

SambaNova, an AI chip and inference-infrastructure company, closed the first tranche of a $1bn Series F at an $11bn valuation, led by General Atlantic with BlackRock, Capital Group, Intel Capital, Qatar Investment Authority, T. Rowe Price and Vista Equity Partners all participating. The company has now raised roughly $2.5bn to date. Separately, Keyfactor (digital and machine identity management, not pure AI but riding the same AI-infrastructure-security wave) closed a $1bn private-equity round led by Summit Partners.

SambaNova is a direct Nvidia-alternative compute play and the cleanest comp available for the GPU compute venture's competitive landscape — an $11bn valuation with Qatar Investment Authority and BlackRock both in the cap table is a useful benchmark for how institutional capital is currently pricing non-Nvidia inference infrastructure.
