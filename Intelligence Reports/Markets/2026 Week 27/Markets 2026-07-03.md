## Story 1: US Markets Closed for Independence Day — Reading Thursday's Session as the Live Data Point

**Source:** Yahoo Finance | https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-thursday-july-2-223136955.html | CNBC (web search)
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

NYSE and Nasdaq are shut today and reopen Monday, 6 July — not a footnote, but the reason there is no fresh Nvidia/Micron/Broadcom/AMD print to lead with. The relevant data point is Thursday's close. The Dow rose about 1.1% (nearly 600 points) to a fresh record, but the Nasdaq fell roughly 0.8%, continuing Wednesday's chip-led slide. The split is the story: mega-cap industrials and non-tech names caught a bid while AI-infrastructure names kept bleeding. Intel fell 5.25% to $120.35, Nvidia slipped 1.39% to $194.83, and AMD and Micron "continued to get hit" per Yahoo's live coverage, without a clean percentage in the source. Tesla dropped 7% despite beating Q2 delivery estimates — a reminder that good fundamentals are not currently buying anyone protection in this tape.

The macro trigger was a cooler-than-expected June jobs report: nonfarm payrolls added only 57,000 against a 113,000 estimate, with unemployment at 4.2% versus a 4.3% forecast. That is a soft print, not a bad one, and markets read it as supportive of the Fed's continued hold rather than as recession signal — consistent with new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's stated preference for letting data, not guidance, do the talking.

With no trading today, the meaningful question heading into Monday is whether Thursday's Dow/Nasdaq divergence was a one-day rotation into "safe" mega-caps or the start of a longer unwind in AI-infrastructure names. That is exactly the question the GPU compute venture thesis needs answered, and it will not get an answer until the market reopens.

## Story 2: Seoul's Chip Stocks — Thursday's Rout, Friday's Bargain-Hunting Rebound

**Source:** CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/02/samsung-sk-hynix-shares-slide-kospi-tech-selloff-nasdaq.html | Korea JoongAng Daily | https://www.koreajoongangdaily.com/business/seoul-stocks-open-12-higher-despite-overnight-weakness-in-us-tech-stocks/12753725 | Etoday (Korean financial press)
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

This is today's genuinely live story, because Korea trades on its own calendar regardless of the US holiday. Thursday was brutal: the Kospi plunged 7.89% to 7,648.09 — locally dubbed the "Meta Shock" — as Samsung Electronics fell 9.06% to 286,000 won and SK Hynix dropped 14.57% to 2,187,000 won. The proximate cause was Meta's cloud-business reveal (see Story 3) rattling assumptions about AI compute scarcity, compounded by the overnight Nasdaq slide. Because Samsung and SK Hynix now make up roughly half the Kospi's total weight (up from about a quarter a year ago), their moves function as a referendum on the whole memory-chip AI trade.

Today (Friday), Seoul opened around 1.2% higher on bargain-hunting, trimming to about +0.7% (7,701.53) by mid-morning local time as investors bought the dip in both names. The flow detail matters more than the headline bounce: foreign investors have net-sold roughly 2.55 trillion won of Samsung and 2.4 trillion won of SK Hynix over the past two sessions, while Korean retail investors bought roughly 3.67 trillion won of Samsung and 4.56 trillion won of SK Hynix — a classic "smart money out, retail in" divergence that tends to precede more volatility, not less. Two scheduled catalysts sit directly ahead: Samsung reports Q2 preliminary results on 7 July, and SK Hynix lists ADRs in the US on 10 July ahead of its own earnings on 29 July. Either could re-trigger the move in either direction.

For the GPU compute venture, this is the clearest real-time gauge available of how fragile AI-hardware sentiment has become — a single US cloud-strategy announcement wiped out nearly 8% of Korea's benchmark index in one session.

## Story 3: Anthropic in Talks With Samsung for a Custom AI Chip — Still Circulating, No New Detail Today

**Source:** The Information (via TechCrunch) | https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/02/anthropic-is-discussing-a-new-custom-chip-with-samsung/ | Bloomberg (via syndication)
**Related vault notes:** [[Agent Org]], [[Orwell Corp Stack Decisions]], [[Ventures]]

This broke Thursday (2 July) via The Information and is still being carried today by secondary outlets, but with no material new detail — treating it as an update rather than a fresh story. Anthropic has held early-stage talks with Samsung about manufacturing a custom AI chip, reportedly eyeing Samsung's 2nm process and advanced packaging. Nothing is decided: Anthropic hasn't settled what the chip would do, how it fits into a server, or how powerful it would be, and the company has explicitly said AWS Trainium, Google TPUs and Nvidia GPUs remain central to its compute strategy regardless. The company has also reportedly hired Clive Chan, an early member of OpenAI's own custom-chip team — mirroring OpenAI's 2024 move to design silicon with Broadcom, which shipped its first product ("Jalapeño") last month.

The pattern across the frontier labs is now unmistakable: every major AI lab is hedging its Nvidia dependence with a custom-silicon side bet, without abandoning the incumbent supply chain. That is a multi-year hardware story, not a today's-news trading catalyst, but it is directly relevant to Orwell's own Claude API cost base over time — cheaper proprietary inference capacity for Anthropic is a plausible long-run cost lever, even if nothing changes this quarter.
