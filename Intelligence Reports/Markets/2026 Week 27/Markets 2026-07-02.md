## Story 1: Micron's Post-Earnings Slide Deepens — Record Results, Sell-the-News Reaction

**Source:** TradingKey | https://www.tradingkey.com/news/market-movers/262004405-market-movers-mu-20260701
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Micron is now down 12–18% from its 52-week high, a slide that started after its record FQ3 2026 results and has continued into today's session. The results themselves were strong — record revenue, HBM capacity reportedly sold out — but three things are weighing on the stock: rival SK Hynix's potential US listing raising competitive concerns, heavy call-selling in the options market, and a growing analyst chorus questioning whether the AI memory upcycle is peaking rather than accelerating. Micron's board declared a routine $0.15/share quarterly dividend (payable 21 July, record date 6 July) and signed a new semiconductor supply deal with GM for future vehicles — neither materially offsets the sentiment shift.

This is a genuine "priced for perfection, then bad news isn't required to sell off" pattern. It doesn't invalidate the compute/memory supercycle thesis flagged in prior reports, but it's the first real crack in what had been an uninterrupted YTD run.

## Story 2: Broadcom Up on the Day, Still Well Below June Highs — AI Revenue Guidance Unchanged

**Source:** TradersUnion | https://tradersunion.com/news/financial-news/show/2521831-broadcom-rises-2-27percent-today-to/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

AVGO is up 2.27% ($8.28) today, but still down more than 20% from its June high. The company's Q2 revenue hit a record $22.2bn, and management maintained guidance for $100bn in AI revenue by 2027 — the headline number the market has been trading on. The joint Broadcom-OpenAI inference chip project ("Jalapeño") remains live, alongside the $35bn AI infrastructure financing platform with Apollo and Blackstone targeting 20+ gigawatts of AI compute capacity by 2028 — both multi-year stories, not today's news, but the backdrop against which today's guidance miss is being read.

## Story 5: Oil Slides Sharply — Brent to $71.19, Extending a ~29% Monthly Drop

**Source:** Investing.com / Al Jazeera (via web search) | https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/6/29/oil-prices-rise-as-us-iranian-strikes-threaten-strait-of-hormuz-reopening
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Comparison Network]]

Brent fell to an intraday low of $71.19 today, down roughly 2.3% on the day and down about 29% for the month — its steepest monthly drop since 2020. WTI weakened in step (prints today ranged $67.78–70.11 across sources, itself a sign of a fast-moving, thin market rather than a single clean number). The driver: Hormuz shipping traffic continuing to recover plus rising Iranian and Russian export volumes reaching seaborne inventories, per Iran's own reported export ramp of roughly 1.66m bpd in June. Iran has separately agreed not to impose Hormuz transit fees for 60 days under the interim deal.

**Cross-reference:** today's Geopolitics report has the fuller picture — the price is falling faster than the underlying shipping-security picture is actually improving (insurers are still tagging the Strait a war-risk zone through 9 July, and actual vessel crossings remain thin). Read this oil move as the market betting on durability, not confirmation that the corridor is secure.

## Story 7: IPO Watch — Anthropic's ARR Climbing Toward $50bn, OpenAI Reportedly Wavering Toward 2027

**Source:** Web search aggregation (Reuters, Zacks, ThinkMarkets)
**Related vault notes:** [[Agent Org]], [[Orwell Corp Stack Decisions]]

No new filing today, but the state of play as of this week: Anthropic's confidential S-1 (filed 1 June) shows ARR of roughly $47bn at filing time, with an internal target of $50bn+ by end of July. That's against the $965bn post-money valuation from its $65bn Series H. Prediction markets currently put roughly 76% odds on an Anthropic IPO closing by 31 December 2026. OpenAI, by contrast, is reportedly leaning toward pushing its own IPO into 2027 (per late-June Reuters reporting) — it remains at a $852bn prior valuation while seeking a further raise near $830bn-$1tn, with internal projections reportedly showing $14bn in 2026 losses and no profitability expected until around 2030.

**No change in guidance from prior reports** — still watching for a confirmed public filing date on either company.
