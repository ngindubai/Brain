## Story 1: TSMC reports Q2 today — the AI capex health check the whole sector is waiting on

**Source:** [TradingKey](https://www.tradingkey.com/analysis/stocks/us-stocks/262031904-tsmc-tsm-stock-forecast-july-15-2026-june-revenue-q2-earnings-tradingkey), [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320142/20260711/tsmc-q2-earnings-july-16-three-cowos-signals-that-test-ais-spending-ceiling.htm), [SEC EDGAR 6-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1046179/000104617926000199/a1q26e_withguidancexfinal.htm)
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Priorities]]

TSMC's Q2 2026 results land today at 2pm Taiwan time, with consensus at $39.5-40.2 billion revenue and $3.83 EPS (+55% YoY), gross margin guided 65.5-67.5%. June revenue alone came in at NT$442.68 billion, up 67.9% year-on-year, pulling H1 revenue to NT$2.4 trillion (+35.6% YoY) — momentum strong enough that prediction markets are pricing a 93% probability of an EPS beat. TSMC is essentially sold out on N3, the process node underpinning nearly every AI GPU shipping this year. No clean confirmation of the actual printed numbers was retrievable via search at time of writing (the report drops after this brief is filed) — flag for tomorrow's same-day follow-up. This is the one number that resolves whether the last week of chip-stock volatility is a valuation correction sitting on top of genuine demand, or the first sign demand itself is softening.

## Story 2: Asia chip selloff deepens — SK Hynix -11%, Samsung -7%, rout spreads to equipment makers

**Source:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/16/sk-hynix-falls-amid-asia-tech-rout-tracking-us-semiconductor-losses.html), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/global-semiconductor-stocks-slide-record-104819847.html)
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Asian semiconductor stocks fell hard again today as the US chip selloff spread through the region: SK Hynix -11% in Seoul, Samsung Electronics -7%, Seoul Semiconductor -5%, LG Innotek -1%, Samsung SDI -2%. In Japan, Advantest fell more than 6%, SoftBank Group nearly 7%, Tokyo Electron over 5%, Renesas 4%. SK Hynix has been unusually volatile since its US listing last week; a trader cited in coverage flagged that semiconductors now make up roughly 20% of the S&P 500 — "incredibly difficult to sustain" as a concentration level. This is a valuation unwind, not (yet) a demand signal — worth reading against tonight's TSMC print rather than in isolation.

## Story 3: CXMT prices Shanghai IPO today — largest Chinese chip listing ever, $85bn valuation

**Source:** [South China Morning Post](https://www.scmp.com/tech/article/3360615/china-memory-giant-cxmt-valued-us85-billion-record-shanghai-ipo), [Nikkei Asia](https://asia.nikkei.com/business/tech/semiconductors/cxmt-to-raise-8.5bn-in-largest-chinese-chip-ipo)
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

ChangXin Memory Technologies priced its Shanghai Star Market IPO today at 8.66 yuan (~$1.28) per share, raising 57.9 billion yuan (~$8.5 billion) and implying an $85.2 billion valuation — the largest A-share IPO by a Chinese chip company on record, surpassing SMIC's 2020 Shanghai listing. CXMT makes DRAM, the memory type powering both laptops and AI server racks. The geopolitical undertow is real: the Pentagon added CXMT to its "Chinese military companies" list last month, and Reuters has reported a US interagency committee has cleared the firm for Commerce's Entity List. China is capitalising its domestic memory champion at record scale in the same window Washington is moving to blacklist it — a direct read on how contested the memory-chip supply chain has become, relevant to any GPU compute sourcing that touches Chinese-made components.

## Story 4: Hormuz war re-escalation pushes oil back above $80 — full detail in Geopolitics

**Source:** [Al Jazeera live coverage](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/7/16/iran-war-live-sirens-in-bahrain-kuwait-us-disables-iranian-oil-tanker), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/07/15/nx-s1-5894582/us-iran-updates)
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Comparison Network]]

Iran declared its ceasefire memorandum with Washington voided today, calling itself in an "existential war" with the US after the American naval blockade of Iranian ports was reinstated and fresh strikes hit Qeshm Island, Bandar Abbas and Chabahar; the US Navy separately disabled an oil tanker attempting to reach an Iranian port. WTI is trading above $80/barrel, up more than 11% over the past three sessions. This is a structural repricing of the Hormuz risk premium, not a headline spike — see today's [[Geopolitics]] brief for the full military and diplomatic picture, including Trump's one-week ultimatum on Iranian infrastructure. The market-relevant point: a functioning war footing over the strait that supplies roughly a fifth of global oil flow is now the base case for pricing, not a tail risk, with obvious knock-on for UAE mortgage and property costs via the risk premium.

## Story 5: EU cans the Russian oil price cap decision for another week — Greece blocks on LNG

**Source:** [Reuters via Kyiv Independent reporting pattern](https://kyivindependent.com/), [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/07/10/eu-countries-rush-to-avoid-disastrous-revision-of-russian-oil-price-cap)
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

EU ambassadors agreed yesterday to freeze the Russian oil price cap at $44.10/barrel for one more week rather than let it auto-revert toward $58 today as scheduled, buying time to resolve Greece's objection to a proposed ban on EU countries trading LNG to third countries — the one holdout blocking the full 21st sanctions package. This resolves yesterday's open loop for now: no reversion today, but the mechanism is only paused to 23 July, not resolved, and remains a live upward risk on global oil pricing stacked directly on top of the Hormuz premium in Story 4.
