## Story 1: Warsh's Senate Banking debut — inflation-first, no forward guidance, AI buildout flagged as the swing factor

**Source:** PBS NewsHour | https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/watch-live-kevin-warsh-testifies-in-senate-banking-confirmation-hearing-for-fed-chair ; CNN | https://us.cnn.com/2026/07/14/economy/takeaways-kevin-warsh-congressional-testimony ; Senate Banking Committee | https://www.banking.senate.gov/hearings/07/08/2026/the-semiannual-monetary-policy-report-to-the-congress
**Related vault notes:** [[Priorities]], [[Ventures]]

Warsh appeared before the Senate Banking Committee at 10am ET today in Dirksen 538 — the second half of his back-to-back congressional debut after yesterday's House hearing. He held the line lawmakers should expect from here: no forward guidance, inflation-back-to-2%-target as the "main thrust," and a pointed line on AI investment — "what is now called 'AI investment' will soon be called just 'investment'" — while conceding the Fed still can't quantify how much the economy will actually benefit from the AI buildout. Markets were watching specifically for a September-hike signal given June's minutes showed a minority already arguing a hike was warranted; nothing in today's testimony closed that door, but nothing opened it either.

This closes out one of two live threads from yesterday's brief. Combined with the CPI print below, the rate path is now genuinely two-sided for the first time this year — cooler inflation data pulling toward "no need to hike," a still-live Hormuz oil shock pushing the other way. For Orwell, that's the signal to track for GPU compute financing costs rather than either data point alone.

## Story 3: Bank earnings reconciled — Goldman's record trading quarter, Morgan Stanley closes the sequence today

**Source:** Yahoo Finance (Morgan Stanley) | https://finance.yahoo.com/news/morgan-stanley-beats-q2-earnings-135800859.html ; StockTitan (Bank of America) | https://www.stocktitan.net/news/BAC/bank-of-america-reports-second-quarter-2026-financial-zpfm1v4kvurg.html ; Invezz (Goldman Sachs) | https://invezz.com/news/2026/07/14/goldman-sachs-stock-soars-after-massive-earnings-beat-shocks-wall-street/
**Related vault notes:** [[Priorities]]

Correction to yesterday's schedule: Bank of America and Goldman Sachs actually reported alongside JPMorgan on Tuesday 14 July, not today as the prior brief's earnings calendar expected. BofA posted Q2 net income of $9.1 billion (+27% YoY), EPS of $1.21 against a $1.13 estimate, revenue of $31.6 billion (+15%) and net interest income up 9% to $16 billion — CEO Brian Moynihan called it "one of our strongest quarters to date." Goldman Sachs was the outlier of the whole bank-earnings run: EPS of $20.98 against a $14.40 estimate, net revenue of $20.34 billion against $16.13 billion expected (+39% YoY), and a record $7.42 billion in combined equities and FICC trading revenue — the stock's best single day in a year.

Morgan Stanley closed the sequence out today: net revenues of $16.79 billion against a $15.92 billion estimate (+12% YoY), Investment Management pre-tax income of $323 million (+45% YoY, well past the $217.2 million estimate), a reauthorized $20 billion multi-year buyback following its 2025 stress test, and an 8% dividend increase to $1.00/share payable 15 August. Set against JPMorgan's already-confirmed beat, this was a genuinely strong quarter across the whole US banking sector — trading desks, not lending margins, did the heavy lifting, which tracks with a volatile macro backdrop (Hormuz, rate uncertainty) generating exactly the kind of client activity trading floors profit from.

## Story 5: Kuwait hit by fresh Iranian drone wave overnight — Hormuz oil floor stays elevated

**Source:** Anadolu Agency | https://www.aa.com.tr/en/middle-east/kuwait-says-its-air-defenses-responding-to-hostile-drone-attacks/3998420 ; CNBC (oil) | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/oil-prices-today-brent-wti-hormuz-trump-toll-iran.html
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Comparison Network]]

Kuwait's military said early Wednesday its air defences were responding to "hostile" drone attacks it attributed to Iran, with explosions heard nationwide from interceptions and a fire contained at one struck site; Bahrain activated air-raid sirens in parallel. No injuries reported in Kuwait this time. This lands on top of, not instead of, Tuesday's confirmed physical strikes on two UAE-flagged/operated ADNOC L&S tankers (Al Bahyah and Mombasa) in the strait itself, which killed one Indian crew member and injured eight — a harder, more locally-relevant data point than the generic "blockade takes effect" framing. Brent settled Tuesday at $84.73 (+1.72%), WTI at $79.34 (+1.5%); full details on the toll-collection mechanics and the day's regional framing are in today's [[Geopolitics]] brief, but the market read here is straightforward — a naval blockade with a functioning Iranian toll system and now a widening drone-attack radius (Kuwait, Bahrain, UAE tankers) is a structural floor under oil prices, not a fading headline spike.

## Story 6: Nvidia H200 exports to China confirmed "trivial" despite cleared licences

**Source:** ChinaTechNews.com | https://www.chinatechnews.com/2026/07/15/125588-us-says-nvidias-h200-exports-to-china-remain-trivial-despite-approvals ; CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/nvidia-h200-ai-chips-china.html
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

US Commerce official Jeffrey Kessler confirmed today that only a "trivial" number of H200 chips have actually shipped to China despite licences being cleared for roughly ten Chinese firms — including a ZTE Kangxun Telecom unit, Maginfra and a Kingsoft Cloud subsidiary, each capped at 75,000 chips. This is the cleanest data point yet that the headline "licences approved" story from earlier this year is not translating into real throughput — the licensing regime exists but is being run deliberately tight. For a GPU compute venture, this is the more reliable signal on actual China AI-chip supply than the approval headlines themselves: the bottleneck is real and current, not a paperwork formality working itself out.

But it's sitting directly on top of a Hormuz risk floor that widened rather than narrowed: a fresh Kuwait drone attack, confirmed casualties on UAE-flagged tankers, and an EU sanctions deadline that could push the Russian oil price cap up by 15 July, all point the same direction on energy costs. Tomorrow's TSMC print, alongside today's ASML numbers once confirmed, will be the cleaner read on whether the AI capex story is decelerating or just repricing.
