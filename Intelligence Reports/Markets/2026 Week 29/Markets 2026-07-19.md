## Story 1: Claude Fable 5's free-access window closes tonight — the third extension ends and pay-per-use pricing begins

**Source:** [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/artificial-intelligence/claude-fable-5-stays-free-for-paid-users-until-july-19-as-anthropic-buys-more-time/), [Dataconomy](https://dataconomy.com/2026/07/13/claude-fable-5-free-access-extended-july-19/), [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320528/20260715/claude-fable-5-free-window-ends-sunday-gpt-56-sol-closes-benchmark-gap.htm)
**Related vault notes:** [[Priorities]], [[Agent Org]]

Anthropic's promotional window giving Pro, Max, Team and select Enterprise subscribers free access to Claude Fable 5 — plus a 50% bump to Claude Code's weekly usage limits — expires at 11:59:59pm PT tonight. This is the third extension in five weeks (pushed from 7 July, to 12 July, to today), each one timed to a competing OpenAI release, most recently GPT-5.6 Sol. From tomorrow, usage beyond the standard subscription allocation on Fable 5 moves to prepaid credits at $10 per million input tokens and $50 per million output tokens; Free-tier users, standard Enterprise seats and API usage were never covered by the promotion and are unaffected. The direct relevance here isn't industry colour — Gareth runs Claude Code on Max 20x as the primary build tool across the whole Orwell portfolio, so the end of the extra 50% weekly headroom is worth watching over the next few days: if Fable 5 usage was running near the old ceiling, output may compress back toward standard Max 20x limits from tomorrow. Worth a look at actual usage patterns this week rather than assuming it won't bite.

Bitcoin is trading around $63,900–$64,250, roughly flat to slightly up on the day and off Friday's sell-off; Ethereum has stabilised near $1,850 after underperforming bitcoin through the week. The Crypto Fear & Greed Index ticked from 27 down to 25 through Friday into the weekend before flattening — still "extreme fear" territory but no longer deteriorating hour to hour. That it has stopped falling over the weekend — even as the Iran war took its most serious turn yet with the first US combat deaths since March (full detail in [[Geopolitics]]) — suggests the market has, for now, priced the chip-stock rotation and isn't yet pricing a wider war as an incremental new shock. That could change fast; it hasn't yet.

## Story 3: Oil hits one-month highs as the Iran war widens to direct US casualties

**Source:** [tradingeconomics.com](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/brent-crude-oil), [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/live-updates/iran-war-trump-strait-of-hormuz-attacks-persian-gulf/)
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]], [[Ventures]]

Brent crude is trading around $88 a barrel, up roughly 4.5% on the session and off a prior close of $84.23, after news broke that two US service members were killed and a third went missing in an Iranian ballistic missile strike on Jordan's Muwaffaq Salti air base — the first American combat deaths in this war since March. WTI is tracking just above $82. This is a genuine repricing of war-risk premium rather than a single-headline spike: the move sits on top of a week that had already carried Brent up more than 14% on Hormuz-blockade fears, and it comes with the US now on an eighth consecutive night of strikes and Iran reportedly suspending its ceasefire commitments outright. The full geopolitical picture — the Jordan attack, the widening strikes on Gulf allies, and the Bab el-Mandeb threat as a second chokepoint — is in today's [[Geopolitics]] brief; the market read here is straightforward: nothing about this move looks like a fading spike, and it is the one line item most directly exposed to UAE mortgage and property costs through the comparison network.
