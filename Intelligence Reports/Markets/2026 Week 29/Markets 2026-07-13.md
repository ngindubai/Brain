## Story 1: Asian chip and tech stocks fall at the Monday open; US futures softer, not routed

**Source:** CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/12/stock-market-today-live-updates.html and CNBC Asia markets coverage (via search)
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Comparison Network]]

The first real test flagged in yesterday's report has an answer, and it's a partial repricing rather than a full flight to safety. SK Hynix fell around 5% in Seoul trading Monday — notable because it had just surged roughly 13% on its Nasdaq debut — while Samsung Electronics dropped more than 6% as the broader Korean chip complex came under pressure. Japan's SoftBank, Tokyo Electron, Advantest and Renesas were all trading lower too. That's a genuine, dated-today move across the region's most AI-exposed names, driven by the weekend's renewed US-Iran airstrikes and Iran's redeclaration that the Strait of Hormuz is closed "until further notice."

US index futures told a calmer story overnight: Dow futures slipped about 135 points (-0.3%) and S&P 500 futures lost roughly 0.3%, a soft-landing open rather than the kind of gap-down that would signal panic. That gap between Asian severity and US restraint is itself the story — either US traders are pricing this as another round in a cycle that has de-escalated before, or the full reaction is still to show up once the cash session opens and digests the oil move described in Story 2. Full confirmed US cash-market closing data for today was not yet available at time of writing; the picture above reflects futures and the Asian session only.

The read for Orwell is narrow but real: the GPU compute venture's cost backdrop tracks these chip-name moves directly, and a sustained Asian semiconductor sell-off — even a modest one — is worth watching for whether it survives the week or reverses once TSMC's numbers land this afternoon and bank earnings start tomorrow. One session of Asian weakness is not yet a trend.

## Story 2: Brent pushes toward $79 as Hormuz standoff continues, CENTCOM disputes Iran's "closure" claim

**Source:** Bloomberg (via search summary) | https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-12/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-july-13 and CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/08/oil-prices-brent-wti-iran-us-hormuz.html
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]], [[Ventures]]

Brent rose toward $79 a barrel today after already gaining 5.4% last week, extending the run that started with Friday's $76.58 close. The move is a direct continuation of the weekend escalation covered in yesterday's report: Iran says the Strait is closed "until further notice," a claim US Central Command explicitly denies, stating its forces are conducting further strikes specifically to keep the waterway open for navigation. That dispute over whether the Strait is actually closed, rather than simply dangerous, is the crux of today's oil move — a confirmed closure would be a different order of event than an elevated-risk transit corridor.

Shipping through Hormuz — a route that carries roughly a fifth of the world's oil and gas trade — remains significantly disrupted regardless of whose framing is accurate, and that is what's keeping a risk premium baked into the price rather than a single dramatic spike. This is a slower, grinding repricing rather than the kind of single-session shock that would force an emergency response from OPEC+ or consuming-nation strategic reserves, at least based on today's information.

For the UAE comparison network this is the variable worth tracking most closely of anything in today's report: sustained Gulf risk premium in oil flows through to regional risk pricing, freight costs and — via the CBUAE's dollar peg — eventually to the rate backdrop that the mortgage and insurance comparison sites are sensitive to, even though CBUAE itself made no move today (see open loops table). No policy response from the UAE or Bahrain central banks was reported today beyond the air-defence activity already covered in yesterday's report and the Geopolitics brief.

## Story 3: TSMC's delayed June revenue report lands today, first real AI-demand data point of the week

**Source:** TSMC investor relations financial calendar | https://investor.tsmc.com/english/financial-calendar and search-aggregated coverage (Catacal, Bloomberg via search)
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Taiwan Semiconductor's June 2026 monthly revenue report was due today, 13 July, at 13:30 Taipei time — pushed back three days from its original 10 July slot because of a typhoon-related closure. This is a small operational detail but a genuinely dated-today event: it is the first hard AI-demand data point of the week, landing before Thursday's full Q2 earnings call and ahead of ASML's results on the 15th. May's print, for context, showed revenue of NT$416.9 billion, up 30% year on year, driven by AI chip orders.

The reason this matters more than a routine monthly filing is the timing collision with Story 1: if June's number confirms the same run-rate strength as May despite the Gulf backdrop, it is the cleanest available evidence that AI infrastructure capex is decoupling from geopolitical risk, echoing the pattern flagged in Friday's report. If it disappoints, it would compound today's Asian chip weakness rather than offset it. TSMC's full Q2 earnings conference is scheduled for Thursday the 16th, with 2026 capex guidance already running at $52-56 billion, weighted toward 2-nanometer ramp — a plan wholly dependent on ASML's EUV tools, whose own results land Wednesday.

Worth flagging as background rather than a today story: TSM and ASML have moved in lockstep roughly 74% of the time this cycle, so a weak TSMC print would be a leading indicator to watch into Wednesday's ASML release.

## Story 4: Bank earnings preview intensifies — options markets price outsized moves ahead of Tuesday's reports

**Source:** Benzinga | https://www.benzinga.com/markets/equities/26/07/60405656/bank-earnings-week-goldman-jpmorgan-citi-bofa-morgan-stanley-set-to-deliver-a-profit-bonanza and tickeron Q2 2026 earnings preview
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

New detail today sharpens what was a calendar note in yesterday's report into a genuine market-positioning story. Options desks are now pricing unusually large post-earnings moves for the five majors: Goldman Sachs 6.0%, Citigroup 5.5%, Wells Fargo 5.5%, Bank of America 4.5% and JPMorgan 4.4%. Those are big implied swings for money-centre banks and reflect exactly the thesis flagged Friday and Sunday — that Hormuz-driven volatility across energy, FX and rates markets this quarter should show up as blowout FICC trading revenue, and traders are positioning for a genuine surprise in either direction rather than an in-line print.

Consensus estimates back the bullish case: JPMorgan is expected around $5.44-5.61 EPS (roughly 10% year-on-year growth) on investment banking and trading strength, while Goldman Sachs consensus sits near $14.47, up more than 32% year on year, largely on trading and advisory activity. Reporting sequencing is now confirmed as JPMorgan, Citigroup, Wells Fargo and BlackRock before Tuesday's open, with Bank of America, Morgan Stanley and Goldman Sachs following Wednesday — a slight refinement on the "Tuesday/Wednesday" split flagged in earlier reports.

This is a two-day event that will do more to set the tone for the rest of July than anything happening in markets today. It sits directly upstream of the "is this actually a step-change or another cycle of escalate-then-fade" question from Story 1.

**Related vault notes:** none directly

Slightly more detail than yesterday's report carried: Warsh's first semiannual monetary policy testimony is confirmed as a two-day format — House Financial Services Committee on Tuesday 14 July at 10am ET, followed by the Senate Banking Committee on Wednesday 15 July. Markets are watching specifically for any signal on a September rate move. Combined with Story 4's earnings wall and Story 3's TSMC print, tomorrow is shaping up as the single most information-dense day of the month; today is essentially the calm positioning day ahead of it.
