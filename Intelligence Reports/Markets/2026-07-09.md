## Story 1: SK Hynix prices the largest-ever ADR offering by a foreign company, ahead of tomorrow's Nasdaq debut

**Source:** BigGo Finance | https://finance.biggo.com/news/467ea716-14e1-4c1d-8ac1-301a905bd145 | Yahoo Finance | https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/sk-hynix-launches-28-billion-115850372.html | CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/24/sk-hynix-nasdaq-adr-listing-south-korea.html
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

SK Hynix is pricing its Nasdaq American Depositary Receipt offering today, with trading set to open tomorrow, 10 July, under the ticker SKHY. The deal is roughly $28-29bn, structured as one Korean common share splitting into ten ADRs, with an indicative price of around ₩242,500 per ADR (roughly $158-165). At that size it is the largest ADR offering ever completed by a non-US company, ahead of both the Alibaba and Saudi Aramco US listing records. Bookbuilding opened Monday (6 July) with reported heavy demand from index-tracking passive funds, which some estimates put at up to $15bn of automatic inflows once SKHY is added to major benchmarks.

The timing is notable on two fronts. First, it lands the day after the FOMC minutes and in the middle of a live Hormuz-driven risk-off tape — a company choosing to price a record-breaking deal into that backdrop is a signal that underlying demand for AI-memory exposure is strong enough to absorb macro noise. Second, it follows directly from Samsung's record-but-sold-off Q2 print two days ago: the market is currently trying to work out whether AI-cycle memory demand justifies the capex, and SK Hynix is about to become the cleanest, most liquid US-listed proxy for that exact question. HBM and DRAM pricing sit directly upstream of GPU system cost, so SKHY's aftermarket behaviour from tomorrow is a genuinely useful data point for anyone pricing the GPU compute venture's input costs over the next 12-18 months — worth a specific follow-up once it opens.

## Story 2: Iran ceasefire "over" per Trump; oil punches to a fresh high, equities close mixed

**Source:** TheStreet | https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-july-8-2026 | CNBC | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/08/oil-prices-brent-wti-iran-us-hormuz.html
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Comparison Network]]

This updates rather than repeats yesterday's lead. Speaking at the NATO summit in Ankara, President Trump said of the Iran ceasefire memorandum "to me, I think it's over... I don't want to deal with them anymore," and the US carried out further strikes overnight. That's a materially harder public position than Tuesday's retaliatory-strike framing, and it moved oil further: Brent settled up 5.43% at $78.19 (from Tuesday's $74.16 close), WTI rose 4.37% to $73.52. Equities took it more unevenly than Tuesday's clean risk-off session — the Dow fell 576.76 points (-1.09%) and the S&P slid 0.28% to 7,482.71, but the Nasdaq actually closed up 0.2% at 25,870.65, suggesting some rotation back into large-cap tech even as oil-sensitive and cyclical names sold off. Trump reportedly softened the tone the following day, saying the exchange of fire wouldn't necessarily mean sustained military action, which is the kind of whiplash that has characterised this ceasefire since it was signed. Nothing here changes the standing read from yesterday's report: Hormuz is the market's daily swing factor for as long as this stays this unresolved, and a tanker was hit close enough to UAE waters last week that this remains directly relevant to [[Comparison Network]]'s operating environment, not just a background macro story.

## Story 3: FOMC minutes show a genuine "family fight" over the rate path, and a notably unhedged line on inflation

**Source:** Fortune | https://fortune.com/2026/07/08/laura-ullrich-fed-reserve-kevin-warsh-minutes-fomc/ | Axios | https://www.axios.com/2026/07/08/warsh-fomc-minutes-policy | Federal Reserve | https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260708a.htm
**Related vault notes:** none directly — logged for macro/CBUAE tracking

The minutes from the 16-17 June meeting dropped yesterday afternoon at 2pm ET, just after this brief's usual filing point, so the actual content is new to the vault even though the release itself isn't dated today. Two things stand out. First, the substance: a handful of participants argued for raising rates immediately given inflation running hot on both Iran-war energy pass-through and AI-boom cost pressures, but agreed to go along with a hold; the rest of the committee then split over where rates should sit by December, some wanting the current range or slightly below, others arguing for higher. That's a genuinely divided committee, not a consensus hold dressed up as one. Second, the framing: Warsh's decision to withhold his own dot-plot projection means this transcript is the only on-record institutional read on the committee's thinking rather than a supplement to chair guidance, and one economist flagged a single unqualified sentence in the minutes — "the Committee will deliver price stability" — as carrying more signal than anything else in the document, precisely because it has no hedge attached. Read together with June's weak 57,000 non-farm payrolls print and still-hot 4.2% CPI, this is a Fed that is publicly undecided while privately leaning hawkish on inflation risk. CBUAE remains pegged and unmoved at 3.65%; that only changes if the Fed actually moves, and today's minutes make a September hike look more plausible than a cut.

## Story 4: Capital rotates into Chinese AI names as Korean and Taiwanese chip stocks wobble

**Source:** 24/7 Wall St | https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/07/08/alibaba-surges-9-ahead-of-earnings-baidu-gains-5-as-chinese-e-commerce-and-tech-stocks-rally/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Alibaba jumped roughly 9% and Baidu gained around 5%, with the Hang Seng China Enterprises Index climbing as much as 4.5% — its biggest advance since April 2025 — as traders rotated out of South Korean and Taiwanese chipmakers into beaten-down Chinese tech. The immediate catalyst was Alibaba telling analysts that losses in its instant-commerce business narrowed for the June quarter, alongside continued strong cloud numbers: Alibaba's Cloud Intelligence Group revenue grew 38% last quarter with AI product revenue now 30% of external cloud revenue for an eleventh straight quarter of triple-digit AI growth, while Baidu's AI Cloud infrastructure revenue was up 79% year-on-year. Flagged with a date caveat: this move is reported against a 7-8 July dateline given Hong Kong/Asia trading hours, so it sits right at the edge of "today" for a US-dateline brief, but it's included because it's a genuinely new development not covered previously and it bears directly on the SK Hynix story above — money is visibly leaving the memory-chip trade for Chinese AI-platform exposure in the same week SK Hynix is trying to raise a record $28bn from the same pool of AI-adjacent capital. Worth watching whether that rotation holds once SKHY actually starts trading tomorrow.
