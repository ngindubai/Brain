## Story 1: Gulftainer commits $2 billion to nearly triple Khor Fakkan capacity as a hedge against another Hormuz closure

**Source:** The National, "Gulftainer expansion to absorb 90% of UAE container demand if Hormuz shuts" (7 July) | https://www.thenationalnews.com/business/energy/2026/07/07/khor-fakkan-port-terminal-capacity-to-be-doubled-amid-hormuz-disruption/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]], [[Comparison Network]]

Gulftainer group chief executive Farid Belbouab announced a $2 billion investment to expand Khor Fakkan Commercial Terminal from 3.5 million to 10 million TEUs of annual capacity over 36 months, with an interim target of 5 million TEUs within three months, plus development of two inland dry ports (Al Dhaid and Sajaa, 2.3 million TEUs combined annual capacity). The terminal sits on the Gulf of Oman coast, bypassing the Strait of Hormuz entirely — the chokepoint that was closed for four months earlier this year. Weekly throughput at Khor Fakkan has already risen from 8,000 to 65,000 TEUs since Hormuz reopened, and Belbouab says the expanded facility will be able to absorb 80-90% of total UAE container demand if the strait closes again.

This is the clearest capital-commitment evidence yet that the UAE's post-Hormuz trade strategy isn't just "wait for normal service to resume" — it's a deliberate, funded redundancy build so that a repeat closure doesn't repeat the same disruption. For the standing Hormuz-recovery open loop, this shifts the framing from recovery-in-progress to structural hedge-being-built, which is a materially different and more durable story than a bounce-back. No direct portfolio angle for the comparison network or CRM clients today, but it's a genuine data point for anyone assessing UAE logistics/trade resilience as an investment or venture thesis.
