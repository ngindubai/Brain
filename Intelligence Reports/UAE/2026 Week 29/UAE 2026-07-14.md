## Story 1: DFM/ADX print gap resolves, both exchanges close lower Monday, no suspension

**Source:** Trading Economics | https://tradingeconomics.com/united-arab-emirates/stock-market
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

The last two reports flagged that no source had a confirmed closing print for the UAE's two trading sessions following the weekend's direct strikes, a genuine two-day data gap during the most acute phase of this cycle's aftermath. Today's check resolves it: Trading Economics now carries a dated Monday 13 July print for both exchanges, ADX General closed at 9,903.73, down 0.33% on the session, and DFM General closed at 5,967.43, down 1.24%. Dubai's steeper fall than Abu Dhabi's is consistent with DFM's heavier weighting toward consumer and real estate names, which tend to be more sentiment-sensitive than Abu Dhabi's bank- and energy-heavy index.

The practical read: this was a reporting-lag artefact, not a Capital Markets Authority suspension. Trading continued through the weekend's escalation without the formal halt seen in March, which is itself a useful data point on how the market and regulator are calibrating to a now-recurring pattern of strikes, this cycle's shocks are being absorbed as ordinary volatility rather than triggering emergency closures every time. Closing the loop here rather than carrying it forward again.
