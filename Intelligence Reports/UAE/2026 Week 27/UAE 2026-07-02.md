## Story 1: Hormuz open loop — Doha talks on a one-week de-escalation arrangement, Brent drops on the news

**Source:** straits.live (aggregating News18, JMIC/PortWatch data) | https://straits.live/ | Wikipedia 2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis (cross-reference) | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]], [[Ventures]]

Today's delta on the running Hormuz story: US and Iranian delegations concluded talks in Doha focused specifically on a proposed one-week Strait de-escalation arrangement. Coverage describes "positive progress," though military activity elsewhere in the region continues, so this is a narrow technical proposal rather than a breakthrough on the broader ceasefire. Markets reacted before the details did — Brent crude fell 3.72% over 24 hours to $70.70, pricing in diplomatic optimism even though physical shipping conditions on the water have not changed.

The underlying numbers still show a strait that is closed in practice, not open with friction. Today is day 123 of closure to normal commercial shipping. The most recent transit data (28 June, PortWatch) shows 27 vessels crossing against a cited pre-crisis baseline of 84/day — about 32% of normal. Note this baseline differs from the 93-110/day figure this report has been using from other sources; treat both as rough, methodology-dependent estimates rather than a contradiction, and expect the gap to persist across sources until a single authoritative count exists.

Two new data points worth carrying forward: marine war-risk premiums for VLCCs (very large crude carriers) remain around 8x pre-crisis levels, and six P&I clubs (marine insurance mutuals) have withdrawn from Gulf coverage entirely. Prediction markets are sceptical of a fast fix — only 14% odds of normal traffic by 15 July, rising to 83% by year-end, which is a market view that this drags through summer before it resolves.
