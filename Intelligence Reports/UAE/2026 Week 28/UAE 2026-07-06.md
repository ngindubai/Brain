## Story 1: Hormuz traffic recovers further as Iran and Oman clash over transit fee structure

**Source:** Hormuz Strait Monitor tracker (via web search) | https://hormuzstraitmonitor.com/ — Geneva Solutions, "Iran wants to charge 'fees' on Hormuz passage. What impacts could that have" (via web search) | https://genevasolutions.news/peace-humanitarian/iran-wants-to-charge-fees-on-hormuz-passage-what-impacts-could-that-have — Tribune India, "Iran, Oman advance plans for Strait of Hormuz transit fees despite US opposition" (via web search) | https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/usa-news/iran-oman-advance-plans-for-strait-of-hormuz-transit-fees-despite-us-opposition-report/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

Two genuine developments on the standing Hormuz thread. First, traffic: as of 4-5 July, roughly 25 vessels transited in a single day (15 inbound, 10 outbound), with tanker-heavy flow in both directions and daily volume now estimated around 35/day versus the pre-war average near 110 — up from the 27/day figure logged in the 5 July report, a incremental but real recovery. A five-vessel convoy (led by the Liberia-flagged crude tanker Hafeet) threaded the southern route on Sunday, read as a tentative sign of confidence returning.

Second, and more consequential: the transit-fee question that first surfaced as an Iran-Oman "traffic management agreement" in the prior report has hardened into an actual dispute. Under the preliminary June peace agreement, Iran committed to 60 days of free passage — but Iran and Oman are now advancing plans to introduce fees regardless, and the two sides disagree on the legal basis. Oman wants ships to pay a voluntary service fee; Iran insists it reserves the right to impose mandatory "insurance fees" (explicitly not calling them tolls, which international law would not permit a coastal state to levy unilaterally on transit-strait traffic). The US is objecting to both versions. No fees are being charged yet, but this is the first time the dispute has moved from vague "traffic management" language to a concrete disagreement over money and legal authority — worth tracking closely, since a mandatory Iranian fee regime would be a direct new cost layered onto every tanker and cargo ship using the strait, with second-order effects on UAE trade costs and marine war-risk insurance pricing that's already running roughly 8x pre-crisis levels.

## Story 2: Fresh H1 2026 data shows Dubai property price growth decelerating sharply even as yields and off-plan demand hold firm

**Source:** Dubai Chronicle, "Dubai Real Estate Market H1 2026: Prices, Yields, and Off Plan Trends" (5 July) | https://www.dubaichronicle.com/2026/07/05/dubai-real-estate-market-h1-2026-trends/
**Related vault notes:** [[Ventures]]

This is a genuinely new angle on the transaction data already logged in the 5 July report (86,005 H1 transactions, AED 286.43bn), not a re-tell of the volume story. The new detail: price appreciation has decelerated sharply through the first five months of the year, from close to 12% year-on-year in January to under 4% by May — described in the piece as a natural market correction and a "settling into a steadier, more sustainable rhythm" rather than a sign of weakness. Citywide rental yields sit at a confirmed 7.0%, with apartments at 5.7%, both figures still pending an official 2026 update. Off-plan sales continue to dominate at roughly 75% of residential transaction value, with more than 100,000 additional units scheduled for delivery across the rest of 2026.

Read against the June monthly print (volume up 31.3% month-on-month, logged previously), the combined picture is now clearer: transaction volume is recovering fast, but pricing power is not — buyers have leverage, yields are holding rather than compressing, and the market is absorbing a large new-supply pipeline without prices running away. That's a materially more useful data point for the MortgageCompare and distressed-deals theses than volume-only figures: a market with strong yields, decelerating price growth and heavy off-plan supply is one where financing content and buyer-side positioning (rather than seller-side urgency messaging) is likely to earn more attention over H2.
