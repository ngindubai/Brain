## Story 1: Houthis resume tanker strikes off Saudi Arabia, Trump threatens to bomb Iranian infrastructure in response

**Source:** UKMTO / multiple wire reports (via web search) | https://www.ukmto.org (advisory) ; https://gcaptain.com/houthis-resume-attacks-on-shipping-claim-strikes-on-two-saudi-tankers/
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

The UK Maritime Trade Operations centre reported a tanker struck by an unknown projectile roughly 70 nautical miles southwest of Al Shuqaiq, Saudi Arabia, at 2000 GMT on 23 July, causing an onboard fire the crew were fighting; no casualties were reported. Yemen's Houthis subsequently claimed responsibility, saying they targeted two Saudi-flagged tankers — named as ENCELIA and LAYLA — with ballistic and cruise missiles, on the grounds that the vessels violated a naval blockade the Iran-aligned group has declared against Saudi Arabia. Separately, President Trump has threatened to destroy Iranian bridges and power plants each time Tehran (or its proxies) strikes a ship in the Strait of Hormuz corridor.

This is a materially different shape of threat from the direct US-Iran exchange covered in recent reports: the active attacker here is the Houthis rather than Iran's own forces, widening the number of actors capable of disrupting Gulf shipping and giving Iran a layer of deniability while the practical effect — rising war-risk premiums, rattled shipping routes, a jumpier oil market — is the same. Worth watching whether Brent moves further on this specific news over the next day, and whether the affected-tanker list grows.

## Story 2: DP World signs 50-year deal for two new Fujairah ports, explicitly positioned outside the Strait of Hormuz

**Source:** Gulf News (via web search) | https://gulfnews.com/business/markets/dp-world-signs-50-year-deal-to-develop-al-rughailat-and-dibba-ports-in-fujairah-1.500616669
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

DP World and the Fujairah Ports Authority signed a 50-year concession on 22 July to develop two new multi-purpose ports: Al Rughailat Container Port, designed for up to 2.5 million TEUs annually plus 1.7 million tonnes of general cargo and 190,000 car-equivalent units, and Dibba Al Fujairah Port, adding up to 3.6 million tonnes of annual general cargo capacity. Together they lift DP World's total UAE container handling capacity from 19.4 million to nearly 22 million TEUs once operational.

This revives a thread this report parked several cycles ago for lack of movement, and the timing is not incidental: both new ports sit on the Gulf of Oman side, outside the Strait of Hormuz chokepoint that's been the subject of the escalating conflict in Story 1. Building deep-water capacity that doesn't depend on Hormuz transit is exactly the kind of structural hedge the ongoing conflict makes look prescient rather than opportunistic — worth reading this as the UAE's port infrastructure quietly de-risking itself from the same corridor that's driving today's insurance and shipping story.

## Story 3: Commercial Bank of Dubai's net loans pass Dh100 billion for the first time, H1 profit up 1.2%

**Source:** Gulf News (via web search) | https://gulfnews.com/business/banking/cbd-net-loans-top-dh100-billion-as-first-half-profit-reaches-dh172-billion-1.500616622
**Related vault notes:** [[CRM Systems]]

CBD reported H1 2026 net profit after tax of Dh1.716 billion, up 1.2% year-on-year, with net loans and advances crossing Dh100 billion for the first time (Dh104.2 billion, up 3.1% since December 2025). Customer deposits reached Dh107.7 billion and total assets Dh154.5 billion. This is steady, unspectacular growth rather than a standout quarter, but the Dh100 billion loan-book milestone is a genuine first for the bank and continues the pattern of solid mid-tier UAE bank earnings this season.

## Story 4: United Arab Bank posts Dh201 million H1 profit as Q2 profit jumps 68% quarter-on-quarter

**Source:** Gulf News / Zawya (via web search) | https://gulfnews.com/business/banking/united-arab-bank-reports-dh201-million-h1-profit-assets-reach-dh28-billion-1.500616652
**Related vault notes:** [[CRM Systems]]

United Arab Bank's Q2 2026 profit reached Dh126 million, up 68% on Q1 and 19% year-on-year, taking H1 profit to Dh201 million. Total assets rose 16% year-on-year to Dh28 billion, loans and Islamic financing grew 14% to Dh15.3 billion, the investment portfolio grew 25% to Dh8.3 billion, and customer deposits rose 16% to Dh18.2 billion. A smaller bank than CBD but a stronger growth rate across nearly every line — worth noting as evidence the UAE banking sector's H1 earnings season is broadly positive so far, ahead of Emirates NBD's H1 results, which were due today but had not yet surfaced in any source checked; FAB's remain due 29 July.

**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

An off-plan apartment at Aman Residences Dubai in Jumeirah Second sold for Dh166.07 million, a 10,021 sq ft unit averaging over Dh16,572 per square foot. This continues the divergence flagged in this week's Q2 property data: overall transaction volume down roughly a third year-on-year, but the very top of the market — branded residences, ultra-prime apartments in established locations — still setting records.
