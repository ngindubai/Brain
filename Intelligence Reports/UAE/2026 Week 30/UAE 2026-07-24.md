## Story 1: US carries out 13th consecutive night of strikes on Iran as Houthi tanker attacks continue, Brent near $100

**Source:** Taipei Times / CNN / multiple wire reports (via web search) | https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/world/archives/2026/07/24/2003861312
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

US Central Command confirmed it carried out its 13th consecutive night of strikes against Iran overnight, stating the goal is to "hold Iran accountable and diminish threats from the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps to commercial shipping." This is a materially different situation from Wednesday's report, which covered Trump's threat to strike Iranian infrastructure in response to Houthi attacks — that threat has become sustained, nightly US military action, not a one-off warning. Iran's response has been to threaten a "severe and escalatory" counter-response through senior military adviser Mohsen Rezaei, and the Houthis' blockade of Saudi-linked shipping through the Bab el-Mandeb Strait — a corridor handling roughly 12% of global trade — remains in place following Wednesday's tanker strikes on the Encelia and Layla.

Brent crude is now up roughly 40% this month and has traded above $100 a barrel for the first time in two months, a level not seen since the conflict began escalating. This is now a genuine sustained war rather than an isolated flare-up, and the practical Gulf-facing effects — marine war-risk premiums, insurance pricing, shipping route disruption — are compounding rather than resolving. Worth treating this as the dominant open thread for the region until there's a real de-escalation signal, not another one-day story.

## Story 2: Emirates NBD delivers record $4.41 billion H1 profit, resolving yesterday's overdue open loop

**Source:** Economy Middle East / AGBI (via web search) | https://economymiddleeast.com/news/emirates-nbd-pre-tax-profit-rises-5-percent-to-record-4-4-billion-as-income-grows-16-percent-balance-sheet-tops-354-billion/
**Related vault notes:** [[CRM Systems]]

Emirates NBD reported H1 2026 results on 23 July — one day later than this report's open loop had flagged as due — with record pre-tax profit of AED16.2 billion ($4.41 billion), up 5% year-on-year, on a 13% rise in net interest income and 25% growth in non-funded income. Net profit reached AED12.9 billion ($3.5 billion), up 3%, as total income climbed 16% to AED23.9 billion. The balance sheet crossed AED1.3 trillion, with gross loans up 17% to AED771 billion — partly boosted by the consolidation of India's RBL Bank, acquired earlier this year — and deposits up 13% to AED892 billion. Cost of risk held at a strong 42 basis points.

This closes out the UAE Q2 2026 banking season's two largest names alongside CBD and United Arab Bank covered earlier this week; FAB's results remain due 29 July. The pattern across all three reporting banks so far is consistent: solid, broad-based growth with no red flags, continuing the season's steady-good-news theme.

## Story 4: UAE expands visa-on-arrival to six more nationalities from tomorrow

**Source:** UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs / web search | https://www.mofa.gov.ae/en/mediahub/news/2026/6/25/uae-grants-visa-on-arrival
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

From 25 July, citizens of Indonesia, Vietnam, Thailand, the Philippines, Kenya and South Africa holding a valid residency permit from the US, EU, UK, Singapore, Japan, South Korea, Australia, New Zealand or Canada become eligible for UAE visa-on-arrival — either a renewable 14-day permit or a single-stay 60-day permit. This continues 2026's steady loosening of UAE entry requirements (following the Blue Visa for environmental contributors and the smart medical visa initiative flagged in recent months), part of the wider push to keep the UAE's population and tourism growth pipeline open.

Background rather than action item — flagged for completeness given the tourism and expat-services angle relevant to the wider portfolio, but no direct site impact today.

## Story 5: Dubai property market holds steady through the summer — daily transaction volumes resilient, Q2 handover volumes highest in years

**Source:** Multiple property market trackers (via web search) | https://edwardsandtowers.com/dubai-real-estate-market-update-july-2026/
**Related vault notes:** [[Comparison Network]]

Dubai's daily DLD data continues to show a resilient if unspectacular market: 801 sales transactions on 22 July worth AED 2.09 billion, alongside 4,418 lease registrations worth AED 1.53 billion the same day. Q2 2026 brought the highest handover volume Dubai has seen in years, alongside the sharpest slowdown in new project launches — developers visibly pacing supply against the transaction-volume cooling this report has tracked through July. Ready-home sales posted their strongest monthly surge in three years, consistent with buyers favouring completed stock over off-plan risk while regional tensions run hot.

This is steady-state background rather than a standalone development — no new data point that changes the "volume cooling, value holding" read this report has carried for several cycles.
